'''John Arnold Fielding''' (born 2 September 1939) is an English retired professional [[association football|football]] [[Midfielder#Winger|winger]] who played in [[the Football League]] for [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]], [[Southport F.C.|Southport]] and [[Grimsby Town F.C.|Grimsby Town]]. He later had a long career in [[Non-League football|non-league football]] and also played in [[Soccer in South Africa|South Africa]].

== Playing career ==

=== Early years ===
A [[Right winger (football)|right winger]] or [[Right midfielder|midfielder]], Fielding began his career at [[Lancashire Combination]] First Division club Wigan Athletic, scoring a goal every other game during the 1959–60 season and earning a £300 move to [[Football League Fourth Division]] club [[Southport F.C.|Southport]] in March 1961.<ref name="Hugman">{{hugman|6350|accessdate=8 January 2017}}</ref><ref name="Timeless">{{Cite book |title=Timeless Bees: Brentford F.C. Who's Who 1920–2006 |last=Haynes |first=Graham |last2=Coumbe |first2=Frank |publisher=Yore Publications |year=2006 |isbn=978-0955294914 |page=56}}</ref> He broke into the team on a regular basis during the [[1961–62 Football League|1961–62]] season, scoring 12 goals in 45 games.<ref name="southport">{{Cite web |url=https://www.southportfootballclub.co.uk/players/player-details/?id=1184 |title=Port Online – Johnny Fielding |access-date=12 May 2019}}</ref> He went even better the [[1962–63 Football League|1962–63]] season, bagging 11 goals in 32 games before departing the club in March 1963.<ref name="Timeless" /><ref name="southport" /> Fielding scored made 86 appearances and scored 24 goals for Southport.<ref name="southport" />

=== Brentford ===
Fielding signed for Fourth Division high-flyers [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] in March 1963 in a £7,000 deal.<ref name="Timeless" /> He had impressed manager [[Malky McDonald]] with his performance while a Southport player at [[Griffin Park]] in a 3–3 draw earlier in the season.<ref name="Timeless" /> After the return fixture, McDonald tabled a big-money offer.<ref name="Timeless" /> Fielding made seven appearances in what remained of the [[1962–63 Brentford F.C. season|1962–63]] season to help the Bees over the line to the Division Four title.<ref name="White">{{Cite book |title=100 Years Of Brentford |publisher=Brentford FC |year=1989 |isbn=0951526200 |editor-last=White |editor-first=Eric |pages=387–388}}</ref> He made semi-regular appearances in the [[Football League Third Division|Third Division]] and made 31 appearances, before fully breaking into the team in the [[1964–65 Brentford F.C. season|1964–65]] season, when he made 42 appearances and scoring 10 league goals.<ref name="White" /> After making just 9 appearances in the first half of the [[1965–66 Brentford F.C. season|1965–66]] season, Fielding left the Bees in December 1965.<ref name="Timeless" /><ref name="White" /> He made 93 appearances and scored 21 goals during two-and-a-half years at Griffin Park.<ref name="Timeless" />

=== Grimsby Town ===
Fielding joined Third Division club [[Grimsby Town F.C.|Grimsby Town]] in December 1965.<ref name="Timeless" /> Though not a regular pick for the team, he managed eight goals in 30 league games before departing in 1967.<ref name="Hugman" />

=== Port Elizabeth City ===
Fielding moved to [[Soccer in South Africa|South Africa]] in 1967, where he linked up with former Brentford teammate [[Matt Crowe]] at [[National Football League (South Africa)|National Football League]] club [[Port Elizabeth City F.C.|Port Elizabeth City]].<ref name="Timeless" /> He had a successful 1967 season, winning the league title and departing after the 1968 campaign.

=== Return to Wigan Athletic ===
Fielding returned to Wigan Athletic in 1969 for a second spell with the now high-flying [[Northern Premier League]] club.<ref name="Timeless" /> He made 27 league appearances and scored three goals during the [[1969–70 Northern Premier League|1969–70]] season and helping the Latics to a second-successive runners-up finish in the league. He departed at the end of the season.

=== Non-league football ===
Fielding wound down his career with spells at Northern Premier League clubs [[Altrincham F.C.|Altrincham]], [[Ellesmere Port Town F.C.|Ellesmere Port Town]], [[South Liverpool F.C.|South Liverpool]] and [[Runcorn F.C.|Runcorn]].<ref name="Timeless" />
