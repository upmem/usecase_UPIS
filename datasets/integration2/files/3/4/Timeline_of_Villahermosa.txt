The following is a timeline of the history of the [[List of cities in Mexico|city]] of [[Villahermosa]] in [[Centro Municipality, Tabasco|Centro Municipality]], [[Tabasco]] state, [[Mexico]].

==Prior to 20th century==
{{Expand section|date=December 2014}}
{{History of Mexico}}

* 1596 - Settlement founded.<ref name=enciclo-cronologia>{{cite web |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111212045250/http://www.e-local.gob.mx/work/templates/enciclo/tabasco/mpios/27004a.htm |title=Centro: Cronología de Hechos Históricos |work= Enciclopedia de los Municipios de México |publisher= [[Instituto Nacional para el Federalismo y el Desarrollo Municipal]] |language=Spanish |archivedate= 12 December 2011 |url= http://www.e-local.gob.mx/work/templates/enciclo/tabasco/mpios/27004a.htm |url-status=dead }}</ref>
* 1598 - Settlement named "Villahermosa".<ref name=seltzer1952>{{Citation |publisher = Columbia University Press |location = New York |editor = Leon E. Seltzer |title = Columbia Lippincott Gazetteer of the World |ol=6112221M |date = 1952 |page=2030 |url= https://archive.org/stream/columbialippinco00selt#page/2030/mode/1up }}</ref>
* 1677 - Regional seat of government relocated from Villahermosa to [[Tacotalpa]].
* 1797 - Regional seat of government relocated to Villahermosa from Tacotalpa.
* 1824
** Villahermosa becomes capital of [[Tabasco|State of Tabasco]].
** [[Congress of Tabasco]] headquartered in town.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.congresotabasco.gob.mx/acerca/historia |title=Historia |language=Spanish |publisher=Congreso del Estado de Tabasco |accessdate=December 18, 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141107125638/http://www.congresotabasco.gob.mx/acerca/historia |archivedate=November 7, 2014 }}</ref>
** Printing press in operation.<ref name=enciclo-cronologia />
* 1826
** Town renamed "San Juan Bautista".<ref name=seltzer1952 />
** ''Argos Tabasqueño'' newspaper begins publication.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.heytabasco.com/?p=4217 |title=El libro en Tabasco |date=2 December 2013 |work=Hey Tabasco |language=Spanish |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303215956/http://www.heytabasco.com/?p=4217 |archivedate=3 March 2016 }}</ref>
* 1846 - 24–26 October: [[First Battle of Tabasco]] against U.S. forces.
* 1847
** 15–16 June: [[Second Battle of Tabasco]].
** Regional seat of government relocated from Villahermosa to Tacotalpa.
* 1880 - [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Tabasco]] established.<ref name=katolsk>{{cite web |title=Chronology of Catholic Dioceses: Mexico |url= http://www.katolsk.no/organisasjon/verden/chronology/mexico |work=www.katolsk.no |publisher=Oslo katolske bispedømme (Oslo Catholic Diocese) |location=Norway |accessdate= December 18, 2014 }}</ref>
* 1881 - ''El Tabasqueño'' newspaper in publication.<ref name=worldcat>{{cite web |url= http://www.worldcat.org/search?q=su%3AVillahermosa+%28Tabasco%2C+Mexico%29+Newspapers.&qt=hot_subject |title=Villahermosa (Tabasco, Mexico) Newspapers |location=USA |work=WorldCat |publisher=[[Online Computer Library Center]] |accessdate= December 18, 2014 }}</ref>
* 1890 - Casa de los azulejos (residence) built.
* 1894 - {{illm|Governor's Palace (Tabasco)|es|3=Palacio de Gobierno de Tabasco}} built on the {{illm|Plaza de Armas de Villahermosa|es}}.
* 1895 - Population: 9,604.<ref>{{cite book|author=J. Figueroa Doménech |title=Guía General Descriptiva de la República Mexicana |volume=2 |year=1899|publisher=R. de S. N. Araluce |chapter=Estado de Tabasco |chapterurl= http://hdl.handle.net/2027/mdp.39015051117227?urlappend=%3Bseq=622 |language=Spanish |via=Hathi Trust }}</ref>
* 1900 - Population: 10,548.<ref name=brit1910>{{Citation |location = New York |title =  Encyclopædia Britannica |date = 1910 |oclc = 14782424 |edition=11th |chapterurl =https://books.google.com/books?id=dXIYAQAAIAAJ&pg=PA323 |chapter =Tabasco |title-link = Encyclopædia Britannica Eleventh Edition }}</ref>

==20th century==
{{Expand section|date=December 2014}}
* 1915 - City named "Villahermosa" again.<ref name=seltzer1952 />
* 1921 - ''Diario de Tabasco'' newspaper begins publication.<ref name=enciclo-cronologia />
* 1924 - Liga Central de Resistencia (political group) headquartered in city.<ref>{{cite journal |title=Monoculture, Monopoly, and the Mexican Revolution: Tomás Garrido Canabal and the Standard Fruit Company in Tabasco (1920–1935) |author= Stan Ridgeway |journal= Mexican Studies |volume= 17 |year= 2001 }}</ref>
* 1932 - 12 October: Flood.<ref name=enciclo-cronologia />
* 1958 - {{illm|Parque-Museo La Venta|es}} (museum) opens.
* 1974 - [[Villahermosa Institute of Technology]] established.
* 1977 - Oil discovered near city (approximate date).{{sfn|New York Times|1983}}<ref name="Grayson1981">{{cite book|author=George Grayson|title=The Politics of Mexican Oil|year= 1981|publisher=University of Pittsburgh Press |isbn=978-0-8229-7423-9}}</ref>
* 1979 - [[Carlos Rovirosa Pérez International Airport|Pérez International Airport]] inaugurated.{{citation needed|date=December 2014}}
* 1982
** 1 January: {{illm|Tabasco state election, 1982|es|3=Elecciones estatales de Tabasco de 1982}} held.
** Casa de Artes (cultural institution) founded.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://iec.tabasco.gob.mx/content/casa-jose-gorostiza |title=Escuela de Iniciación Artística Casa de Artes José Gorostiza |language=Spanish |author=Instituto Estatal de Cultura |publisher= State of Tabasco |accessdate= December 18, 2014 }}</ref>
* 1987
** [[Peñitas Dam]] commissioned near city on the [[Grijalva River]].{{sfn|New York Times|1999}}
** ''{{illm|Tabasco Hoy|es}}'' newspaper begins publication.<ref name=europa2004>{{cite book |title= Europa World Year Book |year=2004 |isbn=1857432533 |publisher=Taylor & Francis |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=gP_-8rXzQs8C |chapter=Mexico }}</ref>
* 1988
** 9 October: {{illm|Tabasco state election, 1988|es|3=Elecciones estatales de Tabasco de 1988}} held.
** {{illm|Museo de Historia Natural José Narciso Rovirosa|es}} (museum) opens.
* 1990
** {{illm|Catedral del Señor de Tabasco|es}} (church) built.
** Population: 261,131 city; 386,776 [[Centro Municipality, Tabasco|Centro Municipality]].
* 1994 - 20 November: {{illm|Tabasco state election, 1994|es|3=Elecciones estatales de Tabasco de 1994}} held.
* 1995 - Population: 301,328 city; 465,449 [[Centro Municipality, Tabasco|Centro Municipality]].
* 1999 - September: Flood.{{sfn|New York Times|1999}}
* 2000
** 15 October: {{illm|Tabasco state election, 2000|es|3=Elecciones estatales de Tabasco de 2000}} held.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2000/10/23/world/23WIRE-MEX.html |title=Ruling Party Declared Winner of Disputed Governor´s race in Mexico |date= 23 October 2000 |work= New York Times }}</ref>
** [[Villahermosa Mexico Temple|Mormon temple]] built.
** Population: 430,846 city; 520,308 [[Centro Municipality, Tabasco|Centro Municipality]].

==21st century==
{{Expand section|date=December 2014}}
* 2005 - Population: 558,524 city; 664,629 [[Centro Municipality, Tabasco|Centro Municipality]].
* 2006 - 15 October: [[2006 Tabasco state election]] held.
* 2007 - October: [[2007 Tabasco flood]].<ref name=bbc-timeline>{{cite web |url= https://www.bbc.com/news/world-latin-america-19828041 |author=BBC News |title=Mexico Profile: Timeline |accessdate= December 18, 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/11/05/world/americas/05mexico.html |title=In Mexico, Residents Contend With a Flooded City |date= 5 November 2007 |work= New York Times |author=Elisabeth Malkin }}</ref>
* 2009 - Jesús Alí de la Torre elected [[:es:Municipio de Centro (Tabasco)#Presidentes municipales|mayor]] of [[Centro Municipality, Tabasco|Centro Municipality]].<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.citymayors.com/mayors/mexican-mayors.html |title=Mexican Mayors |location=London |work=City Mayors.com |publisher=[[City Mayors Foundation]] |accessdate= December 18, 2014 }}</ref>
* 2010 - Population: 353,577 city;<ref name=SEDESOL>{{cite web|title= Municipio de Centro: Villahermosa |url= http://www.microrregiones.gob.mx/catloc/LocdeMun.aspx?tipo=clave&campo=loc&ent=27&mun=004 |work= Catálogo de Localidades |publisher=[[Secretaría de Desarrollo Social]] |language=Spanish |accessdate= December 18, 2014}}</ref> 755,425 [[:es:Zona Metropolitana de Villahermosa|metro]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://unstats.un.org/unsd/demographic/products/dyb/dyb2011.htm |work=Demographic Yearbook 2011 |year=2012 |publisher=[[United Nations Statistics Division]] |title=Population of Capital Cities and Cities of 100,000 or More Inhabitants }}</ref>
* 2012 - 1 July: [[2012 Tabasco gubernatorial election]] held.
