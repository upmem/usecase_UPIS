The '''Orlando Humason House''' is a historic [[house]] located in [[The Dalles, Oregon|The Dalles]], [[Oregon]], [[United States]]. Humason (1828â€“1875), the "Father of Wasco County", lived in this modest [[Gothic Revival architecture|Gothic Revival]] house from its construction in 1860 until his death. Originally from [[Ohio]], he worked in [[Practice of law|law]], [[prospecting]], [[agriculture]], and [[journalism]], prior to settling in The Dalles as a prosperous [[merchant]] and [[River transportation|river transport]] businessman. Representing first [[Oregon City]] then The Dalles in the [[Oregon Territorial Legislature|territorial]] and [[Oregon Legislative Assembly|state]] legislatures, he introduced [[Bill (law)|legislation]] establishing Wasco County, [[Multnomah County]], and the City of The Dalles. He also championed a bill to build [[Cascade Locks and Canal|a canal]] around the [[Cascades Rapids]] to allow [[Columbia River|Lower Columbia]] shipping to reach The Dalles, which did not come to fruition in his lifetime. Humason's other public positions included [[mayor]] of The Dalles and Wasco County [[judge]]. The house is [[Architecture|architecturally]] notable as one of very few Gothic Revival houses in The Dalles.<ref name="HumasonNom">{{Citation | last1 = Mlnarik-Holt | first1 = Jan | last2 = Meader | first2 = Dan | date = November 15, 1990 | title = National Register of Historic Places Registration Form: Humason, Orlando, House | url = {{NRHP url|91000809}} | accessdate = February 4, 2013 | format = PDF}}.</ref>

At some date between 1875 and 1908, it was moved to its present location from elsewhere in The Dalles.<ref name="HumasonNom"/> It was entered on the [[National Register of Historic Places]] in 1991.<ref name="WkList">{{citation | author = National Park Service | authorlink = National Park Service | title = Weekly List of Listed Properties: 6/17/91 through 6/21/91 | date = June 28, 1991 | url = http://www.nps.gov/nr/listings/Weekly_Register_List_1991.pdf | format = PDF | accessdate = March 15, 2016 | at = p. 65 }}.</ref>
