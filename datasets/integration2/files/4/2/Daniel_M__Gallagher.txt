'''Daniel M. Gallagher, Jr.''' (born May 31, 1972) is an American lawyer in private practice who served as a Commissioner of the [[U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission]] (SEC) from 2011 to 2015. Gallagher was appointed to the SEC by President [[Barack Obama]].<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|url=https://www.sec.gov/about/commissioner/gallagher.htm |title=SEC Biography: Commissioner Daniel M. Gallagher |publisher=Sec.gov |date= |accessdate=March 1, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pli.edu/Content/Faculty/Hon_Daniel_M_Gallagher/_/N-4oZ1z138lf?ID=PE652771 |title=Faculty Profile – Hon. Daniel M. Gallagher |publisher=Pli.edu |date= |accessdate=March 1, 2013}}</ref> He also previously served as the deputy director and co-acting director of the Division of Trading and Markets.

==Education==
Gallagher graduated from [[Georgetown University]] in 1994 with a Bachelor of Arts in English and went on to receive a Juris Doctor from the [[Columbus School of Law]] at the [[Catholic University of America]] in 1998.<ref name="autogenerated1"/>

==Professional career==
Gallagher began his career in the private sector when he joined Wilmer, Cutler & Pickering (later WilmerHale) as an associate in the firm’s Securities Department. His next position took him to [[Fiserv]] Securities Inc. to serve as the company’s senior vice president and general counsel.<ref name="autogenerated1"/><ref name="bloomberg1">{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2011-04-21/white-house-said-to-pick-gallagher-as-casey-s-successor-at-sec.html|title=White House Said to Nominate Daniel Gallagher as Casey's Successor at SEC|last=Hamilton|first=Jesse|date=April 21, 2011|publisher=Bloomberg|accessdate=March 1, 2013}}</ref><ref name="finextra1">{{cite web|url=http://www.finextra.com/News/Announcement.aspx?pressreleaseid=22203|title=SEC adds Daniel Gallagher to trading and markets team|date=|publisher=Finextra|accessdate=March 1, 2013}}</ref>

In 2006, Gallagher transitioned to the government sector and joined the SEC as counsel to former SEC Commissioner [[Paul S. Atkins]] and later as counsel to former Chairman [[Christopher Cox]],<ref name="autogenerated1"/> working on matters involving the Division of Enforcement and the Division of Trading and Markets. In 2008, he became the deputy director of the Division of Trading and Markets and served as Division co-acting director from April 2009 through January 2010.<ref name="autogenerated1"/><ref name="finextra1"/> During this time, he was on the front lines of the agency’s response to the financial crisis, including representing the commission in the Lehman Brothers liquidation. Gallagher was appointed by Barack Obama and served as an SEC Commissioner from November 2011 to October 2015. In his role, he championed corporate governance reform, advocated for a comprehensive holistic review of equity market structural issues, and encouraged greatly improving the commission’s fixed income market expertise. 

After his time with the SEC, Gallagher returned to the private sector and in 2016 became president of Patomak Global Partners, a financial services consulting firm. He changed jobs in 2017 and joined global pharmaceutical company Mylan N.V. as the chief legal officer. 

Gallagher rejoined [[WilmerHale]] in September 2019 where he serves as deputy chair of the firm’s Securities Department.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wilmerhale.com/en/insights/news/20190903-daniel-gallagher-and-lucas-moskowitz-return-to-the-wilmerhale-securities-group|title=Daniel Gallagher and Lucas Moskowitz Return to the WilmerHale Securities Group|website=www.wilmerhale.com|language=en|access-date=2019-10-23}}</ref> His practice includes advising corporate boards and management on the full range of legal and strategic issues they face, and counseling financial services and accounting firms in investigations, regulatory proceedings and policy matters.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wilmerhale.com/en/people/daniel-gallagher|title=Daniel Gallagher|website=www.wilmerhale.com|language=en|access-date=2019-10-23}}</ref>

In May 2020, [[Robinhood (company)|Robinhood]] announced that Gallagher would be joining the company as Chief Legal Officer.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Dan Gallagher, Robinhood Board Member and Former SEC Commissioner, Joins Robinhood as Chief Legal Officer|url=https://blog.robinhood.com/news/2020/5/5/dan-gallagher-robinhood-board-member-and-former-sec-commissioner-joins-robinhood-as-chief-legal-officer|access-date=2020-07-15|website=Under the Hood|language=en-US}}</ref> 

== Professional Affiliations ==
Gallagher serves on the boards of the [[National Association of Corporate Directors]] (NACD), a leading membership organization for corporate board directors, and Symbiont, a blockchain technology company developing products in smart contracts and distributed ledgers for use in capital markets; and [[Robinhood (company)|Robinhood]], a commission-free investing app. He was formerly a non-executive director of the Irish Stock Exchange.

He is also on the advisory boards for the Institute for Law and Economics at the University of Pennsylvania and the Center for Corporate Governance, part of the Raj & Kamla Gupta Governance Institute in Drexel University’s LeBow College of Business.
