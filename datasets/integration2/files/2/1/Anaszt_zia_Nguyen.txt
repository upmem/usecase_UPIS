'''Anasztázia Nguyen''' (born 9 January 1993)<ref name=iaafbio>{{cite web|url=http://www.iaaf.org/athletes/biographies/country=hun/athcode=249028/index.html|title=Anasztázia Nguyen Biography|publisher=International Association of Athletics Federations (IAAF)|accessdate=18 February 2012}}</ref> is a [[Hungary|Hungarian]] [[track and field]] [[Sprint (running)|sprinter]] who specializes in the [[60 metres]], [[100 metres]], [[200 metres]] and the [[long jump]].

==Early life and career==
Nguyen was born in the Hungarian capital [[Budapest]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.utanpotlassport.hu/2011/07/09/akkor-jo-ha-se-kep-se-hang/|title=Akkor jó, ha se kép, se hang|publisher=utanpotlassport.hu|date=9 July 2011|accessdate=18 February 2012|language=Hungarian}}</ref> to a [[Vietnamese people|Vietnamese]] father and a [[Hungarian people|Hungarian]] mother. Her father left the family when Anasztázia was a child and she was brought up by her mother.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.felixpromotion.hu/hir/az-atleta-celja-a-2016-os-rioi-olimpia|title=Az atléta célja a 2016-os riói olimpia|publisher=Felix Promotion|accessdate=18 February 2012|language=Hungarian|archive-url=https://archive.is/20120804203353/http://www.felixpromotion.hu/hir/az-atleta-celja-a-2016-os-rioi-olimpia|archive-date=4 August 2012|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

She began her career in 2002<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.felixpromotion.hu/sportolonk/nguyen-anasztazia |title=Anasztázia Nguyen Profile |publisher=Felix Promotion |accessdate=18 February 2012 |language=Hungarian |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120114095221/http://www.felixpromotion.hu/sportolonk/nguyen-anasztazia |archivedate=14 January 2012 }}</ref> by KSI SE under the guidance of former [[Summer Universiade]] winner decathlete [[Dezső Szabó (athlete)|Dezső Szabó]]. First she also practiced long jumping and pole vaulting, but eventually decided to concentrate fully on sprint running.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/nsotv_egyenisportok/a-rioi-olimpian-mar-ott-lenne-csinos-atletank-2117914|title=A riói olimpián már ott lenne csinos atlétánk|publisher=Nemzeti Sport Online|work=NSO TV|date=17 February 2012|accessdate=18 February 2012|language=Hungarian}}</ref> In 2009 Szabó switched to Budapesti Honvéd SE to where Nguyen followed her coach.

Among her best results are a silver medal from the [[2009 World Youth Championships in Athletics|2009 World Youth Championships]] in medley relay,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iaaf.org/wyc09/results/eventCode=4141/racedate=07-12-2009/sex=W/discCode=MEAD/combCode=hash/roundCode=f/results.html#detW_MEAD_hash_f|title=Medley Relay – Women's Final|publisher=International Association of Athletics Federations (IAAF)|date=12 July 2009|accessdate=18 February 2012}}</ref> a seventh place in 100 metres on the same event<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iaaf.org/wyc09/results/eventCode=4141/racedate=07-09-2009/sex=W/discCode=100/combCode=hash/roundCode=f/results.html#detW_100_hash_f|title=100 Metres – Women's Final|publisher=International Association of Athletics Federations (IAAF)|date=12 July 2009|accessdate=18 February 2012}}</ref> and a fifth place from the [[Athletics at the 2010 Summer Youth Olympics – Girls' 100 metres|2010 Summer Youth Olympics]] also in 100 metres.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.singapore2010.sg/public/docroot/results/INF__ATW001B11C73A_____________________00001000_PENG20100821211207984.pdf |title=2010 Summer Youth Olympics – Girls 100m Final A |publisher=2010 Youth Olympics official website |date=21 August 2010 |accessdate=18 February 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110109050142/http://www.singapore2010.sg/public/docroot/results/INF__ATW001B11C73A_____________________00001000_PENG20100821211207984.pdf |archivedate=9 January 2011 }}</ref> In addition, Nguyen had won three [[Hungarian Athletics Championships]] in 100 metres (2009, 2010, 2011) and one in 200 metres (2011).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.all-athletics.com/en-us/node/128618|title=Profile of Barbara Petráhn|publisher=All Athletics|accessdate=20 June 2012}}</ref> She also the national junior record in 60 metres (7.40) and in 100 metres (11.55).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.masz.hu/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=415:magyar-szabadteri-junior-ni-rekordok&catid=61:rekordok&Itemid=81|title=Magyar szabadtéri junior női rekordok|trans-title=Hungarian outdoor junior women records|publisher=Hungarian Athletic Federation|accessdate=18 February 2012|language=Hungarian}}</ref>

For her performances in 2011 she was given the [[Heraklész Award]] in the best female athlete category.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.felixpromotion.hu/hir/heraklesz-elismeres-anasztazianak|title=Héraklész-elismerés Anasztáziának|publisher=Felix Promotion|accessdate=18 February 2012|language=Hungarian|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303223858/http://www.felixpromotion.hu/hir/heraklesz-elismeres-anasztazianak|archive-date=3 March 2016|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

At Gyula memorial in 2019 showed a result of 6.70 metres in the long jump.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/atletika/gyulai-memorial-nguyen-egyeni-csuccsal-harmadik-tavolban-2710537|title=Gyulai Memorial: Nguyen egyéni csúccsal harmadik távolban|date=2019-07-09}}</ref>
