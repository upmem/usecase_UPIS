'''''Day-Time Wife''''' is a 1939 [[Comedy (genre)|comedy]] directed by [[Gregory Ratoff]], starring [[Tyrone Power]] and [[Linda Darnell]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Day-Time Wife|url=http://www.filmaffinity.com/en/film930326.html|website=FilmAffinity|publisher=[[filmaffinity.com]]|accessdate=21 December 2015}}</ref> Darnell and Power play Jane and Ken Norton, a married couple approaching their second anniversary. This was Linda Darnell's second film. ''Day-Time Wife'' was the first of four films that Darnell and Power made together over the next few years, the others being ''[[Brigham Young (film)|Brigham Young]]'' (1940), ''[[The Mark of Zorro (1940 film)|The Mark of Zorro]]'' (1940), and ''[[Blood and Sand (1941 film)|Blood and Sand]]'' (1941).<ref>https://www.imdb.com/search/title?roles=nm0000061,nm0001105&title_type=feature,tv_episode,video,tv_movie,tv_special,mini_series,documentary,game,short,unknown</ref>

==Synopsis==
{{Expand section|date=February 2012}}
Jane ([[Linda Darnell]]) comes to believe her husband Ken ([[Tyrone Power]]) is having an extramarital affair with his secretary Kitty ([[Wendy Barrie]]). To give him a taste of his own medicine, Jane secretly takes a job as secretary to womanizing architect Bernard Dexter ([[Warren William]]), who, as it turns out, and unbeknownst to Jane, has a business relationship with Ken.<ref>{{cite web|title=Day-Time Wife|url=http://www.afi.com/members/catalog/DetailView.aspx?s=&Movie=7767|website=AFI Catalog of Feature Films|publisher=[[afi.com]]|accessdate=21 December 2015}}</ref>

==Production==
Despite playing a married woman, star Linda Darnell was only 16 years old at the time of filming, having been born on October 16, 1923. Fox studio executive [[Darryl F. Zanuck]] added two years to her real age when he signed her to a contract.
