'''Ravendale''' is an [[unincorporated area|unincorporated community]] in [[Lassen County, California|Lassen County]], [[California]].<ref name=gnis /> It is located {{convert|7|mi|km|0}} southeast of [[Termo, California|Termo]],<ref name=CGN>{{California's Geographic Names|411}}</ref> at an elevation of 5305 feet (1617 m).<ref name=gnis /> The town was a station on the [[Nevada-California-Oregon Railway]] line, but unlike many such towns, survived past the abandonment of the line in the late 1990s.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://www.google.com/books/edition/Railroads_of_Nevada_and_Eastern_Californ/dCyVDwAAQBAJ?hl=en&gbpv=1&dq=ravendale+california+railroad&pg=PT197&printsec=frontcover|title=Railroads of Nevada and Eastern California|volume=III: More on the Northern Roads|first=David F.|last=Myrick|publisher=University of Nevada Press|year=2016|location=Reno|accessdate=2020-07-06}}</ref>

The Ravendale US post office opened in 1910, closed in 1920, and re-opened in 1921.<ref name=CGN /> The ZIP Code is 96123.

The county maintains an unattended airstrip at the town with the FAA identifier ''O39''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.airnav.com/airport/O39|publisher=AirNav.com|title=O39â€“Ravendale Airport|accessdate=2020-07-06}}</ref>

Susanville District, Ravendale Resource Area, [[Bureau of Land Management]] operates a fire station here. The fire station transmitter call sign is KMC407. There is also a Ravendale Volunteer Fire Department.<ref>''California Department of Forestry, Lassen-Modoc Ranger Unit 2005'' annual report</ref>

In 1939, Ravendale Elementary School school attendance dropped to zero students. The trustees questioned whether they were obligated to pay a teacher's salary and this matter was referred to [[California Attorney General]] [[Earl Warren]].  Warren ruled in favor of the teacher.<ref name="timpurdy">{{cite web |last1=Purdy |first1=Tim |title=Exploring Lassen County's Past: A Ravendale School Story  |url=https://www.tipurdy.org/a-ravendale-school-story/ |website=tipurdy |accessdate=14 July 2020}}</ref>
