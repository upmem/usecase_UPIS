'''Tatiana Martine Lemos de Lima Barbosa''' (born December 10, 1978) is a [[freestyle swimming|freestyle]] [[swimmer]] from [[Brazil]].<ref name="Sports Reference">{{cite web | title = Profile at Sports Reference | work = Sports Reference | year = 2013 | url = https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/le/tatiana-lemos-1.html | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20200418032200/https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/le/tatiana-lemos-1.html | url-status = dead | archive-date = April 18, 2020 | accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref>

In her career, she represented Minas Brasília, COPM de Brasília, Iate Clube de Brasília, AABB de Brasília, Clube do Exército de Brasília, [[CR Vasco da Gama]], [[Esporte Clube Pinheiros]] and [[Unisanta]]. She has been in two Olympic Games, four World Championships (Long Course), five World Championships (Short Course), four editions of the Pan American Games, and two editions of the Swimming World Military Championships.<ref name=retirement>{{cite web | title = The last of Tatiana | work = Best Swimming | date = December 17, 2013 | url = http://www.bestswimming.com.br/2013/2013/12/17/a-ultima-de-tatiana-lemos/ | accessdate = December 18, 2013 | language = pt | url-status = dead | archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20131219031400/http://www.bestswimming.com.br/2013/2013/12/17/a-ultima-de-tatiana-lemos/ | archivedate = December 19, 2013 }}</ref>

==International career==
She was at the [[1998 World Aquatics Championships]], in [[Perth]], where she finished 10th in the 4×200-metre freestyle, 13th in the 4×100-metre freestyle, and 41st in the 100-metre freestyle.<ref>{{cite web|title = Results at 1998 Perth|work=USA Swimming|year = 2013|url = http://www.usaswimming.org/_Rainbow/Documents/67dbbdc2-3dee-43bc-aec4-b9ae8805b5a7/98_wchamp.pdf|accessdate =May 11, 2013}}</ref>

She participated at the [[1999 FINA World Swimming Championships (25 m)]], in [[Hong Kong]].<ref>{{cite web | title = Best Swimming Profile | work = Best Swimming | date = July 11, 2009 | url = http://www.bestswimming.com.br/2013/2009/07/11/perfis-da-seleo-tatiana-lemos-barbosa-11397/ | accessdate = May 11, 2013 | language = pt | url-status = dead | archiveurl = https://archive.today/20130628013602/http://www.bestswimming.com.br/2013/2009/07/11/perfis-da-seleo-tatiana-lemos-barbosa-11397/ | archivedate = June 28, 2013 }}</ref>

Tatiana had a great debut in his first Pan, the [[1999 Pan American Games]] in [[Winnipeg]], with three bronze medals in three relays (4×100-metre freestyle, 4×200-metre freestyle and 4×100-metre medley).<ref>{{cite web | title = Brazilian Medals at 1999 Pan | work= UOL | year = 2007 | url = http://pan.uol.com.br/pan/2007/historia/1999/nopodio.jhtm | accessdate = April 7, 2013|language=pt}}</ref> She also finished 5th in the 100-metre freestyle, and 6th in the 50-metre freestyle.<ref>[http://www.swimnews.com/Magazine/1999/augmag99/aug99respanam.html 1999 Full results by Swimnews]</ref>

Participated in the [[2000 FINA World Swimming Championships (25 m)]] in [[Athens]], where she finished 22nd in the 50-metre freestyle,<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 50-metre freestyle at 2000 Athens | work = OmegaTiming | date = March 18, 2000 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=0001000E0034000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01|accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref> 25th in the 100-metre freestyle,<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 100-metre freestyle at 2000 Athens | work = OmegaTiming | date = March 16, 2000 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=0001000E0004000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01 | accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref> 9th in the 4×100-metre freestyle,<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×100-metre freestyle at 2000 Athens | work = OmegaTiming | date = March 18, 2000 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=0001000E003C000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01|accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref> and 9th in the 4×200-metre freestyle.<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×200-metre freestyle at 2000 Athens | work = OmegaTiming | date = March 16, 2000 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=0001000E000B000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01 | accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref>

She was at the [[2002 FINA World Swimming Championships (25 m)]] in [[Moscow]], reaching the 4×100-metre freestyle final, being in 8th place.<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×100-metre freestyle at 2002 Moscow | work = OmegaTiming | date = April 6, 2002 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=0001020C004F000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01|accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref> Also ranked 9th in the 4×200-metre freestyle.<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×200-metre freestyle at 2002 Moscow | work = OmegaTiming | date = April 3, 2002 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=0001020C0009000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01|accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref>

At the [[2003 Pan American Games]], in [[Santo Domingo]], she won another bronze in the 4×100-metre freestyle.<ref>{{cite web | title = Brazilian Medals at 2003 Pan | work= UOL | year = 2007 | url = http://pan.uol.com.br/pan/2007/historia/2003/nopodio.jhtm | accessdate = April 7, 2013|language=pt}}</ref>

At the [[2003 World Aquatics Championships]] in [[Barcelona]], finished 14th in the 4×100-metre freestyle.<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×100-metre freestyle at 2003 Barcelona| work = OmegaTiming | date = July 20, 2003 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=000103050006000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01|accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref>

Tatiana also entered the 4×100-metre freestyle relay in [[2004 Summer Olympics]], in [[Athens]]. She was the second to jump into the pool on August 14 that year. The team, which had yet [[Renata Burgos]], [[Rebeca Gusmão]] and [[Flávia Delaroli]], took 12th place in Olympic dispute. Tatiana was also in 19th place in the 100-metre freestyle.<ref name="Sports Reference" />

Participating in the [[2004 FINA World Swimming Championships (25 m)]], in [[Indianapolis]], Tatiana almost gets an historical medal, finishing in 4th place in the 4×100-metre freestyle.<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×100-metre freestyle at 2004 Indianapolis | work = OmegaTiming | date = October 10, 2004 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=000104050051000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01|accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref> She also gets 6th place in the 4×200-metre freestyle.<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×200-metre freestyle at 2004 Indianapolis | work = OmegaTiming | date = October 7, 2004 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=000104050013000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01 | accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref>

At the [[2007 Pan American Games]], in [[Rio de Janeiro]], Tatiana won the bronze in the 4×200-metre freestyle, along with [[Monique Ferreira]], [[Manuella Lyrio]] and [[Paula Baracho]].<ref>{{cite web | title = Women's Relay is bronze | work = Globoesporte | date = July 18, 2007 | url = http://globoesporte.globo.com/PAN/Noticias/0,,MUL72333-3871,00.html| accessdate = April 7, 2013|language=pt}}</ref> She also won the silver medal in the 4×100-metre freestyle, along with [[Flávia Delaroli]], [[Monique Ferreira]] and [[Rebeca Gusmão]]<ref>{{cite web | title = Brazil won silver in the women's 4×100-metre freestyle | work = Globoesporte | date = July 19, 2007 | url = http://globoesporte.globo.com/PAN/Noticias/0,,MUL73002-4020,00.html | accessdate = April 7, 2013|language=pt}}</ref> However, later this medal was revoked due to [[Rebeca Gusmão]]'s doping.

In May 2008, together with [[Flávia Delaroli]], [[Monique Ferreira]] and [[Michelle Lenhardt]], got new South American record of the 4×100-metre freestyle relay with a time of 3:43.16. The swimmer was almost out of the group that went to the Olympics. Her vacancy came only with the exclusion of [[Julyana Kury]], caught in an doping exam.

Participating in [[2008 Summer Olympics]] in [[Beijing]], [[China]], Tatiana finished 10th in the 4×100-metre medley, and 13th in the 4×100-metre freestyle.<ref name="Sports Reference" />

At the [[2009 World Aquatics Championships]] in [[Rome]], Tatiana ranked 29th in the 50-metre freestyle,<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 50-metre freestyle at 2009 Rome | work = OmegaTiming | date = August 1, 2009 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=0001090B0054000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01|accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref> 22nd in the 100-metre freestyle,<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 100-metre freestyle at 2009 Rome | work = OmegaTiming | date = July 30, 2009 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=0001090B0036000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01 | accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref> and was in the 4×100-metre medley final, finishing in 8th place.<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×100-metre medley at 2009 Rome | work = OmegaTiming | date = August 1, 2009 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=0001090B0061000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01|accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref>

She was at the [[2010 Pan Pacific Swimming Championships]] in [[Irvine, California|Irvine]], where she finished 6th in the 4×200-metre freestyle,<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×200-metre freestyle at 2010 Pan Pac in Irvine | work = OmegaTiming | date = August 19, 2010 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=00010A0B0026000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01 | accessdate = April 27, 2013}}</ref> 16th in the 50-metre freestyle,<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 50-metre freestyle at 2010 Pan Pac in Irvine | work = OmegaTiming | date = August 21, 2010 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=00010A0B0045000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01 | accessdate = April 27, 2013}}</ref> 21st in the 100-metre freestyle,<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 100-metre freestyle at 2010 Pan Pac in Irvine | work = OmegaTiming | date = August 19, 2010 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=00010A0B0014000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01 | accessdate = April 27, 2013}}</ref> and 23rd in the 200-metre freestyle.<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 200-metre freestyle at 2010 Pan Pac in Irvine | work = OmegaTiming | date = August 18, 2010 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=00010A0B0002000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01 | accessdate = April 27, 2013}}</ref>

In the [[Military World Games]], conducted in 2010 in [[Warendorf]], [[Germany]], she won the gold medal in the 100-metre freestyle and the 4×100-metre freestyle, and silver in the 50-metre freestyle, 200-metre freestyle and 4×100-metre medley.<ref>{{cite web | title = After success at the Pan Pacific, Brazil wins 14 golds at the World Military | work = Globoesporte | date = August 31, 2010 | url = http://globoesporte.globo.com/aquaticos/noticia/2010/08/apos-sucesso-no-pan-pacifico-brasil-conquista-14-ouros-no-mundial-militar.html|accessdate = April 7, 2013|language=pt}}</ref>

Participating in the [[2010 FINA World Swimming Championships (25 m)]], in [[Dubai]], came in 19th place in the 100-metre freestyle,<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 100-metre freestyle at 2010 Dubai | work = OmegaTiming | date = December 16, 2010 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=00010A000016000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01|accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref> 28th in the 200-metre freestyle<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 200-metre freestyle at 2010 Dubai | work = OmegaTiming | date = December 19, 2010 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=00010A000056000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01 |accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref> was in the 4×100-metre freestyle final, finishing 8th <ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×100-metre freestyle at 2010 Dubai | work = OmegaTiming | date = December 18, 2010 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=00010A000052000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01|accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref> and in the 4×100-metre medley, also finished 8th .<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×100-metre medley at 2010 Dubai | work = OmegaTiming | date = December 17, 2010 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=00010A00003F000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01 | accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref>

At the [[2011 World Aquatics Championships]] in [[Shanghai]], Tatiana got the 25th place in the 100-metre freestyle,<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 100-metre freestyle at 2011 Shanghai | work = OmegaTiming | date = July 28, 2011 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=00010B0D0039000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01|accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref> 13th in the 4×100-metre freestyle,<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×100-metre freestyle at 2011 Shanghai | work = OmegaTiming | date = July 24, 2011 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=00010B0D0006000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01 | accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref> and 17th in the 4×100-metre medley.<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×100-metre medley at 2011 Shanghai | work = OmegaTiming | date = July 30, 2011 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=00010B0D0059000000FFFFFFFFFFFF01|accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref>

At the [[2011 Pan American Games]] in [[Guadalajara]], Tatiana won silver in the 4×100-metre freestyle,<ref>{{cite web | title = Brazil is silver in relay | work = R7 | date = October 15, 2011 | url = http://rederecord.r7.com/pan-guadalajara-2011/noticias/brasil-e-prata-no-revezamento.html| accessdate = April 7, 2013|language=pt}}</ref> in the 4×200-metre freestyle,<ref>{{cite web | title = Brazil won silver in the women's 4×200-metre freestyle in Guadalajara | work = R7 | date = October 18, 2011 | url = http://rederecord.r7.com/pan-guadalajara-2011/noticias/brasil-ganha-prata-no-revezamento-4x200-m-em-guadalajara.html| accessdate = April 7, 2013|language=pt}}</ref> and bronze in the 4×100-metre medley.<ref>{{cite web | title = Brazil gets the bronze in the women's 4×100-metre medley | work = Lancenet | date = October 21, 2011 | url = http://www.lancenet.com.br/minuto/Brasil-fica-bronze-revezamento-medley_0_576542537.html | accessdate = April 7, 2013 | language = pt | url-status = dead | archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20111222025446/http://www.lancenet.com.br/minuto/Brasil-fica-bronze-revezamento-medley_0_576542537.html | archivedate = December 22, 2011 }}</ref>

At the [[2012 FINA World Swimming Championships (25 m)]], in [[Istanbul]], she reached the 4×100-metre freestyle final, placing 6th.<ref>{{cite web | title = Results of the 4×100-metre freestyle at 2012 Istanbul | work = OmegaTiming | date = December 15, 2012 | url = http://www.omegatiming.com/File/Download?id=00010C010D01003302FFFFFFFFFFFF02|accessdate = April 7, 2013}}</ref>

She ended her career in late 2013, at the Open tournament, held in [[Porto Alegre]], [[Brazil]].<ref name=retirement />

==Records==
Tatiana is the current holder of the following records:<ref>{{cite web | title = Women's Swimming Records | work = CBDA | year = 2013 | url = http://www.cbda.org.br/recordes/oficiais-femininos-id-266 | accessdate = May 11, 2013 | language = pt | url-status = dead | archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20121120074131/http://www.cbda.org.br/recordes/oficiais-femininos-id-266 | archivedate = November 20, 2012 }}</ref>

'''Long course (50 meters):'''

* Former South American record holder of the 100m freestyle: 54.72, time obtained on December 18, 2009
* Former South American record holder of the 4x100 freestyle: 3:41.49, time obtained on September 6, 2009, along with [[Michelle Lenhardt]], [[Monique Ferreira]] and [[Julyana Kury]]
* South American record holder of the 4 × 100 m medley: 3:58.49, time obtained on August 1, 2009, along with [[Fabíola Molina]], [[Carolina Mussi]] and [[Gabriella Silva]]

'''Short course (25 meters):'''

* Former South American record holder of the 100m freestyle: 53.19, time obtained on November 11, 2009
* South American record holder of the 200m freestyle: 1:56.43, time obtained on November 14, 2009
* Former South American record holder of the 4x100 freestyle: 3:35.95, time obtained on December 18, 2010, along with [[Flávia Delaroli]], [[Michelle Lenhardt]] and [[Julyana Kury]]
* Former South American record holder of the 4 × 200 m freestyle: 8:01.78, time obtained on September 9, 2005, along with [[Paula Baracho]], [[Manuella Lyrio]] and [[Joanna Maranhão]]
