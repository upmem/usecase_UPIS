'''Alfonso González Fernández''' (born 16 July 1951), commonly known as '''Alfonsín''', is a Spanish retired [[Association football|footballer]] who played as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]], and a [[Coach (sport)|manager]].

==Football career==
Born in [[Cortegana]], [[Province of Huelva|Huelva]], [[Andalusia]], Alfonsín began working as a coach with [[UD Almería B]], being promoted to [[Tercera División]] in 2002. On 26 January 2004, he was appointed at the helm of [[UD Almería|the main squad]], replacing fired [[Juan Martínez Martínez|Casuco]].<ref>[http://www.libertaddigital.com/deportes/alfonsin-nuevo-entrenador-del-almeria-tras-el-cese-de-juan-martinez-casuco-1276213111/ Alfonsín, nuevo entrenador del Almería tras el cese de Juan Martínez Casuco (Alfonsín, new manager of Almería after the firing of Juan Martínez Casuco)]; Libertad Digital, 26 January 2004 (in Spanish)</ref>

However, Alfonsín only remained one game in charge, being later replaced by [[Luis Ángel Duque]]. After the latter's resignment in April he returned,<ref>[http://masdeporte.as.com/masdeporte/2004/04/07/polideportivo/1081311337_850215.html Duque: "No comparto la decisión del Consejo" (Duque: "I don't share the council's decision")]; [[Diario AS]], 7 April 2004 (in Spanish)</ref> narrowly avoiding relegation despite [[2003–04 Segunda División|finishing 13th]].

Alfonsín returned to the ''Rojiblancos'' on 29 January 2005, replacing [[Fernando Castro Santos]].<ref>[http://www.lavozdegalicia.es/hemeroteca/2005/01/29/3415822.shtml Alfonsín se olvida de las bajas y pide profesionalidad (Alfonsín forgets injured players and calls for professionalism)] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141023023956/http://www.lavozdegalicia.es/hemeroteca/2005/01/29/3415822.shtml |date=2014-10-23 }}; [[La Voz de Galicia]], 29 January 2005 (in Spanish)</ref> After being in charge for five matches, he became [[Fabriciano González|Fabri]]'s assistant the following month.<ref>[http://futbol.as.com/futbol/2005/02/16/mas_futbol/1108538846_850215.html Fabri y Lorenzana toman las riendas del Almería (Fabri and Lorenzana take Almería's reins)]; Diario AS, 16 February 2005 (in Spanish)</ref>

In November 2007, Alfonsín returned to Almería's reserves,<ref>[http://www.joinfutbol.com/es/alfonsin_es_el_nuevo_entrenador_del_almeria_b_4955.php Alfonsín es el nuevo entrenador del UD Almería B (Alfonsín is the new manager of UD Almería B)] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141021040236/http://www.joinfutbol.com/es/alfonsin_es_el_nuevo_entrenador_del_almeria_b_4955.php |date=2014-10-21 }}; Join Futbol, 5 November 2007 (in Spanish)</ref> still in the fourth level. A third spell befell in the [[2010–11 Segunda División B|2010–11 season]], and he led them to a comfortable mid-table position in [[Segunda División B]] in their first experience.
