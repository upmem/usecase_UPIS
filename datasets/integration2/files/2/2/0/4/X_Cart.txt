X-Cart, a division of Seller Labs,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Vydrin |first1=Max |title=We’ve Joined the Seller Labs Family — and Here are the Details |url=https://www.x-cart.com/blog/we-joined-seller-labs-details.html |website=X-Cart |publisher=X-Cart |accessdate=17 November 2020}}</ref> is a US-based company headquartered in [[Athens, Georgia|Athens, GA]]. It is also the name of its commercial [[shopping cart]] platform for [[Online store|online stores]] and the [[development agency]]. X-Cart was designed to fit the needs of online stores of any size, but works best for large stores and marketplaces. 

It was originally released in 2001 by Creative Technologies LLC, a web development company that later worked on Ecwid. Upon release, it was the world's first [[PHP]] shopping cart software.<ref name="rbth">{{cite web|title=E-commerce platform from Ulyanovsk goes global|url=http://in.rbth.com/economics/2014/09/12/e-commerce_platform_from_ulyanovsk_goes_global_38251.html|publisher=Russia Beyond The Headlines|author= Leonid Mikheyev|date=12 September 2014|accessdate=16 October 2014}}</ref> Today, X-Cart is reported to be used by over 30,000 [[E-Store|e-stores]] in 111 countries around the world.<ref name="interview">{{cite web|url=http://www.shopping-cart-migration.com/blog/73-experts-insights/22533-interview-with-x-cart-ceo-we-need-to-be-ready-for-the-mobile-age|title=Interview with X-Cart CEO: We Need to be Ready for the Mobile Age|publisher=Cart2Cart|author=|date=16 May 2014|accessdate=16 October 2014}}</ref>
 

== History ==
Back in 2001, a serial [[Entrepreneurship|entrepreneur]] [[Ruslan Fazlyev]] and his friends saw online stores emerging around the world and, at the same time, the lack of tools for building them quickly and efficiently. They developed X-Cart as the first-in-market open source PHP eCommerce platform. 

In 2009, Creative Technologies released a [[freemium]] eCommerce solution Ecwid, in addition to the X-Cart line, which became an independent [[eCommerce]] solution later on.<ref name="reuters">{{cite web|title=Ecwid Completes First Venture Funding From Runa Capital, Announces Silicon Valley, London Presence|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/2011/12/14/idUS172637+14-Dec-2011+BW20111214|publisher=Reuters|author=|date=14 December 2011|accessdate=16 October 2014}}</ref><ref name="tfa">{{cite web|title=X-Cart ECommerce Software Review|url=http://ecommerce-software.topfiveawards.com/x-cart-ecommerce-software.htm|publisher=Top Five Awards|author=|date=2014|accessdate=16 October 2014}}</ref>

In 2009, X-Cart launched its PCI Level 1 certified payment application, X-Payments, that is used for easier PCI DSS compliance by online retailers using X-Cart, [[Magento]], [[WooCommerce]], and Zoey.

In 2011, [[Runa Capital]] invested in X-Cart. 

In 2013, X-Cart launched its own hosting infrastructure, allowing all X-Cart users to host their online stores on their servers. 

In 2014, X-Cart released its new product, named X-Cart 5, that quickly gained popularity for its modern modular architecture. At the same time, X-Cart Classic, also known as X-Cart 4, continued to be developed and updated.<ref name="interview"/> By September 2014 X-Cart Cloud was listed by SitePoint as one of the best hosted e-commerce platforms.<ref name="sitepoint">{{cite web|title=10 of the Best Hosted Ecommerce Platforms|url=http://www.sitepoint.com/10-best-ecommerce-platforms/|publisher=SitePoint|author=Jacco Blankenspoor|date=23 September 2014|accessdate=16 October 2014}}</ref> Among other features, X-Cart Cloud allows certified developers to add custom functionality to customers’ hosted shops.<ref name="interview"/><ref name="next">{{cite web|title=Look to the Cloud for X-Cart's NEXT Service|url=http://www.websitemagazine.com/content/blogs/ecommerce/archive/2013/03/14/look-to-the-cloud-for-x-cart-s-next-service.aspx|publisher=Website Magazine|author=|date=14 March 2014|accessdate=16 October 2014}}</ref>

In 2017, X-Cart tech support and customer care teams expanded to offer 24/7 support for customers and businesses all over the globe. 

In October 2019, the leading [[Amazon (company)|Amazon]] marketplace expert company. <ref>{{cite web |title=XCART- a PHP based shopping cart |url=https://aws.amazon.com/marketplace/pp/Miri-Infotech-XCART-a-PHP-based-shopping-cart/B07TJKFWLQ |website=AWS Marketplace |publisher=Miri Infotech |accessdate=17 November 2020}}</ref>

In 2020, X-Cart moved to offering full solutions for enterprise online stores and [[B2B e-commerce|B2B]] companies. 

== Products and Services ==
{{bulleted list |X-Cart Classic, or X-Cart 4 (no longer available to new customers) |X-Cart 5 |X-Cart Hosting |Solution Services |[[Mobile app development|Mobile apps development]]|Full-service migration from other platforms}}

X-Cart offers integration with various shipping carriers([[USPS]], [[United Parcel Service|UPS]], [[FedEx]]), payment providers and applications ([[PayPal]], [[Apple Pay]], [[Amazon Pay]]), tax automation systems (Avalara), marketing systems ([[Mailchimp]]). An additional X-Payments app makes it [[PA-DSS]] certified and adds support for 39 more payment providers in addition to the 14 in the basic package. It also ensures fraud screening for payments. Among other features, X-Cart allows certified developers to add custom functionality to customers’ hosted shops.<ref name="merchantmaverick">{{cite web|title=X-Cart Review|url= http://www.merchantmaverick.com/reviews/xcart-review/|publisher=Merchant Maverick|author=Amad Ebrahimi|date=1 August 2014|accessdate=16 October 2014}}</ref><ref name="monsterpost">{{cite web|title=Deeper Look into X-Cart eCommerce Engine |url=http://blog.templatemonster.com/2011/07/14/x-cart-ecommerce-review/|publisher=MonsterPost|author=Helga Weber|date=14 July 2011|accessdate=26 October 2014}}</ref><ref name="features">{{cite web|title=X-Cart's features|url= http://www.x-cart.com/features.html|publisher=X-Cart|author=|date=|accessdate=16 October 2014}}</ref>
