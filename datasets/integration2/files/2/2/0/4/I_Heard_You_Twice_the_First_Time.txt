'''''I Heard You Twice the First Time''''' is a [[jazz]] album by [[Branford Marsalis]] that explores different aspects of the [[blues]],<ref name="people" /> featuring guest appearances from [[B.B. King]], [[John Lee Hooker]], [[Russell Malone]], [[Wynton Marsalis]] and [[Linda Hopkins]]. It peaked at number 1 on the Top Jazz Albums chart.<ref>[{{Allmusic|class=album|id=mw0000084782|pure_url=yes}} Allmusic Guide]</ref> The album won the [[Grammy Awards|Grammy Award]] for Best Jazz Instrumental Performance, Individual or Group.<ref name="branford">{{cite web |url=http://www.branfordmarsalis.com/branford/pbuild/linkbuilder.cfm?selection=dn9.9.22 |title=I Heard You Twice the First Time |work=Branford Marsalis.com |accessdate=2014-10-16 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20020107205248/http://www.branfordmarsalis.com/branford/pbuild/linkbuilder.cfm?selection=dn9.9.22 |archivedate=2002-01-07 }}</ref>

==Reception==
In his [[AllMusic]] review, [[Scott Yanow]] calls the album "interesting if erratic," specifically identifying the performance by Linda Hopkins and concluding that this is an "intriguing set that is worth picking up."<ref name="allmusic" /> ''[[People Magazine]]'' praised the album, calling out the "four distinct blues-guitar styles" and the "verve and execution" of the quartet numbers as high points.<ref name="people">{{cite journal |url=http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20109858,00.html |title=Picks and Pans Review: I Heard You Twice the First Time |work=[[People Magazine]] |accessdate=2014-10-16 |date=1993-03-01}}</ref> ''[[Entertainment Weekly]]'' characterized Marsalis as "a commanding, even great, saxophonist of the post-hard-bop school, but a wayward conceptualist," and criticized the album as suffering from a "serious identity crisis."<ref name="ew" />
