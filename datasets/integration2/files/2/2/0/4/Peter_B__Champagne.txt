{{short description|American businessman and politician}}
'''Peter B. Champagne''' (December 8, 1845 – July 1, 1891) was an American businessman and politician.

Born in [[Saint-Félix-de-Valois, Quebec]], Champagne settled in [[Wisconsin Rapids, Wisconsin]], in 1863 and was in the logging business. In 1880, Champagne helped started a saw mill at [[Grandfather Falls]] and also started a mercantile store in [[Wausau, Wisconsin]]. In 1880, Champagne moved to [[Jenny Bull Falls, Wisconsin]], and opened a store. He founded a railway and a lumber business. He served on the [[Lincoln County, Wisconsin]], Board of Supervisors. He served as village president and then on the common council when Jenny Bulls Falls became a city. In 1883, Champaign served in the [[Wisconsin State Assembly]] and was a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]]. He died in [[Merrill, Wisconsin]].<ref>[http://marathoncountyhistory.com/PeopleDetails.php?PeopleId=492&View=C&ItemName= Marathon County, Wisconsin Historical Society-Peter B.
