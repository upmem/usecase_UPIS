'''Alex James Murphy''' (born June 3, 1993) is an American-Finnish [[basketball]] player for [[Fundación CB Granada]] of the [[Liga Española de Baloncesto|LEB Oro]]. He played college basketball for [[Duke University]], the [[University of Florida]] and [[Northeastern University]]. He is the son of former [[National Basketball Association]] (NBA) player [[Jay Murphy]], and the brother of former NBA forward [[Erik Murphy]]. He plays for the [[Finland national basketball team|Finnish national team]].

==High school career==
Murphy was ranked as the No. 41 player in the [[ESPNU]] 100,<ref name="ESPN CBR">{{cite web | url=http://espn.go.com/mens-college-basketball/recruiting/player/_/id/77055/alex-murphy | title=Alex Murphy 2012 Basketball Recruiting Profile | accessdate=April 6, 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110226021704/http://espn.go.com/mens-college-basketball/recruiting/player/_/id/77055/alex-murphy| archivedate= February 26, 2011 | url-status= live}}</ref> the No. 45 player by [[Rivals.com]], and the No. 41 player by [[Scout.com]].  He attended St. Mark's School in Southborough, Massachusetts, where he played alongside [[Kaleb Tarczewski]] and [[Nik Stauskas]].  Murphy led St. Mark's School to a 27–3 record as a junior. Murphy was also named NEPSAC ISL Player of the Year in 2011. In April 2011, he decided to forgo his senior year of high school and join the class of 2011. This allowed Murphy to graduate in the spring of 2011 and attend Duke University in the fall of that year.  Murphy left St. Mark's School and attended [[South Kingstown High School]] for the remaining months in order to graduate and enroll at Duke that summer.<ref name="Max Preps">{{cite web | url=http://www.maxpreps.com/high-schools/8rgaLEojl0SyDNFPysBS8w/st-marks-lions/basketball/home.htm | title=St. Mark's High School Basketball | accessdate=April 6, 2011}}</ref>

Murphy committed to play basketball at [[Duke men's basketball|Duke]] on February 7, 2011.  Recruiting columnists speculated that he would commit to the [[University of Florida]] because his older brother, Erik Murphy, was a four-year player under coach [[Billy Donovan]].  Murphy chose [[Duke men's basketball|Duke]] over Florida, Arizona, Boston College, North Carolina, Villanova and West Virginia.<ref name="Alex Murphy Picks Duke">{{cite web | url=http://sports.espn.go.com/ncaa/recruiting/basketball/mens/news/story?id=6098590 | title=Junior Alex Murphy picks Duke | accessdate=April 6, 2011 | author=Finkelstein, Adam | date=February 7, 2011}}</ref>

==Reclassification==
On April 19, 2011, Murphy announced that he would be graduating a year early from [[St. Mark's School (Massachusetts)|St. Mark's School]] and would enroll at [[Duke University]] a year earlier than expected.<ref name="sports.espn.go.com">[http://sports.espn.go.com/ncaa/recruiting/basketball/mens/news/story?id=6389471 Alex Murphy becomes part of Duke's 2011 class. – ESPN]. Sports.espn.go.com (April 19, 2011). Retrieved on 2012-06-29.</ref> Having met all academic requirements necessary to do this, Murphy was able to enroll at Duke in the summer of 2011, and was the fifth member of Duke's 2011 recruiting class.

'''Murphy on why he reclassified: '''"It was a very difficult decision but at the end of the day, I thought this was the best thing for my future," Murphy said. "It's a great opportunity."<ref name="sports.espn.go.com"/>

==College==

===Redshirt (2011-2012)===
In December 2011, Murphy decided to [[Redshirt (college sports)|redshirt]] his freshman season with fellow recruit [[Marshall Plumlee]]. Murphy said, "It's still to be determined, but a redshirt year is most likely what I'm going to do. That's the plan right now." after No. 5 Duke beat Washington, 86–80, at Madison Square Garden.<ref name="zagsblog.com">[http://www.zagsblog.com/2011/12/10/dukes-alex-murphy-ready-for-redshirt/ Duke’s Alex Murphy Ready for Redshirt]. ZagsBlog.com (December 10, 2011). Retrieved on 2012-06-29.</ref>

==Professional career==
On August 4, 2017, Murphy signed his first professional contract with [[Joensuun Kataja]] of the Finnish [[Korisliiga]].<ref>{{cite web| last = | first = | title = Susijengi-debytantti Alex Murphy aloittaa ammattilaisuransa Kataja Basketissa – "On upeaa päästä osaksi voittamisen kulttuuria" | work = [[Kataja Basket Club]] | date = August 4, 2017 | url = http://www.katajabasket.fi/susijengi-debytantti-alex-murphy-aloittaa-ammattilaisuransa-kataja-basketissa-on-upeaa-paasta-osaksi-voittamisen-kulttuuria/ | accessdate = August 10, 2017 |language = Finnish}}</ref>

On July 13, 2018, Murphy signed a one year deal with [[Szolnoki Olaj KK]] of the [[Hungarian League]].

On August 5, 2019, he signed with [[San Sebastián Gipuzkoa BC|Delteco Gipuzkoa Basket]] of the [[Liga Española de Baloncesto|LEB Oro]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sportando.basketball/en/alex-murphy-signs-with-delteco-gipuzkoa/|title=Alex Murphy signs with Delteco Gipuzkoa|date=August 5, 2019|publisher=Sportando|language=En|access-date=August 7, 2019}}</ref>

==International==
Murphy played for Finland at the FIBA U20 European Championships in the summer of 2012.<ref>[http://www.goduke.com/ViewArticle.dbml?SPSID=22724&SPID=1845&DB_LANG=C&DB_OEM_ID=4200&ATCLID=205530996 Murphy Makes a Name for Himself at FIBA U20]</ref>
Murphy is now a member of the Finnish men's national team.
