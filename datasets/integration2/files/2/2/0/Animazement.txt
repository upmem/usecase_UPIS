'''Animazement''' is an annual three-day [[anime convention]] held during May at the [[Raleigh Convention Center]] in [[Raleigh, North Carolina]]. It is traditionally held over [[Memorial Day]] weekend. The convention is designed to be family-friendly.<ref name="NO20130526"/><ref name="rcnh05182017">{{cite news|last1=Taylor|first1=Holly|title=Broadening your cultural horizons|url=http://www.roanoke-chowannewsherald.com/2017/05/18/broadening-your-cultural-horizons/|accessdate=27 June 2017|work=Roanoke-Chowan News-Herald|date=May 18, 2017}}</ref> Animazement is run by unpaid staff and volunteers.<ref name="NO20140523">{{cite news | work = News & Observer | title = Animazement’s dream world comes alive in Raleigh | date = 2014-05-23 | last = Kenney | first = Andrew | url = http://www.newsobserver.com/2014/05/23/3883729/dozens-of-rules-and-hundreds-of.html | accessdate = 2014-06-01}}</ref>

==Programming==
The convention typically offers an artist alley, classes, concerts, cosplay chess, cosplay contests, dealers room, gaming tournaments, manga library, masquerade ball, musicians, panel discussions, raves, various gaming rooms (arcade, console, retro), video screenings, and voice actors.<ref name="techonline16"/><ref name="NO20130526">{{cite news | work = News & Observer | title = Animazement dazzles with color, form, ingenuity | date = 2013-05-26 | last = Murawski | first = John | url = http://www.newsobserver.com/news/local/article10347641.html | accessdate = 2013-06-01}}</ref><ref name="et20130525">{{cite news | work = Epoch Times | title = Animazement Opens its Doors for 2013 | date = 2013-05-25 | last = Padula | first = Derek | url = http://www.theepochtimes.com/n3/74681-animazement-opens-its-doors-for-2013/ | accessdate = 2013-06-01}}</ref><ref name="techonline12">{{cite news | work = Technician Online | title = Bringing fantasies to life | date = 2012-05-30 | last = Jung | first = Jennifer | url = http://www.technicianonline.com/article_c364fb9c-4d91-51cc-a93a-254a63762b0a.html | accessdate = 2012-06-26}}</ref><ref name="indyweek09">{{cite web | last = Smith| first = Zack | url = http://www.indyweek.com/indyweek/you-havent-met-fankids-until-youve-been-to-animazement/Content?oid=1215774 | title = You haven't met fankids until you've been to Animazement | publisher = Independent Weekly | accessdate = 2012-06-26}}</ref><ref name="techonline13">{{cite news | work = Technician Online | title = Animazement shows growth, change | date = 2013-05-30 | last = Hart | first = Bryce | url = http://www.technicianonline.com/features/article_f290fe28-c8df-11e2-88b0-0019bb30f31a.html | accessdate = 2013-06-01}}</ref>

Animazement raised over $12,000 for several charities in 2015.<ref name="ann2016char">{{cite web|title=Animazement Announces 2016 Charities|url=http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/press-release/2016-04-24/animazement-announces-2016-charities/.101409|website=Anime News Network|accessdate=19 June 2016|date=April 24, 2016}}</ref>

==History==
The convention originates from a 1997 36-hour anime marathon, organized by the Triangle Area Anime Society (TAAS) at [[North Carolina State University]].<ref name="NO2012-05-24" /> It was first held in Raleigh, North Carolina at the North Raleigh Hilton in 1998, moved to the Sheraton Imperial Hotel and Convention Center in Durham, North Carolina for 2001, and to the month of May in 2002.<ref name="AC-1998" /><ref name="AC-2001" /><ref name="AC-2002" /> In 2009, four Japanese guests canceled appearances due to quarantine concerns over the [[Swine influenza|Swine flu]].<ref name="annswine">{{cite web | url = http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2009-05-15/4-japanese-guests-cancel-animazement-trip-due-to-swine-flu-concerns | title = 4 Japanese Guests Cancel Animazement Trip due to Swine Flu Concerns | publisher = Anime News Network | accessdate = 2012-06-24}}</ref> In 2009, Animazement moved to the Raleigh Convention Center.<ref name="AC-2009" /> [[Noboru Ishiguro]] was scheduled to attend the 2012 event, but died prior to the event.<ref name="annishiguro">{{cite web | url = http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2012-03-21/yamato-macross-director-noboru-ishiguro-passes-away | title = Yamato, Macross Director Noboru Ishiguro Passes Away | publisher = Anime News Network | accessdate = 2012-06-24}}</ref> The convention celebrated its 20th anniversary in 2017.<ref name="sn05262017">{{cite news|title=Thousands in Downtown Raleigh for Annual Animazement Convention|url=http://www.twcnews.com/nc/triangle-sandhills/news/2017/05/26/thousands-in-downtown-raleigh-for-annual-animazement-convention.html#/0|accessdate=27 June 2017|work=Spectrum News|date=May 26, 2017}}</ref>

Animazement 2020 was cancelled due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref name="animazement2020">{{cite web |title=Animazement 2020 Cancellation |url=https://animazement.com/2020/03/31/animazement-2020-cancellation/ |website=Animazement |accessdate=1 April 2020}}</ref><ref name="ann03292020">{{cite news |last1=Hodgkins |first1=Crystalyn |last2=Sherman |first2=Jennifer |title=Animazement Suspends Convention Due to COVID-19 Concerns (Updated) |url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2020-03-29/animazement-suspends-convention-due-to-covid-19-concerns/.158062 |accessdate=1 April 2020 |work=Anime News Network |date=March 29, 2020 |language=en}}</ref><ref name="AC-2020">{{cite web | url = http://animecons.com/events/info/14596/animazement-2020 | title = Animazement 2020 Information | publisher = AnimeCons.com | accessdate = 2020-04-01}}</ref>
