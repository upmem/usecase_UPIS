'''Joseph Kingsley Baffour Senkyire''' was a [[Ghanaian]] academic, politician and diplomat. He was the member of parliament for the [[Duayaw Nkwanta|Duayaw-Nkwanta]] constituency from 1965 to 1966.<ref name="Duayaw-Nkwanta">{{Cite journal |date=1965 |title=Parliamentary Debates; Official Report, Part 2 |url=https://books.google.com/?id=fwpIAQAAIAAJ&dq=Senkyire+duayaw |journal=Parliamentary Debates |publisher=Ghana National Assembly |page=9 and iv}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal |year=1965 |title=West Africa Annual, Issue 8 |url=https://books.google.com/?id=y03jAAAAMAAJ&q=Senkyire |publisher=James Clarke |page=81}}</ref> and [[Embassy of Ghana in Washington, D.C.#List of Ambassadors|Ghana's Ambassador to United States of America]] from 1979 to 1981.<ref name="ambassador">{{cite journal |title=Ghana News, Volumes 8–12 |url=https://books.google.com/?id=XAwbx_PKGxsC&pg=RA5-PA5&dq=Baffuor-Senkyire#v=onepage |publisher=Washington, D.C. : Embassy of Ghana |date=1979 |page= 5 |journal=Ghana News}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Baffuor-Senkyire was born on 19 March, 1928. He was the second child of Nana Afua Birago, the late queen mother of the [[Duayaw Nkwanta]] Traditional area and Opanin Kwame Adiyea who hailed from Adugyama.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE">{{cite web|url=http://members.tripod.com/sayit_2/obituary.html |title=Dr Joseph Kingsley Baffour Senkyire |publisher=members.tripod.com |accessdate=1 May 2020}}</ref> He had his early education at the [[Bechem]] Catholic Boys School and his secondary education from [[St. Augustine's College (Cape Coast)|St. Augustine's College]], [[Cape Coast]] from 1943 until 1948 when he obtained his Cambridge School Certificate. In 1958, he won a Government Scholarship to study at the American [[Purdue University]].<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/> There, he studied Agriculture for his [[Bachelor's degree|Bachelors']] and [[Masters' degree|Masters']] degrees from 1956 to 1962.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/><ref name="purdue">{{Cite journal |date=1978 |title=Sixteenth Street Architecture, Volume 2 |url=https://books.google.com/?id=_2dK3n--wAkC&pg=PA445&dq=Baffuor-Senkyire+born#v=onepage |publisher=Commission of Fine Arts |page=445}}</ref> Baffuor-Senkyire later returned to Purdue University to pursue a doctorate degree in Agriculture and was awarded the degree in 1971.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/><ref name="purdue" /> In 1979, he was awarded an honorary [[doctor of letters]] degree by Purdue University.<ref>{{cite journal |title=Ghana News, Volumes 8–12 |url=https://books.google.com/?id=XAwbx_PKGxsC&pg=RA14-PA2&dq=Senkyire+purdue#v=onepage |publisher=Washington, D.C. : Embassy of Ghana |date=1979 |page=2 |journal=Ghana News}}</ref>

==Career==
===Academia and civil service===
After his secondary education Baffuor-Senkyire taught at the Government School at Asem in [[Kumasi]] prior to joining the civil service.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/> From 1954 to 1956, he worked at the Information Service Department (now the [[Ghana Broadcasting Corporation]]).<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/>

After his studies in the United States of America, he returned to Ghana in 1962 and joined the teaching staff of the [[Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology|University of Science and Technology]] (now the [[Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology]]).<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/><ref name="purdue" /> There, he worked as a Lecturer in Animal Husbandry and in 1977, he was elevated to the rank of a Senior Lecturer.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/>

Following the overthrow of the [[Limann government]] on 31 December 1981, Baffuor-Senkyire returned to his alma mater, [[Purdue University]] as a visiting professor in the Department of Agricultural Economics on a two-year contract.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/> He returned to Ghana in 1986 and rejoined the Faculty of Agriculture of the University of Science and Technology.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/> He retired in 1988.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/>

===Politics and foreign service===
Baffuor-Senkyire was elected into parliament to represent the [[Duayaw Nkwanta]] constituency on the ticket of the [[Convention People's Party]] (CPP) in 1965.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/> He served in this position until the overthrow of the [[Nkrumah government]] on 24 February 1966.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/>

At the inception of the [[Third Republic of Ghana|Third Republic]] in 1979, Baffuor-Senkyire was appointed [[Embassy of Ghana in Washington, D.C.#List of Ambassadors|Ghana's Ambassador to United States of America]].<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/><ref name="purdue" /><ref name="ambassador" /> He held this appointment until the overthrow of the [[Limann government]] on 31 December 1981.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/>

==Personal life and death==
After his retirement, Baffuor-Senkyire started a funeral undertaker's business.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/> He was taken ill on 5 February, 1999 and admitted at the Bomso Clinic in [[Kumasi]]. He died on 8 February, 1999.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/> He was survived by his wife, Charlotte Baffuor-Senkyire and five children.<ref name="BAFFUOR-SENKYIRE"/>
