'''Joseph Coëtnempren de Kersaint''' (29 November 1746 – [[Isle de France (Mauritius)|Isle de France]] ([[Mauritius]]), 1797){{sfnp|Roussel|Forrer|2019|p=61}}  was a [[France|French]] Navy officer.

== Biography ==
Joseph Coëtnempren de Kersaint was born to the family of [[Guy François Coëtnempren de Kersaint]], a French Navy officer. His two brothers, [[Armand de Kersaint]] (1742–1793) and [[Guy Pierre Kersaint]] (1747–1822), also served in the Navy.

Kersaint joined the Navy at a [[Garde-Marine]] on 25 July 1762. He was promoted to Ensign on 15 September 1771, and to Lieutenant on 5 February 1786.{{sfnp|Roussel|Forrer|2019|p=61}}

He served on ''Heure du Berger'' from November 1767 to January 1769. {{sfnp|Roussel|Forrer|2019|p=61}}

In 1776, he served on the 40-gun frigate [[French frigate Consolante (1775)|''Consolante'']].{{sfnp|Roussel|Forrer|2019|p=61}} In June 1778, he transferred to the 64-gun [[French ship Brillant (1774)|''Brillant'']].{{sfnp|Roussel|Forrer|2019|p=61}}

Kersaint married at Isle de France on 3 April 1780.{{sfnp|Roussel|Forrer|2019|p=61}}

In June 1782, Kersaint, by then retired from the Navy,{{sfnp|Cunat|1852|p=251}} departed Brest, captaining the corvette ''Duc de Chartes''. He arrived at Achem on 24 November.{{sfnp|Lacour-Gayet|1910|p=538}}
