The '''1977–78 season''' was [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]]'s [[List of Paris Saint-Germain F.C. seasons|8th season]] in existence.<ref name="saison78">{{cite news 
| title = Saison 1977-1978
| url = https://histoiredupsg.fr/saison-1977-1978
| work = Histoire du PSG
| accessdate = 1 May 2020}}</ref><ref name="foot78">{{cite news
| title = Paris S-G 1977/1978
| url = https://www.footballdatabase.eu/en/club/team/3-paris_sg/1977-1978
| work = Footballdatabase.eu
| accessdate = 1 May 2020}}</ref> PSG mainly played their home league games at the [[Parc des Princes]] in [[Paris]], but once at the [[Stade de Paris]] in [[Saint-Ouen-sur-Seine]] as well, registering an average attendance of 21,754 spectators per match.<ref name="saison78"/><ref name="foot78"/><ref>{{cite news
|title = Average Team Attendances Paris-SG
|url = http://www.stadium-attendances.com/Paris-SG,historical,attendances.html
|work = Stadium and Attendances
|accessdate = 19 March 2020}}</ref> The club was presided by [[Daniel Hechter]] until January 1978, when [[Francis Borelli]] became the new president.<ref name="saison78"/> The team was coached by [[Player-coach|player-manager]] [[Jean-Michel Larqué]].<ref name="saison78"/><ref name="foot78"/><ref name="Larqué">{{cite news
| title = Jean-Michel Larqué entraîneur-joueur au PSG - L'interview
| url = https://www.francebleu.fr/sports/football/jean-michel-larque-1566913515
| work = France Bleu
| date = 28 August 2019
| accessdate = 2 May 2020}}</ref> [[Mustapha Dahleb]] was the team captain.<ref name="captain">{{cite news
| title = saison 1977-1978
| url = http://www.paris-canalhistorique.com/saison-1977-1978/
| work = Paris.canal-historique
| date = 21 June 2014
| accessdate = 2 May 2020}}</ref>

In January 1978, [[Daniel Hechter]] was banned for life from football by the [[French Football Federation]] for running a ticketing scheme at [[Parc des Princes]].<ref name="finances">{{cite news
| title = Political and Organizational Factors of PSG
| url = https://firstrosettastoneassignment.weebly.com/political-and-organizational-factors-of-psg.html
| work = Sports and Leisure in France
| accessdate = 10 March 2019}}</ref><ref name="hechter">{{cite news
| title = 1973 - 1978 : Paris se replace sur la scène française
| url = https://parisunited.fr/oeil-du-supporter/1973-1978-paris-se-replace-sur-la-scene-francaise/
| work = Paris United
| date = 19 November 2018
| accessdate = 7 March 2019}}</ref><ref name="greatestpsg">{{cite news 
| title = The Greatest French Club Sides Of All Time – Part 3
| url = http://frenchfootballweekly.com/2011/07/19/the-greatest-french-club-sides-of-all-time-–-part-3/
| work = French Football Weekly
| date = 19 July 2011
| accessdate = 26 October 2019}}</ref> On January 8, 1978, PSG beat [[Olympique de Marseille]] 5–1 in his final match at the stadium as club president. [[Mustapha Dahleb]] offered Hechter the match ball after the final whistle and was then carried in triumph by the players while the fans in attendance chanted his name.<ref name="hechter"/> The next day, [[Francis Borelli]], who had been vice-president until then, became the new president.<ref name="hechter"/><ref name="psg70">{{cite news
| title = Histoire du Paris Saint Germain
| url = http://psg70.free.fr/historique.htm
| work = PSG70
| accessdate = 2 April 2019}}</ref> [[Carlos Bianchi]], who arrived to Paris in June 1977, was Hechter's last big signing. The Argentine striker scored 37 goals in 38 league matches.<ref name="psg70"/>
