The '''1983–84 season''' was [[Colchester United F.C.|Colchester United's]] 42nd season in their history and third consecutive season in fourth tier of [[Football in England|English football]], the [[Football League Fourth Division|Fourth Division]]. Alongside competing in the Fourth Division, the club also participated in the [[FA Cup]], the [[EFL Cup|League Cup]] and the [[Football League Trophy|Associate Members' Cup]].

Another promotion push ended a long way short with an eighth-placed finish, 15 points shy of the promotion places. Colchester reached the third round of the League Cup, where they faced [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] at [[Layer Road]] only to suffer a 2–0 defeat. They also reached the third round of the FA Cup, exiting to [[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]]. In the inaugural [[EFL Trophy|Associate Members' Cup]], the U's fell to a 2–0 defeat to Essex rivals [[Southend United F.C.|Southend United]] in the second round.

==Season overview==
[[Cyril Lea]] began his first full season in charge by bringing in assistant [[Stewart Houston]] in a [[player-coach]] capacity. He had however already seen the frustrating exit of last seasons top scorer [[Ian Allinson]], who was allowed to leave the club for [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] on a free transfer following an administrative error.

In the early stages of the season, Colchester kept pace with the leaders as they embarked on a [[EFL Cup|League Cup]] run. Following a 1–1 draw at [[Football League Second Division|Second Division]] [[Swansea City A.F.C.|Swansea City]], chairman Maurice Cadman pledged Lea funds to purchase two players if there was an attendance greater than 5,000 in the second leg of the tie. As such, 5,204 were in attendance to see the U's beat the Swans 1–0. They were drawn at home to [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] in the third round as [[Layer Road]] saw its final-ever five figure gate when 13,031 saw the 2–0 defeat for the U's.

Colchester's form tailed off in the latter stages of the season as they ended the season 15 points adrift of the promotion places in eighth position, despite [[Tony Adcock]]'s 33 goals. Now frustrated at his board's successive attempts to fund promotion that ultimately ended in failure, Maurice Cadman announced that win bonuses would be dropped for the [[1984–85 Colchester United F.C. season|1984–85]] season replaced by an insurance-backed promotion bonus. He also announced that the club had been put up for sale at a value of £150,000.

Attendances on average were up, largely in thanks to the cup runs, but otherwise league attendances dropped off, and a new low of 1,226 witnessed Colchester's 3–0 win over [[Torquay United F.C.|Torquay United]] on 28 April 1984. Meanwhile, stalwarts [[Micky Cook (footballer, born 1951)|Micky Cook]], who had set a club record of 614 league appearances, and [[Steve Leslie (footballer, born 1952)|Steve Leslie]] both retired through injury.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cu-fc.com/news/2012/july/the-1970s-and-1980s/ |title=The 1970s and 1980s |last=Hudson |first=Matt |date=11 July 2012 |publisher=Colchester United FC |accessdate=29 April 2018 }}</ref>
