'''''Trichomycterus guaraquessaba''''' is a species of [[pencil catfish]] presently only known from [[Rio Bracinho]], a small isolated river in [[Paraná (state)|Paraná]] state, [[Brazil]].

This is an elongate catfish up to {{convert|91.5|cm|in}} [[fish measurement|standard length]]. It has no unique [[comparative anatomy|morphological]] features and can be distinguished from its congeners by a combination of characters including a uniformly grey colouring with only small dark spots on the flank, [[pelvic fin]] not covering the [[urogenital]] opening and square-ended [[caudal fin]].
