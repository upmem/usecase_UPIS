'''Rebecca Spikings-Goldsman''' (September 21, 1967 â€“ July 6, 2010), who was widely credited as '''Rebecca Spikings''', was an [[United States|American]] [[film producer]] and [[filmmaker]]. She produced or co-produced a number of films, including ''[[Deep Blue Sea (1999 film)|Deep Blue Sea]]'' in 1999 and 2004's ''[[Mindhunters]]''.<ref name=variety>{{cite news |first=|last=|title=Producer Spikings-Goldsman dies of heart attack |url=http://variety.com:80/2010/film/news/producer-spikings-goldsman-dies-of-heart-attack-1118021467|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160912030754/http://variety.com:80/2010/film/news/producer-spikings-goldsman-dies-of-heart-attack-1118021467|url-status=dead|archive-date=2016-09-12|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |publisher= |date=2010-07-07 |accessdate=2018-10-12}}</ref> Her television work included the 1996 [[HBO]] movie, ''[[Mistrial (1996 film)|Mistrial]]'', starring [[Bill Pullman]].<ref name=variety/>

Spikings began her film production career as an associate producer on the 1991 [[independent film|independent]] [[comedy film|comedy]], ''[[Pyrates]]'', starring [[Kevin Bacon]] and [[Kyra Sedgwick]].<ref name=variety/> She initially worked at [[Irving Azoff]]'s [[production company]] Giant Pictures before leaving for [[Renny Harlin]]'s
[[Midnight Sun Pictures]], where she worked for six years.<ref name=variety/>

Her producing credits at Midnight Sun Pictures included ''[[Deep Blue Sea (1999 film)|Deep Blue Sea]]'' and ''[[Mindhunters]]'', both of which were directed by Renny Harlin.<ref name=variety/> Spikings-Goldsman was also the co-executive producer for ''[[Cutthroat Island]]'' in 1995 and  ''[[Driven (2001 film)|Driven]]'' in 2001.<ref name=variety/>

Rebecca Spikings-Goldsman died of a heart attack on July 6, 2010, at the age of 42.<ref name=variety/> She was survived by her husband, producer and [[screenwriter]] [[Akiva Goldsman]], who had also written ''Deep Blue Sea''.<ref name=variety/>
