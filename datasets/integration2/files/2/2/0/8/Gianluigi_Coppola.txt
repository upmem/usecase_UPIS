'''Gianluigi Coppola''' ([[Chiavari]], 1928, 16 April 1928 - [[Genoa]], 2015, 24 August) was an Italian illustrator and cartoonist.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lospaziobianco.it/morto-storico-disegnatore-gianluigi-coppola/|title=Notizie - E' morto lo storico disegnatore Gianluigi Coppola|last=Garofoli|first=Michele|date=2015-08-26|website=Lo Spazio Bianco|language=it-IT|access-date=2019-11-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fumettologica.it/2015/08/scomparso-gianluigi-coppola-dylan-dog/|title=È scomparso Gianluigi Coppola, disegnatore di Dylan Dog e Martin Mystère|date=2015-08-26|website=Fumettologica|language=it-IT|access-date=2019-11-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lambiek.net/artists/c/coppola_gianluigi.htm|title=Gianluigi Coppola|website=lambiek.net|language=en|access-date=2019-11-28}}</ref>

== Biography ==
After completing the art high school, in 1949 he moved to Milan where he began to collaborate with ''Goal '' a comic book published by the [[Gazzetta dello Sport]]; he then collaborates with authors such as [[Ferdinando Tacconi]] in the ''Nat del Santa Cruz'' series and [[Franco Paludetti]] in the ''[[Sciuscià (comics)|Sciuscià]]'' series. He then moved to [[England]] in 1956, where for over twenty years he worked as a draftsman creating various series such as ''Billy the Kid'' published on ''The Sun'' and others such as ''Scoop Donovan'' and ''Battler Britton''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sergiobonelli.it/news/gianluigi-coppola/9278/Gianluigi-Coppola.html|title=Gianluigi Coppola|website=www.sergiobonelli.it|language=it|access-date=2019-11-28}}</ref>

In the early sixties he stopped working as a cartoonist to devote himself to the illustration of books for [[Penguin Books]], as well as making covers for novels published by various publishing houses such as Corgi, Fontana, Collins, Pan, Granada. He also dedicates himself to advertising collaborating with magazines such as Forum, Observer, Psychology Today.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sergiobonelli.it/news/gianluigi-coppola/9278/Gianluigi-Coppola.html|title=Gianluigi Coppola|website=www.sergiobonelli.it|language=it|access-date=2019-11-28}}</ref>

He returned to Italy in 1979 where he began collaborating with [[Arnoldo Mondadori]] for that he made illustrations for classic novels for boys as well as covers of the ''Oscar'' series. He also collaborates with the magazines [[Playboy]] and [[Penthouse (magazine)|Penthouse]]; returned to work on comics in 1990 realizing for the [[Sergio Bonelli Editore]] some stories of the [[Dylan Dog]] and [[Martin Mystère]] series; after this experience he returned to devote himself to illustration and painting.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sergiobonelli.it/news/gianluigi-coppola/9278/Gianluigi-Coppola.html|title=Gianluigi Coppola|website=www.sergiobonelli.it|language=it|access-date=2019-11-28}}</ref>
