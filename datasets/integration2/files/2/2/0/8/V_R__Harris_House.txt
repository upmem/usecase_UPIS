The '''V.R. Harris House''', also known as the '''Mitchum House''', is a historic house in [[Erin, Tennessee]], U.S.. It was built in 1891 for Volney Rowe Harris, a businessman who served as the mayor of Erin in 1881.<ref name="nrhpdoc">{{cite web|url={{NRHP url|id=83003040}}|title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory/Nomination: V. R. Harris House |publisher=[[National Park Service]]|author= |date= |accessdate=May 24, 2018}} With {{NRHP url|id=83003040|photos=y|title=accompanying pictures}}</ref> Harris lived here with his wife, n√©e Lizzie Garner, and their twelve children.<ref name="nrhpdoc"/> It remained in the Harris family until 1924.<ref name="nrhpdoc"/> It was purchased by the Mitchum family in 1944, and it became known as the Mitchum House as a result.<ref name="nrhpdoc"/>

The house was designed in the [[Queen Anne style architecture in the United States|Queen Anne architectural style]].<ref name="nrhpdoc"/> It has been listed on the [[National Register of Historic Places]] since August 18, 1983.<ref name="nris"/>
