'''Connell Patrick Rawlinson''' (born 22 September 1991) is a Welsh professional [[association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Defender (association football)|defender]] for [[National League (division)|National League]] club [[Notts County F.C.|Notts County]].

He began his career at [[Chester City F.C.|Chester City]], making three [[National League (division)|Conference Premier]] appearances before the club was liquidated in March 2010. He joined [[The New Saints F.C.|The New Saints]] the following season, and went on to spend the next eight years with the club, winning seven [[Welsh Premier League]] titles, four [[Welsh Cup]]s, and five [[Welsh League Cup]] titles. He was also named in the 2017–18 Welsh Premier League Team of the Season. He switched to the [[English Football League]] when he signed with Port Vale in May 2018. He was given a free transfer to Notts County in August 2019.

==Career==
===Chester City===
Rawlinson began his career with [[Chester City F.C.|Chester City]], making his debut as a substitute against [[Oxford United F.C.|Oxford United]] at the age of 17 in 2009. He made two further [[National League (division)|Conference Premier]] substitute appearances in the [[2009–10 Football Conference|2009–10]] season, which were officially chalked off after the club's records were expunged following their liquidation in March 2010.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Powell|first1=Dave|title=Former Chester City youngster finally gets shot at the Football League|url=https://www.chesterchronicle.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/former-chester-city-youngster-finally-14699333|accessdate=25 May 2018|work=Chester Chronicle|date=24 May 2018}}</ref>

===The New Saints===
Rawlinson joined [[The New Saints F.C.|The New Saints]] and made his debut in the [[Welsh Premier League]] on 29 August 2010, coming on for Phil Baker as a 61st-minute substitute in a 2–1 victory over [[Bala Town F.C.|Bala Town]] at [[Park Hall (football ground)|Park Hall]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The New Saints vs. Bala Town - 29 August 2010 - Soccerway|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2010/08/29/wales/premier-league/the-new-saints-fc/bala-town-fc/953565/|website=int.soccerway.com|accessdate=25 May 2018|language=en}}</ref> He was handed his first start on 4 September, in a 2–1 defeat at [[Llanelli A.F.C.|Llanelli]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Llanelli vs. The New Saints - 4 September 2010 - Soccerway|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2010/09/04/wales/premier-league/llanelli-afc/the-new-saints-fc/953571/|website=int.soccerway.com|accessdate=25 May 2018|language=en}}</ref> He went on to play in the final of the [[Welsh League Cup]], as TNS recorded a [[2010–11 Welsh League Cup|4–3 victory]] over Llanelli on 2 May.<ref>{{cite web|title=Llanelli vs. The New Saints - 2 May 2011 - Soccerway|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2011/05/02/wales/league-cup/llanelli-afc/the-new-saints-fc/1051080/|website=int.soccerway.com|accessdate=25 May 2018|language=en}}</ref> He appeared a total of 21 times in the [[2010–11 Welsh Premier League|2010–11]] campaign and was offered professional terms at TNS for the [[2011–12 Welsh Premier League|2011–12]] season.<ref name="Soccerway"/><ref name="rdt">{{cite news|title=Connell Rawlinson departs The New Saints|url=https://www.shropshirestar.com/sport/football/the-new-saints/2018/05/23/connell-rawlinson-departs-the-new-saints/|accessdate=25 May 2018|work=Shropshire Star|date=23 May 2018|language=en}}</ref> He won a [[Wales national semi-professional football team|Wales semi-professional]] cap against [[Estonia national semi-professional football team|Estonia]] in October 2011.<ref name="rdt"/> He scored his first senior goal on 28 January, in a 4–0 victory over [[Newport County A.F.C.|Newport County]] in the Fourth Round of the [[Welsh Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The New Saints vs. Newport County - 28 January 2012 - Soccerway|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2012/01/28/wales/welsh-cup/the-new-saints-fc/newport-county-afc/1232079/|website=int.soccerway.com|accessdate=25 May 2018|language=en}}</ref> He was sent off for the first time in his career on 18 February, during a 2–1 win at Llanelli.<ref>{{cite web|title=Llanelli vs. The New Saints - 18 February 2012 - Soccerway|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2012/02/18/wales/premier-league/llanelli-afc/the-new-saints-fc/1246626/|website=int.soccerway.com|accessdate=25 May 2018|language=en}}</ref> TNS won the Welsh Premier League at the end of the 2011–12 season.<ref name="Soccerway"/> Rawlinson was also an unused substitute as they won the [[2011–12 Welsh Cup|2012 Welsh Cup]] with a 2–0 victory over [[Cefn Druids A.F.C.|Cefn Druids]] at [[Nantporth]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Welsh Cup final: Cefn Druids 0-2 The New Saints|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/17957364|accessdate=25 May 2018|work=BBC Sport|date=5 May 2012}}</ref>

He was loaned out to [[Newtown A.F.C.|Newtown]] for a six-week spell in November 2012.<ref name="rdt"/> His arrival at [[Latham Park]] allowed manager [[Bernard McNally]] to move Andy Jones from central defence to centre-forward.<ref>{{cite news|title=How McNally uncovered a Christmas cracker|url=https://welsh-premier.com/index.php/archive-2012/9851-how-mcnally-uncovered-a-christmas-cracker|accessdate=25 May 2018|work=welsh-premier.comwelsh-premier.com|date=23 December 2012}}{{Dead link|date=July 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Rawlinson featured in five Welsh Premier League games for the "Robins", and played just six games for the "Saints" by the end of the [[2012–13 Welsh Premier League|2012–13]] season, who successfully defended their league title.<ref name="Soccerway"/> One of these appearances were in the [[2012–13 Welsh League Cup|Welsh League Cup final]], which ended in a penalty shoot-out defeat to [[Carmarthen Town A.F.C.|Carmarthen Town]] following a 3–3 draw after extra-time.<ref>{{cite web|title=Carmarthen Town vs. The New Saints - 12 January 2013 - Soccerway|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2013/01/12/wales/league-cup/carmarthen-town-afc/the-new-saints-fc/1410306/|website=int.soccerway.com|accessdate=25 May 2018|language=en}}</ref> He scored his first goal in the Welsh Premier League on 1 April 2014, in a 3–1 home win over Newtown.<ref>{{cite web|title=The New Saints vs. Newtown - 1 April 2014 - Soccerway|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2014/04/01/wales/premier-league/the-new-saints-fc/newtown-afc/1654749/|website=int.soccerway.com|accessdate=25 May 2018|language=en}}</ref> He ended the [[2013–14 Welsh Premier League|2013–14]] season with 16 appearances, helping the club to again retain their league title.<ref name="Soccerway"/> TNS also won the Welsh Cup, though Rawlinson was again an unused substitute during the [[2013–14 Welsh Cup|3−2 victory]] over [[Aberystwyth Town F.C.|Aberystwyth Town]] at the [[Racecourse Ground]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Welsh Cup Final: Aberystwyth 2-3 The New Saints|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/27266804|accessdate=25 May 2018|work=BBC Sport|date=3 May 2014}}</ref>

He made his debut in the [[UEFA Champions League]] on 15 July 2014, in a 1–0 defeat at Slovakian side [[ŠK Slovan Bratislava]], during the [[2014–15 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round|Second Round of qualification]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Slovan Bratislava vs. The New Saints - 25 January 2015 - Soccerway|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/01/25/wales/league-cup/bala-town-fc/the-new-saints-fc/1971293/|website=int.soccerway.com|accessdate=25 May 2018|language=en}}</ref> He also won his third Welsh Cup as TNS secured a [[Treble (association football)|domestic treble]] with a [[2014–15 Welsh Cup|2–0 win]] over Newtown at [[Latham Park]] on 2 May, though was substituted after ten minutes for Christian Seargeant.<ref>{{cite news|title=Welsh Cup final: The New Saints 2-0 Newtown AFC|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/32486780|accessdate=25 May 2018|work=BBC Sport|date=2 May 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=The New Saints vs. Newtown - 2 May 2015 - Soccerway|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2015/05/02/wales/welsh-cup/the-new-saints-fc/newtown-afc/2026988/|website=int.soccerway.com|accessdate=25 May 2018|language=en}}</ref> He was restricted to 19 appearances in the [[2015–16 Welsh Premier League|2015–16]] season, though TNS again won the league.<ref name="Soccerway"/> He did though feature in the [[2015–16 Welsh League Cup|final of the Welsh League Cup]], as TNS defeated [[Denbigh Town F.C.|Denbigh Town]] 2–0 at [[Maesdu Park]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The New Saints vs. Denbigh Town - 23 January 2016 - Soccerway|url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2016/01/23/wales/league-cup/the-new-saints-fc/denbigh-town-fc/2170362/|website=int.soccerway.com|accessdate=25 May 2018|language=en}}</ref> TNS also secured the Welsh Cup and another treble with a [[2015–16 Welsh Cup|2–0 victory]] over [[Airbus UK Broughton F.C.|Airbus UK Broughton]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Welsh Cup final: The New Saints 2-0 Airbus UK Broughton|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/36172706|accessdate=25 May 2018|work=BBC Sport|date=2 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Airbus UK vs. The New Saints - He re-established himself in the first-team during the [[2016–17 Welsh Premier League|2016–17]] season, scoring two goals in 40 league and cup games, as TNS clinched a sixth straight league title.<ref name="Soccerway"/> TNS won the League Cup for the fourth consecutive time with a 4–0 win over [[Barry Town United F.C.|Barry Town United]] at [[Cardiff Metropolitan University F.C.|Cyncoed Stadium]].<ref>{{cite news|title=FAW / TNS beat Barry Town United to lift first silverware of season|url=http://www.faw.cymru/en/news/tns-beat-barry-town-town-united-lift-first-silverware-season/|accessdate=25 May 2018|work=www.faw.cymru|date=22 January 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=The New Saints vs. Barry Town United - ===Notts County===
Rawlinson signed for newly-relegated [[National League (division)|National League]] club [[Notts County F.C.|Notts County]] on 5 August 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nottinghampost.com/sport/football/football-news/notts-county-bolster-defence-singing-3177358|title=Notts bolster defence by signing Port Vale centre-back|first=Tom|last=Leach|date=5 August 2019|website=nottinghampost}}</ref> He scored two goals in 40 appearances for the "Magpies" in the [[2019–20 National League|2019–20]] season, which was permanently suspended on 26 March due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in England]], with Notts County in the play-offs in third-place.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/52381612 |title=National League clubs vote to end regular season immediately |work=BBC Sport |accessdate=22 March 2020}}</ref> County reached the [[2020 National League play-off Final|play-off final]] at [[Wembley Stadium]], though Rawlinson was an unused substitute as they lost the match 3–1 to [[Harrogate Town A.F.C.|Harrogate Town]].<ref name="2020pof">{{cite news |title=National League promotion final: Harrogate Town beat Notts County 3-1 to secure place in League Two |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/53541549 |accessdate=2 August 2020 |work=BBC Sport |date=2 August 2020 |language=en-gb}}</ref>
