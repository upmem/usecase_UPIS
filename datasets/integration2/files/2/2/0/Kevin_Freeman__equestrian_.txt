'''Kevin John Freeman''' (born October 21, 1941) is an American [[equestrianism|equestrian]] who competed at three Olympic Games, winning silver medals in team eventing in 1964 and 1972.

==Early life==
Born in [[Portland, Oregon]], Freeman grew up on a farm in nearby [[Molalla, Oregon|Molalla]]. After attending [[Cornell University]], Freeman developed his equestrian skills in [[California]].<ref name=oshof>{{cite web
 |url=http://www.oregonsportshall.org/kevin_freeman.html 
 |title=Kevin Freeman – Equestrian 
 |accessdate=April 6, 2011 
 |publisher=[[Oregon Sports Hall of Fame]] 
 |url-status=dead 
 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111002171632/http://www.oregonsportshall.org/kevin_freeman.html 
 |archivedate=October  2, 2011 
}}</ref> He competed in the [[1963 Pan American Games]], earning a gold medal in team competition, and a silver as an individual.<ref name=oshof />

==Olympics==
In 1964, Freeman competed in [[eventing]] for the United States at the [[1964 Summer Olympics]] in [[Tokyo, Japan|Tokyo]] aboard Gallopade, earning a silver medal in team competition and finishing 12th individually.<ref name=oshof /> Freeman competed again on the US Team in the [[1968 Summer Olympics]] in [[Mexico City, Mexico|Mexico City]] riding Chalan. Kevin had ridden Chalan only one time before the Olympics and that was the day before the team shipped out for Mexico. In spite of a torrential rain storm on cross-country which obliterated some of the take-offs and landings, the team still won the silver medal.  <ref name="oshof" /><ref name="sr">{{cite web|url=https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/fr/kevin-freeman-1.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200418000929/https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/fr/kevin-freeman-1.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 18, 2020|title=Kevin Freeman Biography and Olympic Results|publisher=sports-reference.com|accessdate=April 6, 2011}}</ref>
