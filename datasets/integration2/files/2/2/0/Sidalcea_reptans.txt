'''''Sidalcea reptans''''' is a species of flowering plant in the [[Malvaceae|mallow family]] known by the common name '''Sierra checkerbloom''' and '''Sierra checker mallow'''.<ref name=PLANTS/><ref name=calflora>[http://www.calflora.org/cgi-bin/species_query.cgi?where-calrecnum=7583 Calflora: ''Sidalcea reptans'']</ref>

==Distribution==
The plant is [[Endemism|endemic]] to [[California]], where it can be found throughout the [[Sierra Nevada (U.S.)|Sierra Nevada]] at {{convert|1120|-|2000|m|ft}} in elevation, especially the central peaks of the range.<ref name=calflora/> It grows in moist and dry habitat types in the mountains, in meadows of [[yellow pine forest]] and [[red fir]] forest.<ref name=calflora/>

==Description==
''Sidalcea reptans'' is a [[rhizome|rhizomatous]] perennial herb reaching up to {{convert|0.5|m|ft}} tall. Lower portions of the stem sometimes root when in contact with moist substrate. It is coated in long, bristly hairs.

The leaf blades are also bristly. They vary in shape, the lower ones barely lobed and borne on long [[Petiole (botany)|petioles]], and the higher ones often deeply cut into lobes.

The [[inflorescence]] is a long, open series of flowers with pink to lavender petals up to 2 centimeters in length. The bloom period is June to August.
