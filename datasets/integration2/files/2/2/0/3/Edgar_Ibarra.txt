'''Edgar Alexander Ibarra''' (born May 31, 1989) is a Venezuelan former [[professional baseball]] [[pitcher]].

Ibarra began his professional career with the [[Minnesota Twins]]' organization. After the 2013 season, the Twins purchased Ibarra's contract, adding him to their [[40-man roster]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.startribune.com/sports/twins/230597601.html |title=Twins don't make qualifying offer to Pelfrey; sign 8 for Rochester |publisher=Star Tribune |date= |accessdate=November 22, 2013}}</ref> He signed with the Angels before the 2015 season, and was promoted to the major leagues on June 1.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dailynews.com/sports/20150601/angels-pitching-staff-back-to-12-after-calling-up-lefty-edgar-ibarra|title=Angels’ pitching staff back to 12 after calling up lefty Edgar Ibarra|work=dailynews.com|accessdate=June 3, 2015}}</ref> He made his major league debut on June 2.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ocregister.com/angels/ibarra-664104-one-angels.html|title=VIDEO: Angels' Ibarra records first big-league strikeout|work=The Orange County Register|accessdate=June 3, 2015}}</ref>

After signing a minor league contract with the [[Philadelphia Phillies]] before the 2016 season, Ibarra was released by the team at the end of Spring Training.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://m.milb.com/milb/player/500891|title=Edgar Ibarra MiLB profile|work=milb.com|accessdate=April 1, 2016}}</ref>
