[[Brigadier general]] '''Mohammad Jamali-Paqaleh''' ({{lang-fa|محمد جمالی پاقلعه}}) (1963–2013) was an [[Iran]]ian commander in the [[Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps|Revolutionary Guards]] who died in [[Syrian Civil War]].<ref name="voa" />

== Biography ==
Jamali-Paqaleh was born in 1963 in Paqaleh in [[Shahr-e Babak County]], [[Kerman Province]]. When he was two years old, his father died. He completed primary school in the village and then migrated to [[Rafsanjan]] to continue his education.<ref name="bpsb">{{cite web |url=http://bpsb.ir/?q=node/9768 |title=Biography of Sardar Mohammad Jamali |accessdate=25 April 2016}}</ref>

== Iran–Iraq War ==
Jamali-Paqaleh was a [[veteran]] of the [[Iran–Iraq War]]<ref name="voa">{{cite news |url=http://ir.voanews.com/content/iran-sepah-commander-syria-reuters/1783028.html |title=Iranian Revolutionary Guards commander was killed in Syria. |publisher=VOA |date=4 November 2013 |accessdate=25 April 2016}}</ref><ref name="PBBC">{{cite news |url=http://www.bbc.com/persian/iran/2013/11/131104_l12_sepah_syria_iran_murder |title=One of the commanders of the Iranian Revolutionary Guards were killed in Syria |publisher=BBC Persian |date=4 November 2013 |accessdate=26 April 2016}}</ref> and was a member of the Sarallah division, the same division outfitted that had trained by General [[Qassem Soleimani]].<ref name="ahram">{{cite web|author= |url=http://english.ahram.org.eg/NewsContent/2/8/85647/World/Region/Iran-Guards-commander-killed-in-Syria-Reports.aspx |title=Iran Guards commander killed in Syria: Reports |work=Al-Ahram |date=4 November 2013 |accessdate=5 November 2013}}</ref> He had participated in several operations such as [[Operation Tariq al-Qods]], [[Operation Fath ol-Mobin|Fath ol-Mobin]], [[Operation Beit ol-Moqaddas|Beit ol-Moqaddas]], [[Operation Ramadan|Ramadan]], [[Operation Dawn 8|Dawn 8]], and [[Siege of Basra|Karbala-5,4,1]].<ref name="bpsb" />

== Syrian Civil War ==
The Iranian news outlet [[Mehr News Agency]] reported that Jamali voluntary went to Syria to protect the [[Sayyidah Zaynab Mosque]] and another holy shrine against [[Bashar al-Assad|Assad]]-opposing forces in the [[Syrian Civil War]].<ref name=bbc>{{cite web|author= |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-middle-east-24818043 |title=Iran buries Guards commander 'killed in Syria' |publisher=BBC News |date=5 November 2013 |accessdate=5 November 2013}}</ref><ref name="PBBC" /><ref name="voa" />

Jamali-Paqaleh was reported to have been killed by Syrian rebels either in the final days of October or early November 2013.<ref name="voa" /> He was buried on 5 November in [[Kerman]] with full military honors.<ref name=bbc /><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.radiofarda.com/content/news/25157658.html |title=One of the Sepah's commander, were killed in Syria. |publisher=Radio Farda |date=4 November 2013 |accessdate=26 April 2016}}</ref>
