'''David Lee Wilson''' (born January 8, 1950) is an American politician and a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] member of the [[Delaware Senate]], where he has represented the [[Delaware's 18th Senate district|18th district]] since 2019.<ref name="DL">{{cite web |url= https://legis.delaware.gov/AssemblyMember/150/Wilson |title= Senator David L. Wilson (R) |publisher= [[Delaware General Assembly]] |location= [[Dover, Delaware]] |accessdate= November 5, 2013}}</ref> He was previously a member of the [[Delaware House of Representatives]] from 2008 to 2018.<ref name="PVS">{{cite web |url= http://votesmart.org/candidate/biography/109426 |title= Representative David Wilson's Biography |publisher= [[Project Vote Smart]] |accessdate= November 5, 2013}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Wilson was born on January 8, 1950, at [[Bayhealth Medical Center|Milford Memorial Hospital]] in [[Milford, Delaware]] to Leroy and Nellie Wilson. He was raised in [[Lincoln, Delaware]], where he lived and worked on his [[Family farm|family's farm]], growing [[Maize|corn]] and [[Soybean|soybeans]], as well as raising [[livestock]], including hogs, [[Dairy cattle|dairy cows]], and [[Rabbit|rabbits]]. Wilson is one of five children, including four sisters: Gladys, Etta, Margaret, and Naomi. He married his wife, Carolyn, in 1970.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Senator David L. Wilson (R) - Delaware General Assembly|url=https://legis.delaware.gov/AssemblyMember/151/Wilson|access-date=2020-11-10|website=legis.delaware.gov}}</ref> Together, they have one son, David Wilson Jr., who passed away in 1997. 

Wilson began his career at Milford Tractor Company before opening Wilson's Auction Sales, Inc. in 1971. Wilson's Auction Sales has been at its current location on [[U.S. Route 113]] in Lincoln, Delaware since 1975.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Wilson's Auctions Â» Delaware's Leading Auction Company|url=http://www.wilsonsauction.com/|access-date=2020-11-10|website=www.wilsonsauction.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Electoral history==
*In 2008, Wilson ran for the seat vacated by retiring Republican J. Benjamin Ewing, and won the general election with 5,174 votes (61%) against [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] nominee L. Aaron Chaffinch.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://elections.delaware.gov/archive/elect08/elect08_general_election/html/elect08_gen_office.shtml |title= State of Delaware General Election Official Results |date= November 4, 2008 |publisher= Delaware Department of Elections  |department= Office of the State Election Commissioner |accessdate= November 5, 2013}}</ref>
*In 2010, Wilson won the general election with 4,719 votes (70.3%) against Democratic nominee James Westhoff.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://elections.delaware.gov/archive/elect10/elect10_General/html/election.shtml |title= State of Delaware General Election Official Results |date= November 2, 2010 |publisher= Delaware Department of Elections  |department= Office of the State Election Commissioner |accessdate= November 5, 2013}}</ref>
*In 2012, Wilson won the general election with 5,836 votes (92.6%) against [[Libertarian Party of Delaware|Libertarian]] candidate Ronnie Fitzgerald.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://elections.delaware.gov/archive/elect12/elect12_general/html/election.shtml |title= State of Delaware General Election Official Results |date= November 6, 2012 |publisher= Delaware Department of Elections  |department= Office of the State Election Commissioner |accessdate= November 5, 2013}}</ref>
*In 2014, Wilson was unopposed in the general election, winning 4,155 votes.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://elections.delaware.gov/archive/elect14/elect14_General/html/election.shtml |title= State of Delaware General Election Official Results |date= November 7, 2014 |publisher= Delaware Department of Elections  |department= Office of the State Election Commissioner |accessdate= January 6, 2019}}</ref>
*In 2016, Wilson won the Republican primary with 1,252 votes (69.2%) against Robert Mitchel.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://elections.delaware.gov/archive/elect16/elect16_general/html/election.shtml |title= State of Delaware Primary Election Official Results |date= September 13, 2016 |publisher= Delaware Department of Elections  |department= Office of the State Election Commissioner |accessdate= January 6, 2019}}</ref> He went on to win the general election with 6,553 (72.9%) against Democratic nominee Gary Wolfe.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://elections.delaware.gov/archive/elect16/elect16_general/html/election.shtml |title= State of Delaware General Election Official Results |date= November 8, 2016 |publisher= Delaware Department of Elections  |department= Office of the State Election Commissioner |accessdate= January 6, 2019}}</ref>
*In 2018, Wilson ran for an open seat in the Delaware Senate, and won the general election with 10,816 (65.2%) against Democratic nominee James Purcell.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://elections.delaware.gov/archive/elect18/elect18_general/html/election.shtml |title= State of Delaware General Election Official Results |date= November 6, 2018 |publisher= Delaware Department of Elections  |department= Office of the State Election Commissioner |accessdate= January 6, 2019}}</ref>
