'''''Desmarestia''''' is a genus of [[brown algae]] found worldwide.  It is also called '''acid weed''', '''acidweed''',<ref name="Watson">{{Cite journal|last1=Watson|first1=Jane|title=Spatial and temporal variation in kelp forest composition off the NW coast of Vancouver Island, British Columbia|url=http://cedar.wwu.edu/ssec/2014ssec/Day1/106/|journal=Salish Sea Ecosystem Conference|accessdate=12 December 2017|language=English|date=30 April 2014|quote=In general, annual species such as Acid Weed (''Desmarestia'' spp,) were highly variable in abundance}}</ref><ref name="Warneke">{{cite web|last1=Warneke|first1=Alex|title=These are a few of my favorite species: ''Desmarestia''|url=http://www.deepseanews.com/2014/12/these-are-a-few-of-my-favorite-species-desmarestia/|website=Deep Sea News|publisher=Deep Sea News|accessdate=12 December 2017|language=English|date=5 December 2014|quote=Rightly named “Acid Weed,” the internal pH of ''Desmarestia'' has been estimated as low as 0.6 pH.}}</ref> '''''{{lang|fr|oseille de mer}}''''', '''sea sorrel''',<ref name="Dickinson">{{cite book|last1=Dickinson|first1=Carola I.|title=British Seaweeds|date=1963|publisher=Eyre & Spottiswoode|page=76|url=https://books.google.com/?id=yv8dMQAACAAJ&q=sea+sorrel|accessdate=13 December 2017|language=English|oclc=1437555|location=London|series=Kew series|volume=3}}</ref> '''{{lang|ja|ウルシグサ}}''' ('''{{transl|ja|urushi-gusa}}'''), '''''{{lang|de|stacheltang}}''''', '''mermaid's hair''', '''landlady's wig''', or '''''{{lang|ga|gruagach}}'''''.<ref name="SuriaLink">{{cite web|author1=<!-- not stated -->|title=Desmarestia|url=http://surialink.seaplant.net/HANDBOOK/Genera/browns/Desmarestia/Desmarestia.htm|website=SuriaLink SeaPlants Handbook|accessdate=13 December 2017|language=English}}</ref>  However, 'sea sorrel' can also specifically refer to ''[[Desmarestia viridis]]''.  Members of this genus can be either annual or perennial.<ref name="W1">{{cite web
|url=http://algaebase.org/search/genus/detail/?genus_id=56&-session=abv4:86996EF31afb128815jWx299BF9E
|title=''Desmarestia'' J.V.Lamouroux
|author=M.D. Guiry
|publisher=AlgaeBase
|accessdate=November 5, 2013
}}</ref>  Annual members of this genus store [[sulfuric acid]] in intracellular [[vacuole]]s. When exposed to air they release the acid, thereby destroying themselves and nearby seaweeds in the process. They are found in shallow [[intertidal zone]]s.<ref name="W1"/>

Ingesting sulfuric acid can cause severe digestive problems.<ref name="Turner">{{cite book|last1=Turner|first1=Nancy J.|last2=von Aderkas|first2=Patrick|title=The North American Guide to Common Poisonous Plants and Mushrooms|date=2009|publisher=Timber Press|location=Portland, OR|isbn=9780881929294|page=116|language=English|chapter=3: Poisonous Plants of Wild Areas|oclc=747112294}}</ref> but since sulfuric acid tastes extremely sour, members of the genus are unlikely to be eaten in harmful quantities.<ref name="Turner" />

The genus was named in honor of [[Anselme Gaëtan Desmarest]].<ref name="Lamouroux" />
