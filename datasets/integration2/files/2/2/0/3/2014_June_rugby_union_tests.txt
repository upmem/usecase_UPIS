The '''2014 mid-year rugby union internationals''' (also known as the '''summer internationals''' in the [[Northern Hemisphere]]) were international [[rugby union]] matches mostly played in the [[Southern Hemisphere]] during the June international window.

These matches were part of the [[International Rugby Board]] (IRB) global rugby calendar (2012–19) that includes [[Test match (rugby union)|test matches]] between touring Northern Hemisphere nations and home Southern Hemisphere nations, whilst some of the touring teams played mid-week matches against provincial or regional sides. In addition to this, the calendar gave Tier 2 nations the opportunity to host Tier 1 nations outside the [[End-of-year rugby union tests|November international window]] leading up to the [[2015 Rugby World Cup]].

All [[Six Nations Championship|Six Nations]] teams were in action, with [[England national rugby union team|England]] playing a three-test series against [[New Zealand national rugby union team|New Zealand]], whilst playing a mid-week match against the [[Crusaders (rugby union)|Crusaders]]. [[France national rugby union team|France]] played [[Australia national rugby union team|Australia]] in a three-test series, whilst [[Italy national rugby union team|Italy]] played [[Fiji national rugby union team|Fiji]], [[Samoa national rugby union team|Samoa]] and [[Japan national rugby union team|Japan]]. [[Wales national rugby union team|Wales]] played [[South Africa national rugby union team|South Africa]] in a two-test series, with an additional mid-week match against the {{Rut Eastern Province Kings}}, and [[Ireland national rugby union team|Ireland]] played [[Argentina national rugby union team|Argentina]] in a two-test series. [[Scotland national rugby union team|Scotland]], the only nation that played four tests, visited the Americas, playing the [[United States national rugby union team|United States]], [[Canada national rugby union team|Canada]] and [[Argentina national rugby union team|Argentina]], before playing South Africa outside the IRB international window. Unlike previous June windows, 2014 saw Tier 3 fixtures with [[Uruguay national rugby union team|Uruguay]] hosting Canadian side [[BC Bears]], to celebrate the 125th Anniversary of the [[British Columbia Rugby Union]]. It also acted as a preparation match for Uruguay, ahead of their [[2015 Rugby World Cup – Repechage qualification|2015 Rugby World Cup Repechage play-offs]] in August against [[Hong Kong national rugby union team|Hong Kong]].

[[Tonga national rugby union team|Tonga]] played a test match against a Pacific Barbarians side in Auckland to fill a shortfall in their international calendar leading into the World Cup.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.radionz.co.nz/international/pacific-news/242334/sport-tonga-to-play-pacific-barbarians,-accuse-irb-of-'starving'-island-teams|title=Sport: Tonga to play Pacific Barbarians, accuse IRB of 'starving' Island teams|date=22 April 2014|work=Radio New Zealand}}</ref>
