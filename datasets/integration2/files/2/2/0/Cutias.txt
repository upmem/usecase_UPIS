'''Cutias''' ([[Portuguese language|Portuguese]]: ''Município de Cutias do Araguari''), formally known as '''Cutias do Araguari''' ({{IPA-pt|kuˈtʃi.ɐs dwaɾɐgwaˈɾi|native}}), is a [[municipality]] located in the southeast of the state of [[Amapá]] in [[Brazil]]. Its population is 4,634 and its area is {{convert|2,115|km2|mi2}}. The name of the municipality comes from a rodent of the same name, known in English as the [[agouti]]. Cutias do Araguari was elevated to the level of municipality on May 1, 1992.<ref name="mun"/><ref name="gov"/>

Cutias is bordered by Pracuúba and Amapá to the north, Macapá to the south, Macapá to the east, and Ferreira Gomes to the west. It is {{Convert|120|m|ft}} from the state capitol of [[Macapá]]. Cutias is dominated by the [[Araguari River (Amapá)|Araguari River]] and its tributaries, which partially submerges the area for nearly half the year. It is very flat, with the highest point reaching only {{Convert|3|m|ft}} above sea level. Cutias has a wet, tropical climate; it averages 2,800&nbsp;mm of rain per year, and has an average humidity of 80%.<ref name="mun"/><ref name="gov"/>

Cutias do Araguari is the largest exporter of ''pirarucú'' ([[arapaima]]) in Brazil, an endangered fish noted for its boneless steaks. The common agouti, from which the municipality takes its name, is also used in local cuisine.<ref name="mun"/><ref name="gov"/>
