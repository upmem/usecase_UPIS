'''Michael D. Hayes''' is a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] member of the [[Michigan State Transportation Commission]] and former member of the [[Michigan House of Representatives]].

A native of [[Owosso, Michigan|Owosso]], Hayes graduated from [[Central Michigan University]] with both a bachelor's and master's degree. He was elected [[Midland County, Michigan|Midland County]] Clerk in 1976 and served one term before being elected to the House in 1980. Hayes was re-elected three times and was succeeded by [[Dave Camp]].<ref>1987â€“1988 Michigan Manual: [http://legislature.mi.gov/documents/1987-1988/michiganmanual/1987-MM-P0139-P0166.pdf#page=22 Michael D. Hayes]</ref>

After leaving the Legislature, Hayes joined the [[Dow Chemical Company]] and worked there in several capacities over two decades, retiring as vice president of executive relations. In late 2011, Governor [[Rick Snyder]] appointed Hayes to the [[Michigan State Transportation Commission]] and to the authority overseeing the [[Gordie Howe International Bridge]].<ref>Michigan Department of Transportation: [http://www.michigan.gov/mdot/0,4616,7-151-9623_31969_31970-270273--,00.html Commissioner Michael D. Hayes]</ref>

Hayes helped bring the [[Great Lakes Loons]] to Midland, and serves as vice president and secretary of the Michigan Baseball Foundation. He is also president and CEO of the [[Midland Center for the Arts]] and serves on several community boards.<ref>Great Lakes Loons: [http://www.milb.com/content/page.jsp?ymd=20130418&content_id=45138018&fext=.jsp&sid=t456&vkey= Michael D. Hayes]</ref>

Hayes was a delegate to the [[1988 Republican National Convention]] which nominated [[George H. W. Bush]] for [[President of the United States]].<ref>The Political Graveyard: [http://politicalgraveyard.com/bio/hayes.html#897.66.47 Hayes, Michael D.]</ref>
