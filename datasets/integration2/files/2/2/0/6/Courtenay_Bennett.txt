'''Sir Courtenay Walter Bennett''' {{post-nominals|CIE}} (11 May 1855 – 17 December 1937) was a British [[diplomat]].<ref name="times">{{cite news |title= Obituary: Sir Courtenay Bennett |work=[[The Times]] |publisher=The Times Digital Archive |date= 17 December 1937|page=18 }}</ref> 

Bennett was born in [[Plymouth]], [[Devon]], the son of John Nicholas Bennett and Emily Gribble Prance. He began his career in the [[India Office]]. He subsequently moved to [[Her Majesty's Diplomatic Service]], and held postings in Spain, Guatemala, Rio Grande do Sul and Réunion.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=26044|date=22 April 1890|page=2338}}</ref> He was made a [[Companion of the Order of the Indian Empire]] on 21 June 1900.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=11211|date=3 July 1900|page=650|city=e}}</ref> He served as the British [[List of Consuls-General of the United Kingdom in San Francisco|Consul-General in San Francisco]] between 1901 and 1907. He then served as Consul-General in New York between 1907 and his retirement in 1915.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=12556|date=18 April 1913|page=436|city=e}}</ref> He was made a [[knight bachelor]] in 1914.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=28808|date=3 March 1914|page=1728}}</ref> In retirement he lived in Devon.<ref>{{cite news |last= |first= |date=26 June 1915 |title=Consul-General at New York Resigns |url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=22&dat=19150626&id=lB8BAAAAIBAJ&pg=2319,4404673&hl=en |newspaper=The Toronto World |location=Toronto |accessdate=25 July 2016 }}</ref> 

Bennett was serving in the British diplomatic mission to the US during the first year of the [[First World War]], and was engaged in early attempts to end American neutrality under the British ambassador, [[Cecil Spring Rice]].<ref>Stephen Hartley, Licínia Simão, [https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=DbSuCwAAQBAJ&dq=The+Irish+Question+as+a+Problem+in+British+Foreign+Policy,+1914%E2%80%9318&source=gbs_navlinks_s ''The Irish Question as a Problem in British Foreign Policy, 1914–18''] (Springer, 1987), p.45 (Retrieved 25 July 2016).</ref>  

He died at his home in [[Postbridge]], [[Dartmoor]], aged 82.<ref name="times"/>
