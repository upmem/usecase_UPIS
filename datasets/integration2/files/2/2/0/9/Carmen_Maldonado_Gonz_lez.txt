'''Carmen Maldonado González''' is a [[Puerto Rican people|Puerto Rican]] politician and mayor of [[Morovis, Puerto Rico|Morovis]], [[Puerto Rico]]. Maldonado is affiliated with the [[Popular Democratic Party (Puerto Rico)|Popular Democratic Party]] (in {{lang-es|Partido Popular Democrático (PPD)}}), has been serving as mayor since 2017 and is running again for 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elnuevodia.com/opinion/columnas/lacrisisdelgobiernopnplaresuelveelela-columna-2448436/|title=La crisis del gobierno PNP la resuelve el ELA|date=20 September 2018|website=El Nuevo Dia |language=es }}</ref> As of 2019, she is the vice-president of her party.<ref name="VP" />

==Initiatives and career==
In early 2019, Maldonado pushed an initiative to promote the agriculture of Morovis, along with forty farmers from Morovis, so that the products may be marked and marketed as "Made in Morovis".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elvocero.com/actualidad/iniciativa-hecho-en-morovis-para-mercadear-producci-n-agr-cola/article_050de6a4-2cc8-11e9-89a2-fbeb188d4ed5.html|title=Iniciativa Hecho en Morovis para mercadear producción agrícola|first=|last=AP|website=El Vocero de Puerto Rico|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metro.pr/pr/noticias/2017/01/10/juramenta-primera-alcaldesa-morovis.html|title=Juramenta primera alcaldesa de Morovis|first=Metro Puerto|last=Rico|website=Metro |language=es  }}</ref>

Maldonado voted against governor [[Ricardo Rosselló|Ricardo Rosselló's]] proposal to change [[Municipalities of Puerto Rico]], (administrative divisions) to "counties" of Puerto Rico, as they are called in the United States. Puerto Rico was established by Spain and until now has the administrative divisions that closely resemble the Spanish ways. Maldonado argued that there is no equivalent in the United States to Puerto Rico's next level of administrative division, [[barrios of Puerto Rico]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elvocero.com/gobierno/alcaldesa-de-morovis-rechaza-establecer-condados/article_4fe05f48-5e34-11e9-ad6c-cfb58c904e26.html|title=Alcaldesa de Morovis rechaza establecer condados|first=|last=InterNewsService|website=El Vocero de Puerto Rico |language=es }}</ref>

As of 2019, Maldonado is the vice-president of the Popular Democratic Party and she is running for reelection for the office of mayor of Morovis, in 2020.<ref name="VP">{{cite web|url=https://www.primerahora.com/noticias/policia-tribunales/nota/vicepresidentadelppddestacalaparticipacionfemeninaenlapolitica-1330697/|title=Vicepresidenta del PPD destaca la participación femenina en la política|date=8 March 2019|website=Primera Hora |language=es }}</ref>

==Accomplishments==
[[File:Fránquez, Morovis, Puerto Rico.jpg|thumb|Sign in Fránquez barrio with works completed under Maldonado's watch]]
In December 2019, Maldonado stated she would run again for mayor of Morovis and talked of having inherited a multi-million budget deficit upon first taking office and celebrated a budget surplus in 2019. She also stated that under her jurisdiction Morovis now offered free public transport from varios barrios to the downtown area of Morovis.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.primerahora.com/noticias/gobierno-politica/nota/carmenmaldonadoradicacandidaturaparasureeleccionalaalcaldiademorovis-1381549/|title=Carmen Maldonado radica candidatura para su reelección a la alcaldía de Morovis|date=December 14, 2019|website=Primera Hora}}</ref> 

===Hurricane Maria===
[[File:Home in Morovis, Puerto Rico with blue tarp May 2019.jpg|thumb|left|Home in Morovis, Puerto Rico with blue tarp May, 2019]]
[[Hurricane Maria]] struck the island of Puerto Rico on September 20, 2017, and the electrical power of Morovis was completely destroyed, leaving many residents in the dark for more than four months, some for eight months, and some for even longer.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.americanprogress.org/issues/green/reports/2018/07/11/453481/ground-puerto-rico/|title=On the Ground in Puerto Rico|first1=Enrique|last1=Fernández-Toledo|first2=Erin|last2=Cohan|first3=Winnie|last3=Stachelberg|first4=Angela|last4=Hanks|first5=Laura|last5=Jimenez|first6=Connor|last6=Maxwell|first7=Cathleen|last7=Kelly|first8=Michael|last8=Madowitz|first9=Kristina|last9=Costa|first10=Brent|last10=Woolfork|website=Center for American Progress}}</ref> Stories of how people in the barrios were surviving without electrical power, and some without access to clean water were featured on the local and U.S. national news regularly.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.primerahora.com/fotogalerias/noticias/puerto-rico/loquemariasellevoenmorovis-1251915/|title=Lo que María se llevó en Morovis|website=Primera Hora|language=Spanish}}</ref> The community in [[San Lorenzo, Morovis, Puerto Rico|San Lorenzo]], a barrio of Morovis with about 1000 people, was isolated when the bridge into the barrio was washed away by the [[Morovis River]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metro.pr/pr/noticias/2018/08/07/municipio-anuncia-reparacion-puente-afectado-huracan.html|title=Municipio anuncia reparación de puente afectado por huracán|first=Lyanne Meléndez|last=García|website=Metro |language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://cnnespanol.cnn.com/video/cnnee-vo-rec-ramos-una-cuerda-entre-la-vida-y-la-muerte-en-puerto-rico/|title=Con cuerda cruzan río peligroso en Puerto Rico|date=28 September 2017|publisher=CNN|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/videos/world/2017/09/28/bridge-collapse-morovis-puerto-rico-watson-lok-cnni.cnn|title=Bridge collapse strands Puerto Ricans - CNN Video|publisher=|via=edition.cnn.com}}</ref>
Maldonado delivered food to residents, the municipal town hall became a food distribution center and she arranged for the purchase and delivery of a water truck to provide clean water to the residents who did not have access to clean water.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.primerahora.com/fotogalerias/noticias/puerto-rico/loquemariasellevoenmorovis-1251915/|title=Lo que María se llevó en Morovis|website=Primera Hora|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2017/11/09/563028732/frustration-mounts-over-puerto-ricos-new-normal-as-federal-troops-leave-the-isla|title=Frustration Mounts Over Puerto Rico's 'New Normal' As Federal Troops Leave The Island|website=NPR.org}}</ref>

Maldonado spoke to New York governor [[Andrew Cuomo]], requesting his help and Cuomo responded by contacting H.e.a.r.t. 9/11, a non-profit organization. In early 2019, Maldonado received and thanked volunteers from H.e.a.r.t. 9/11, mostly carpenters who came to Morovis to repair and rebuild homes damaged by the hurricane.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.primerahora.com/noticias/puerto-rico/nota/llegandesdenuevayorkparareconstruircasasenmorovis-1319499/|title=Llegan desde Nueva York para reconstruir casas en Morovis|date=2 January 2019|website=Primera Hora|language=es}}</ref> In late 2019, H.e.a.r.t. 9/11 was still rebuilding roofs in Morovis, Puerto Rico.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/plugins/post.php?href=https://www.facebook.com/HEART911/posts/10157762169484723&width=500|title=Facebook|website=www.facebook.com}}</ref><ref>https://heart911.org/projects/hurricane-maria-puerto-rico-response-ongoing/</ref>
