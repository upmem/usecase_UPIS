'''Jhapandanga railway station''' is a [[Kolkata Suburban Railway]] station on the [[Howrah-Bardhaman chord]] line operated by [[Eastern Railway zone]] of [[Indian Railways]]. It is situated at Panch Shimul, Jhapandanga in [[Purba Bardhaman district]] in the [[India]]n [[States and territories of India|state]] of [[West Bengal]]. This railway station serves in Abujhati Gram Panchayet and Jhapandanga village area.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://indiarailinfo.com/station/map/jhapandanga-jpq/9570|title=JPQ/Jhapandanga|last=|first=|date=|website=|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=May 13, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ndtv.com/indian-railway/jhapandanga-jpq-station|title=JHAPANDANGA (JPQ) Railway Station|last=|first=|date=|website=ndtv.com|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=May 13, 2019}}</ref>

==History==
The Howrah–Bardhaman chord, the 95 kilometers railway line was constructed in 1917. It was connected with [[Sealdah railway station|Sealdah]] through [[Dankuni Junction railway station|Dankuni]] after construction of [[Vivekananda Setu]] in 1932.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://er.indianrailways.gov.in/print_section.jsp?lang=0&id=0,6,441,737|title=HISTORICAL PERSPECTIVE - THE FIRST JOURNEY|last=|first=|date=|website=|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=May 13, 2019}}</ref> [[Howrah Junction railway station|Howrah]] to [[Barddhaman Junction railway station|Bardhaman]] chord line including Jhapandanga railway station was electrified in 1964–66.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.irfca.org/docs/electrification-history.html|title=History of Electrification|last=|first=|date=|website=|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=May 13, 2019}}</ref>
