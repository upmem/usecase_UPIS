'''Raphael Ohin''' (born 25 May 1995) is a Ghanaian professional [[footballer]] who plays for Canadian club [[Valour FC]] as a [[midfielder]].

==Club career==
===Early career===
Before moving to Canada, Ohin played football in Ghana from 2008 to 2010 before being recruited by a club in Switzerland, where he played from 2010 to 2011.<ref name="VFC">{{cite web |title=Valour FC sign midfielder Raphael Ohin and defender Raphaël Garcia |url=https://valourfc.canpl.ca/article/valour-fc-sign-midfielder-raphael-ohin-and-defender-raphael-garcia |publisher=Valour FC |accessdate=14 May 2019 |date=10 January 2019}}</ref> He later played in Germany, Thailand and Sweden.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Eva Wasney |title=Ohin signs with Valour |url=https://www.winnipegfreepress.com/our-communities/sports/Ohin-signs-with-Valour-504877231.html |publisher=Winnipeg Free Press |accessdate=14 May 2019 |date=25 January 2019}}</ref>

===WSA Winnipeg===
In 2016, Ohin began playing with [[USL League Two|USL Premier Development League]] side [[WSA Winnipeg]] and made nine appearances that season, scoring one goal.<ref>{{cite web |title=Raphael Ohin 2016 |url=https://www.uslleaguetwo.com/roster_players/12628799?subseason=278141 |publisher=USL League Two |accessdate=14 May 2019}}</ref> In 2017 he made eight appearances and added four goals, and made another eight appearances in 2018.<ref>{{cite web |title=Raphael Ohin 2017 |url=https://www.uslleaguetwo.com/roster_players/18260276?subseason=376352 |publisher=USL League Two |accessdate=14 May 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Raphael Ohin 2018 |url=https://www.uslleaguetwo.com/roster_players/25292890 |publisher=USL League Two |accessdate=14 May 2019}}</ref>

===Valour FC===
On 10 January 2019, Ohin signed with [[Canadian Premier League]] side [[Valour FC]].<ref name="VFC" /> He made his debut on 11 May 2019 as a substitute in a 1–0 win over [[HFX Wanderers FC]].<ref name="SW">{{cite web |title=Raphael Ohin |url=https://int.soccerway.com/players/raphael-ohin/605833/ |publisher=Soccerway |accessdate=14 May 2019}}</ref>
