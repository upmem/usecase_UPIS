'''Thomas Lynch''' (born 15 September 1990) is a professional [[Australian rules football]] player who plays for the [[Adelaide Football Club]] in the [[Australian Football League]] (AFL). He was recruited by {{AFL StK}} from [[Sandringham Dragons]] Under 18 [[TAC Cup]] team with pick 13 in the [[2008 AFL draft|2008 National Draft]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.footywire.com/afl/footy/ft_drafts?year=2008&t=N&s=P|title=AFL National Draft held at the end of Season 2008|publisher=FootyWire.com}}</ref> Lynch was then traded to Adelaide following the 2011 season.<ref name="trade" />

==AFL career==

===St Kilda (2009–2011)===
St Kilda's first pick in the 2008 draft, Lynch did not play senior football in 2009.<ref>{{cite news|last=Blake|first=Martin|date=30 September 2009|title=St Kilda looks to grow from within|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/rfnews/st-kilda-looks-to-grow-from-within/2009/09/29/1253989912725.html?page=2|newspaper=The Age}}</ref> He made his AFL debut in round 18 of the 2010 season against {{AFL Ess}}.<ref>{{cite news|last=Williams|first=Rebecca|date=30 July 2010|title=Jimmy Bartel out as Cats spring surprises|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/sport/afl/jimmy-bartel-out-as-cats-spring-surprises/story-e6frf9jf-1225898744354|newspaper=Herald Sun}}</ref> He had five disposals and was dropped for the following week's match.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.foxsports.com.au/afl/collingwood-and-geelong-strengthen-teams-ahead-of-clash/story-e6frf3e3-1225923500841?from=public_rss|title=Collingwood and Geelong strengthen teams ahead of clash|date=5 August 2010|website=foxsports.com.au|publisher=Fox Sports|access-date=12 January 2011}}</ref>

Lynch played five games in 2011 and kicked four goals, including two in a game against {{AFL NM}}.<ref name="afc profile">{{cite web|url=http://www.afc.com.au/player-profile/Tom-T-Lynch|title=Tom Lynch – AFC.com.au|website=AFC.com.au|publisher=Adelaide Football Club}}</ref> At the end of the season, Lynch was traded from St Kilda to Adelaide as part of a three-way deal with {{AFL Ric}}, who received ruckman [[Ivan Maric]] from Adelaide whilst trading pick 37 to St Kilda.<ref name="trade">{{cite news|date=14 October 2011|title=Trade week updates – day five|url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/news/trade-week-rolls-on/story-e6frg6n6-1226166417480|newspaper=Herald Sun}}</ref>

===Adelaide (2012–)===
Lynch played his first game for the Crows in the first round of the 2012 season.<ref>{{cite news|last=Rucci|first=Michelangelo|date=30 March 2012|title=Adelaide Crows AFL trading pays off with Tom Lynch|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/sport/afl/adelaide-crows-afl-trading-pays-off-with-tom-lynch/story-e6frf9ko-1226313925802|newspaper=The Advertiser}}</ref> He went on to play six games in his first season at the Crows, but became a regular member of the team the next year, playing 17 matches and leading Adelaide's goalkicking with 33 goals.<ref name="afc profile" /> This included a 10-goal game against {{AFL GWS}}, making Lynch only the third player to score ten or more goals in a game for Adelaide.<ref>{{cite news|date=12 May 2013|title=Dismal crowd sees Crows humiliate GWS Giants by 135 points|url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/sport/afl/dismal-crowd-sees-crows-humiliate-gws-giants-by-135-points/story-fnca0u4y-1226640563395|newspaper=The Australian}}</ref> In 2014, however, Lynch suffered a wretched run of injuries, including a dislocated shoulder in a pre-season trial game,<ref>{{cite news|last=Capel|first=Andrew|date=7 March 2014|title=Crows forward Tom Lynch injures shoulder against GWS in trial match and could miss first half of season|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/sport/afl/crows-forward-tom-lynch-injures-shoulder-against-gws-in-trial-match-and-could-miss-first-half-of-season/story-fni5f4l8-1226848144141|newspaper=The Advertiser}}</ref> a broken jaw in a collision with {{AFL Mel}}'s [[Jack Viney]] in round 7,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.afl.com.au/news/2014-05-03/brenton-sanderson-postmatch|title=Broken jaw set to sideline Crow Tom Lynch for six weeks|last1=Thring|first1=Harry|date=3 May 2014|website=AFL.com.au|publisher=Australian Football League|access-date=22 June 2016}}</ref> and, after a late-season return, a season-ending fractured neck in a collision against the {{AFL Bri}} in round 20.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2014-08-15/tom-lynch-fractured-neck/5675230|title=Adelaide Crows' Tom Lynch has neck fracture, expected to make full recovery|date=15 August 2014|website=ABC.net.au|publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation|access-date=22 June 2016}}</ref> In total, he played eight games, and only one where he was available and uninjured for the whole game: a 25-disposal, two-goal effort against the {{AFL WB}} in round 6.<ref name="afc profile" />

Lynch established himself in Adelaide's forward line in 2015, playing all 23 games, kicking 32 goals and having 30 goal assists, the most for the season by any player.<ref name="afc profile" /> Late in the season he signed a new three-year contract with the Crows.<ref>{{cite news|last=Walsh|first=Scott|date=1 September 2015|title=Tom Lynch signs three-year contract extension with Adelaide Crows|url=http://www.adelaidenow.com.au/sport/afl/teams/adelaide/tom-lynch-signs-threeyear-contract-extension-with-adelaide-crows/news-story/31b463a0ffb3bd874b5f147e89e950f8|newspaper=The Advertiser}}</ref>

In Round 7 of 2017, he was tackled to the ground heavily by {{AFL Nor}} player [[Jarrad Waite]] in the second quarter, getting concussed and not being able to return to the ground for the rest of the match.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.afl.com.au/match-centre/2017/7/nmfc-v-adel|title=Match report: Rampaging Roos thump League leaders|last=Drummond|first=Andrew|website=AFL.com.au|publisher=Australian Football League|date=6 May 2017|access-date=5 September 2017}}</ref> Later in the season Lynch was hospitalized with viral meningitis, but he bounced back from it in Round 17 against {{AFL Mel}}, managing 27 possessions, 11 marks and three goals for the match.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.afl.com.au/news/2017-07-16/crows-ready-for-test-against-cats-says-pyke|title=Crows ready for test against Cats, says Pyke|last=McGowan|first=Marc|website=AFL.com.au|publisher=Australian Football League|date=16 July 2017|access-date=5 September 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Lynch is an ambassador for the [[RSPCA]] [[greyhound]] adoption plan. He has 11 ex-racing greyhounds.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rspca.org.au/blog-tags/greyhound-adoption|title=RSPCA: Adopt a Greyhound|website=RSPCA.com.au|publisher=RSPCA|date=24 May 2017|access-date=30 April 2018}}</ref>
