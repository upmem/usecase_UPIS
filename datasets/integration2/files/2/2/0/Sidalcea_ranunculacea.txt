'''''Sidalcea ranunculacea''''' is a species of flowering plant in the [[Malvaceae|mallow family]] known by the common name '''marsh checkerbloom''' and '''marsh checker mallow'''.<ref name=PLANTS/><ref>[http://www.calflora.org/cgi-bin/species_query.cgi?where-calrecnum=7582 Calflora: ''Sidalcea ranunculacea'']</ref>

==Description==
The plant is [[Endemism|endemic]] to [[California]], known only within [[Tulare County, California|Tulare County]] and [[Kern County, California|Kern County]]. It grows in the Southern [[Sierra Nevada (U.S.)|Sierra Nevada]] and [[Greenhorn Mountains]] at {{convert|1820|-|3050|m|ft}} in elevation.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.calflora.org/cgi-bin/county_taxon.cgi?where-calrecnum=7582 |title=Calflora distribution map: ''Sidalcea ranunculacea'' |access-date=2016-03-09 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160309132249/http://www.calflora.org/cgi-bin/county_taxon.cgi?where-calrecnum=7582 |archive-date=2016-03-09 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Many populations are within the [[Sequoia National Forest]], [[Sequoia National Park]], or [[Kings Canyon National Park]].

It grows in moist areas, such as wet meadows and on stream banks, in [[yellow pine forest]], [[red fir]] forest, [[Lodgepole pine|lodgepole]] forest habitats.

==Description==
''Sidalcea ranunculacea'' is a [[rhizome|rhizomatous]] perennial herb reaching up to {{convert|0.5|m|ft}} tall. It is coated in hairs, the lower ones becoming bristly. The fleshy lobed leaf blades also have hairs and bristles.

The [[inflorescence]] is a dense, spikelike cluster or series of clusters of flowers. Each flower has five pink to purple petals up to 1.5 centimeters long. The blooming period is June to August.
