'''''Callophrys sheridanii''''', the '''Sheridan's hairstreak''' and '''Sheridan's green hairstreak''', is a [[butterfly]] in the family [[Lycaenidae]]. It is found in North America along the south coast of [[British Columbia]] and parts of  [[Nevada]], [[Arizona]], [[Saskatchewan]], [[North Dakota]], and [[New Mexico]]. In 2009, this species was adopted as the [[List of U.S. state butterflies|U.S. state butterfly]] for [[Wyoming]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.netstate.com/states/symb/butterflies/wy_sheridans_green_hairstreak.htm |title=Wyoming State Butterfly, Sheridan's Green Hairstreak, (Callophrys sheridani) from |publisher=Netstate.com |date= |access-date=2010-08-02}}</ref>

==Description==
This small, tail-less<ref name="enature1">{{Cite web |url=http://www.enature.com/fieldguides/detail.asp?shapeID=978&curGroupID=2&lgfromWhere=&curPageNum=11 |title=FieldGuides: Species Detail |publisher=eNature |access-date=2010-08-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101203145339/http://enature.com/fieldguides/detail.asp?shapeID=978&curGroupID=2&lgfromWhere=&curPageNum=11# |archive-date=2010-12-03 |url-status=dead }}</ref> butterfly has a wingspan of {{convert|20|-|24|mm|1}}.<ref name="discoverlifecl"/> The upperside is dark gray brown, with underside color ranging from a bright green to dark gray green. The lower part of the forewing is gray. ''Callophrys sheridanii'' has a white postmedian line of dots that may be straight or bulged out, and even reduced or absent. It is named for its characteristic straight, white line crossing the underside of both wings,<ref name="enature1"/> although this may be less distinct in specimens from southern British Columbia.<ref name="discoverlifecl"/><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.nearctica.com/butter/plate8/Csherid.htm |title=Butterflies and Skippers of North America - ''Callophrys sheridani'' |publisher=Nearctica.com |access-date=2010-08-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091003163711/http://www.nearctica.com/butter/plate8/Csherid.htm# |archive-date=2009-10-03 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Distribution==
Although uncommon in Canada,<ref name="discoverlifecl"/> this species is found from southern [[Alberta]] east to [[British Columbia]].<ref name="enature1"/><ref name="butterfliesandmoths">{{Cite web |url=http://www.butterfliesandmoths.org/species?l=1522 |title=Species Detail &#124; Butterflies and Moths of North America |publisher=Butterfliesandmoths.org |access-date=2010-08-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071117223256/http://www.butterfliesandmoths.org/species?l=1522# |archive-date=2007-11-17 |url-status=dead }}</ref> From British Columbia, the range extends south through [[Eastern Washington|Eastern]] [[Washington (state)|Washington]] down to the [[Sierra Nevada (U.S.)|Sierra Nevada]] of California. From Alberta, the species extends southward through the [[Rocky Mountains]] down to [[New Mexico]].<ref name="discoverlifecl"/><ref name="enature1"/><ref name="butterfliesandmoths"/>

==Range and habitat==
''Callophrys sheridanii'' remains within a range of approximately {{convert|6000|-|10000|ft}}, living in such environments as sagebrush scrub, dry, brushy hillsides in badlands, chaparral, woodlands, subalpine scree, open hillsides, and on canyon slopes and washes.<ref name="discoverlifecl"/><ref name="enature1"/><ref name="butterfliesandmoths"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.npwrc.usgs.gov/resource/insects/bflynd/nd/76.htm |title=Atlas of ND Butterflies - Callophrys sheridanii |publisher=Npwrc.usgs.gov |date=2006-08-16 |access-date=2010-08-02}}</ref><ref>Ross A. Layberry, Peter W. Hall, and J. Donald Lafontaine (1998), ''The Butterflies of Canada'', [[University of Toronto Press]]</ref>

==Life history==
Females have one brood between April and May.<ref name="enature1"/> They lay eggs singly on various species of wild [[buckwheat]] leaves. The eggs are flattened and hemispherical in shape, and very pale green in color. The [[larva]]e range from a green to pink color and feed on buckwheat.<ref name="enature1"/>
The [[caterpillars]] are a light green to pink color, and are covered with bunches of short stiff pines. They have two rows of white spots running down the back. They eat the leaves, but some prefer to eat the flowers and young fruits. Chrysalids then hibernate.<ref name="discoverlifecl"/><ref name="butterfliesandmoths"/> In Washington, the caterpillars feed on sulphur umbrella plants (''[[Eriogonum umbellatum]]'').<ref name="discoverlifecl"/> In adulthood, males perch looking for female mates in depressions or gulch bottoms. The adults drink flower [[nectar]].<ref name="butterfliesandmoths"/> They take flight between March and June,<ref name="discoverlifecl"/> with specimens from British Columbia flying from mid-April to late May, while specimens in Alberta flying mid-May until late June.<ref>http://www.discoverlife.org/mp/20q?search=Callophrys+sheridanii&guide=Butterflies&cl=US</ref>

==Host plants==
''Callophrys sheridanii'' larvae and caterpillars feed on various species of wild [[buckwheat]], including ''[[Eriogonum racemosum]]''<ref name="discoverlifecl"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://floralore.com/fauna_EDCButterflies.html |title=Butterflies listed for El Dorado County |publisher=floralore |access-date=2010-08-02 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100126100444/http://floralore.com/fauna_EDCButterflies.html |archivedate=January 26, 2010 }}</ref> and ''[[Eriogonum heracleoides]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.wildutah.us/html/butterflies_moths/lycaenidae/h_b_callophrys_sheridani_neoperplexa.html |title=Utah Butterfly photos, Sheridan's Green Hairstreak, male - Lycaenidae ''Callophrys sheridani neoperplexa'', April 13, 2006 - Taylor's Canyon, Davis County, Utah |publisher=Wildutah.us |date=2006-04-13 |access-date=2010-08-02}}</ref>

==Similar species==
All green hairstreaks appear very similar.<ref name="enature1"/>

==Conservation status==
Although quite rare in certain parts of its range, conservation is usually not required.<ref name="butterfliesandmoths"/>
