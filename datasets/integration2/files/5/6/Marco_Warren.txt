'''Marco Warren''' (born 13 December 1993) is a Bermudian [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[PHC Zebras]] in the [[Bermudian Premier Division]]. He made his debut for the [[Bermuda national football team|Bermuda national team]] in 2015.

==Career==

Marco Warren was born in [[Warwick Parish]], [[Bermuda]]. He started his career at [[Flagler College]], where he originally studied Graphic Design as well as receiving a sports scholarship. He began to play for the college team in 2012, known as the [[Flagler Saints]], who were then playing in the [[NCAA Division II]]. In his first season, he played in all of the 19 available games and scored 1 goal. He also appeared in all the available games for the 2013 season, this time getting 7 goals in 19 games. For his final college season in 2014 he played 10 games and scored 1 goal. In total in 3 years for [[Flagler Saints]] he played in 48 games, scoring 9 goals.<ref>http://www.national-football-teams.com/player/55210/Marco_Warren.html</ref> After leaving college at the end of 2014, he joined [[Bermudian Premier Division]] side [[PHC Zebras]].

==International career==

In 2012 Marco Warren played 3 games for the [[Bermuda national football team|Bermuda U-20s]] team in the 2012 [[CONCACAF Gold Cup]], his debut coming in a 0–2 loss against [[Barbados national football team|Barbados U-20]]<ref>https://int.soccerway.com/players/marco-warren/254158/</ref>

Marco Warren made his debut for the [[Bermuda national football team]] on 8 March 2015 in a 3–0 victory over [[Grenada national football team|Grenada]]. He got an assist and was only stopped from scoring on his debut by a last ditch tackle from a [[Grenada national football team|Grenada]] defender.<ref>http://mobile.royalgazette.com/article/20150309/SPORT01/150309709&template=mobileart</ref> He has gone on to receive nine official caps for [[Bermuda national football team|Bermuda]].

==Trivia==

He is the son of former [[Bermuda]] National Footballer and [[Boulevard Blazers]] [[midfielder]] [[Dwight Warren]], has two sisters, and he lists [[Ronaldinho]] as his favorite footballer.<ref>http://athletics.flagler.edu/roster.aspx?rp_id=1671</ref>
