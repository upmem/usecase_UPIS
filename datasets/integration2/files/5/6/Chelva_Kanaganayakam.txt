Professor '''Chelvanayakam Kanaganayakam''' ({{lang-ta|செல்வநாயகம் கனகநாயகம்}}; May 7, 1952 &ndash; November 22, 2014) was a [[Tamil Canadian]] translator, author and academic.

==Early life and family==
Kanaganayakam was born on May 7, 1952 in [[Colombo]], [[Dominion of Ceylon|Ceylon]].<ref name="GM122614">{{cite news|last1=McKean|first1=Matthew|title=Chelva Kanaganayakam: A 'shining beacon' for Tamil literature|url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/news/toronto/chelva-kanaganayakam-a-shining-beacon-for-tamil-literature/article22215711/|work=[[The Globe and Mail]]|date=December 26, 2014}}</ref><ref name="TS112814">{{cite news|last1=Bhandari|first1=Aparita|title=U of T prof was admired for compassion, academic contributions|url=https://www.thestar.com/news/gta/2014/11/28/u_of_t_prof_was_admired_for_compassion_academic_contributions.html|work=[[Toronto Star]]|date=November 28, 2014}}</ref> He was the son of V. Chelvanayakam, head of the Department of Tamil at the [[University of Ceylon, Peradeniya]], and Kamalambikai.<ref name="GM122614"/><ref name="TS112814"/><ref name="TN112314">{{cite news|title=Professor Chelva Kanaganayakam passes away|url=https://www.tamilnet.com/art.html?catid=13&artid=37499|work=[[TamilNet]]|date=November 23, 2014}}</ref><ref name="Memoriam0">{{cite web|title=In Memoriam: Professor Chelvanayakam Kanaganayakam (1952 - 2014)|url=http://noolaham.net/project/153/15254/15254.pdf}}</ref> He was educated at [[Trinity College, Kandy]].<ref>{{cite web|title=In Memoriam: Professor Chelvanayakam Kanaganayakam (1952 - 2014)|url=http://noolaham.net/project/153/15254/15254.pdf|page=36}}</ref> After school Kanaganayakam joined the [[University of Sri Lanka Peradeniya campus]] but following [[Osmund Jayaratne]]'s "re-organisation" of universities, he and other  language and literature students were moved to the university's campus in [[Kelaniya]].<ref name="GM122614"/><ref name="TI012115">{{cite news|last1=Halpé|first1=Ashley|title=A memorial tribute to Chelva Kanaganayakam|url=http://www.island.lk/index.php?page_cat=article-details&page=article-details&code_title=118001|work=[[The Island (Sri Lanka)]]|date=January 21, 2015}}</ref> He graduated in 1976 with a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] degree in  English language and literature.<ref name="GM122614"/><ref name="TS112814"/><ref name="TN112314"/><ref name="UoT">{{cite web|title=In Memoriam: Professor Chelva Kanaganayakam|url=http://www.english.utoronto.ca/newsevents/news/In_Memoriam__Professor_Chelva_Kanaganayakam.htm|publisher=[[University of Toronto]]}}</ref>

Kanaganayakam was married to Thirumagal.<ref name="GM122614"/><ref name="TS112814"/><ref name="Memoriam0"/> They had a daughter (Shankary) and a son (Jegan).<ref name="GM122614"/><ref name="TS112814"/><ref name="Memoriam0"/>

==Career==
Kanaganayakam was a lecturer in the Department of English at the [[University of Jaffna]] before joining the [[University of British Columbia]] on a [[Commonwealth of Nations|Commonwealth]] scholarship, receiving a [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] degree in 1985 after producing a [[thesis]], supervised by [[W. H. New]], on the writings of [[Zulfikar Ghose]].<ref name="GM122614"/><ref name="TS112814"/><ref name="TN112314"/><ref name="UoT"/> Kanaganayakam joined the [[University of Toronto]]'s Department of English in 1989 to research and teach Commonwealth literature.<ref name="GM122614"/><ref name="TS112814"/><ref name="UoT"/> Appointed a professor in 2002, he went on to become director of the Centre for South Asian Studies at the [[Munk School of Global Affairs]] and co-ordinator of the independent studies program at [[Trinity College, Toronto]].<ref name="GM122614"/><ref name="TS112814"/><ref name="TN112314"/><ref name="UoT"/>

Kanaganayakam was a founding member of the [[Tamil Literary Garden]] and the Toronto Tamil Studies Conference.<ref name="GM122614"/><ref name="TS112814"/><ref name="TN112314"/> On the morning of November 22, 2014 Kanaganayakam was inducted to the [[Royal Society of Canada]] as a fellow in [[Quebec City]].<ref name="GM122614"/><ref name="TS112814"/><ref name="TN112314"/><ref name="UoT"/> That evening, as he went to a celebratory dinner in [[Montreal]], he suffered a heart attack and passed away.<ref name="GM122614"/><ref name="TS112814"/><ref name="TN112314"/><ref name="UoT"/>
