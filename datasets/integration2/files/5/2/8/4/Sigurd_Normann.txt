'''Sigurd Normann''' (9 April 1874 – 14 July 1950) was a Norwegian newspaper editor and politician for the [[Conservative Party (Norway)|Conservative Party]].

He was born in [[Nanset]], but after spending the years 1891 to 1895 in Germany and northern England, he settled in [[Holmestrand]] where he owned and edited the newspaper ''[[Jarlsberg (newspaper)|Jarlsberg]]'' from 1904 to 1933.<ref name=nsd>{{cite web|url=http://www.nsd.uib.no/polsys/index.cfm?urlname=storting&lan=&MenuItem=N1_1&ChildItem=&State=collapse&UttakNr=33&person=12208|title=Sigurd Normann|publisher=Norwegian Social Science Data Services (NSD)|language=Norwegian|accessdate=16 July 2015}}</ref>

He was a member of Holmestrand [[municipal council (Norway)|city council]] from 1910 to 1919 and 1925 to 1937, serving as mayor from 1916 to 1919 and 1927 to 1931. He was elected as a deputy representative to the [[Parliament of Norway]] in [[1930 Norwegian parliamentary election|1930]] from the constituency [[Market towns of Vestfold county]].<ref name=nsd/>

From 1933 he reverted to just writing in ''Jarlsberg'', and was also managing director and chairman of the board of Holmestrand Samlag.<ref name=nsd/> He died in 1950.<ref>"Dødsfall" (in Norwegian), ''Aftenposten'' 14 July 1950 p. 8</ref><ref>Death announcement, ''Aftenposten'' 15 July 1950 p. 14</ref>
