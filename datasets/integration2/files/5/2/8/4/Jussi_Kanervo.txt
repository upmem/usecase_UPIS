'''Jussi Aleksi Samuli Kanervo''' (born 1 February 1993) is a Finnish [[hurdling|hurdler]]. He won silver in the [[400 m hurdles]] at the [[2015 European Athletics U23 Championships|2015 European U23 Championships]] and bronze in the [[110 m hurdles]] at the [[2010 Youth Olympic Games]].

==Biography==
Kanervo was born in [[Tornio]] in northern Finland and set national age group records from an early age, breaking the national under-11 record for boys' 150 metres in 2003 and the national indoor under-12 record for [[60 metres]] the following year.<ref name="tp"/><ref name="yukl">{{cite magazine |title=Historiantekijä Kanervo aitoi etelään |author=Sonninen, Antti-Pekka |magazine=Yleisurheilun Kuvalehti |volume=8 |year=2010 |language=Finnish}}</ref> He competed in both the 110 m hurdles and the 400 m hurdles at the [[2009 World Youth Championships in Athletics|2009 World Youth Championships]] in [[Brixen]], Italy, but was eliminated in the early rounds in both events.<ref name="tp"/> In 2010 Kanervo's family relocated to [[Helsinki]], and he joined the training group of national hurdles coach Anatoly Mikhailov<!-- the younger; the son of the Anatoly Mikhailov with a Wikipedia article -->.<ref name="yukl"/> Kanervo represented Finland at the inaugural [[Youth Olympic Games]] in [[Singapore]] in August 2010, winning bronze in boys' 110 m hurdles (91.4&nbsp;cm) in 13.53 and breaking his own national youth record; he was the first Finn to win a Youth Olympic medal.<ref name="yukl"/>

Kanervo placed fifth in the 110 m hurdles at the [[2011 European Athletics Junior Championships|2011 European Junior Championships]].<ref name="tp"/> He won his first senior-level medals at the Finnish championships in 2012, winning bronze indoors in the [[60 m hurdles]] and gold outdoors in the 110 m hurdles.<ref name="tp"/> At the [[2012 World Junior Championships in Athletics – Men's 110 metres hurdles|2012 World Junior Championships]] in [[Barcelona]] he again competed in the 110 m hurdles, setting a national junior record (13.61/99&nbsp;cm) in the semi-finals; in the final he ran 13.62 and placed fifth.<ref name="tp"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://vanhat.yleisurheilu.fi/uutiset/uutispalvelu/arttu-kangas-mm-finaalin-nelj%C3%A4s-jussi-kanervo-aitoi-sen-keskiviikkoilta?page=8 |title=Arttu Kangas MM-finaalin neljäs, Jussi Kanervo aitoi SE:n |date=11 July 2012 |accessdate=16 July 2015 |author=Nieminen, Mikko |language=Finnish |publisher=Yleisurheilu.fi |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150720181735/http://vanhat.yleisurheilu.fi/uutiset/uutispalvelu/arttu-kangas-mm-finaalin-nelj%C3%A4s-jussi-kanervo-aitoi-sen-keskiviikkoilta?page=8 |archivedate=20 July 2015 }}</ref>

Kanervo repeated as Finnish champion in the 110 m hurdles in 2013; he represented Finland in that event at the [[2013 European Athletics U23 Championships|European U23 Championships]] in [[Tampere]], but was eliminated in the heats.<ref name="tp"/> After the 2013 season Kanervo became a student at [[South Carolina University]], coached by [[Curtis Frye]].<ref name="yle">{{cite web |url=http://yle.fi/urheilu/carl_lewis_neuvoi_jussi_kanervoa_keep_things_simple/8001942 |title=Carl Lewis neuvoi Jussi Kanervoa: "Keep things simple" |language=Finnish |publisher=[[Yleisradio]] |author=Hänninen, Petri |date=19 May 2015 |accessdate=16 July 2015}}</ref> He took up the [[400 m hurdles]] again in 2014, running 51.52 in his first race over the senior (91.4&nbsp;cm) hurdles; the time broke [[Ossi Mildh]]'s HIFK club record from 1954 and was the fastest ever debut by a Finn.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hifkfriidrott.fi/?x118281=501781 |publisher=HIFK Friidrott |title=Jussi Kanervo sävähdytti! |date=24 March 2014 |language=Finnish |accessdate=16 July 2015}}</ref><ref name="lex">{{cite web |url=http://blogit.mtv.fi/lexhollo/2014/03/26/jussi-kanervo-virittelee-kesan-yleisurheilupommia/ |title=Jussi Kanervo virittelee kesän yleisurheilupommia |publisher=[[MTV3]] |language=Finnish |author=Hollo, Lex |date=26 March 2014 |accessdate=16 July 2015}}</ref> He won his third consecutive Finnish championship title in the 110 m hurdles that year, but his times in that event had stagnated and the 400 m hurdles became his main event;<ref name="tp"/><ref name="yle"/> at the [[2014 European Athletics Championships]] in [[Zürich]] he competed only in the 400 m hurdles, setting a personal best 50.35 in the heats but withdrawing from the semi-finals due to an injury.<ref name="yle"/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.iltasanomat.fi/yleisurheilu/art-1288725104299.html |title=Jättiyllätys! Oskari Mörö SE-ajalla loppukilpailuun! |date=13 August 2014 |accessdate=16 July 2015 |language=Finnish |publisher=[[Ilta-Sanomat]] |author=Heikura, Kerttu}}</ref>

Kanervo broke 50 seconds in the 400 m hurdles for the first time on 11 April 2015 in [[Greenville, North Carolina]], running 49.78.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.yleisurheilu.fi/uutinen/kanervo-aitoi-ennatyksensa-4978 |title=Kanervo aitoi ennätyksensä 49,78 |date=12 April 2015 |accessdate=16 July 2015 |language=Finnish |publisher=Yleisurheilu.fi}}</ref> He suffered from a persistent groin injury through the 2015 collegiate season, but stayed in good form; he qualified for his first [[2015 NCAA Division I Outdoor Track and Field Championships|NCAA championship final]], placing sixth in 50.20.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.yleisurheilu.fi/uutinen/kanervo-kuudes-yhdysvaltain-yliopistomestaruuskisoissa |title=Kanervo kuudes Yhdysvaltain yliopistomestaruuskisoissa |date=13 June 2015 |accessdate=16 July 2015 |publisher=Yleisurheilu.fi |language=Finnish}}</ref> At the [[2015 European Athletics U23 Championships|2015 European U23 Championships]] in [[Tallinn]] Kanervo improved his personal best to 49.66, winning silver behind [[Patryk Dobek]] of Poland; he was Finland's only medalist.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.aamulehti.fi/Urheilu/1194989675544/artikkeli/kanervo+rutisti+alle+50+sekunnin+ja+juhli+em-hopeaa.html |title=Kanervo rutisti alle 50 sekunnin ja juhli EM-hopeaa |author=Sonninen, Antti-Pekka |publisher=[[Aamulehti]] |date=12 July 2015 |accessdate=16 July 2015 |language=Finnish}}</ref>
