'''Kevin Nkem Nwankwor''' (born 15 August 1976) is a [[Nigerian]]-born [[actor]], [[film director]] and [[Executive producer|producer]]. Kevin gained fame after he wrote and produced the movies ''[[Enemy Within (2012 film)|Enemy Within]]'' and ''[[A Mother's Love (2012 film)|A Mother's Love]]'' which went on to be among the official film selection at Gwinnett Center Film Festival and [[Marbella International Film Festival]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.washingtonnewsworld.com/item/entertainment/2515/INTERNATIONAL-FILM-COMPANY-SEEKS-DISTRIBUTION-FOR-NEW-INDIE-FILM | title=INTERNATIONAL FILM COMPANY SEEKS DISTRIBUTION FOR NEW INDIE FILM | publisher=Washington NewsWorld | date=4 April 2014 | accessdate=16 July 2015}}</ref>

==Early life and career==
Kevin Nwankwor was born in [[Issele Ukwu]], an [[Igbo people|Igbo]] city in [[Aniocha North|Aniocha North local government area]] of [[Delta State]] where he went on to finish his primary and secondary school education. In 2000, he earned a [[Bachelor of Science]] degree in [[Project Management]] from the [[Federal University of Technology, Owerri]]. While in the University, he starred in Nollywood movies ''Enemies'' and ''Heartless'' which also starred veteran actors [[Kanayo O. Kanayo]], [[Saint Obi]] and [[Eucharia Anunobi]]. He also formed a Christian drama group "The Heavenly Theater" and went on to write short plays like ''In The Den of Agbalakwem I & II'' and ''Ashes of Regret''.<ref name="nwa">{{cite web | url=http://www.kevstelgroup.com/meet-kevstel-1.html | title=Meet Kevstel | publisher=Kevstel Group | accessdate=16 July 2015 | archive-url=https://archive.is/20150109142645/https://www.kevstelgroup.com/meet-kevstel-1.html | archive-date=9 January 2015 | url-status=dead }}</ref>

Kevin relocated to [[Atlanta]] in 2008 where he earned a [[Master of Business Administration|MBA degree]] in [[Marketing]] and then obtained a
[[Master of Fine Arts]] in filmmaking from the [[New York Film Academy]]. After writing and producing ''[[Enemy Within (2012 film)|Enemy Within]]'' and ''[[A Mother's Love (2012 film)|A Mother's Love]]'', he went on to make his directing debut in the award-winning film ''[[Tempting Fate (2015 film)|Tempting Fate]]''.<ref name="kevin">{{cite web | url=http://www.newswire.com/press-release/award-winning-tempting-fate-film-director-kevin-nkem-nwankwor | title=Award Winning "Tempting Fate" Film Director Kevin Nkem Nwankwor (KNN) makes his Director's | publisher=NewsWire | date=27 January 2015 | accessdate=16 July 2015}}</ref>

He is the C.E.O of KevStel Group LLC, a [[Faith-based marketing|faith-based entertainment]] company he founded with the aim “to provide quality content based on faith, hope and love through visuals and print”.<ref name="nwa" />

==Personal life==
Kevin Nwankwor is married to Unoma Nwankwor, a Christian author who is known for her writings of ''Unexpected Blessing'', ''When You Let Go'' and ''The Christmas Ultimatum''. They reside in Atlanta with their two children.<ref name="kevin" />
