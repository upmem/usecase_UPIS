'''Inga-Brita Castrén''' (1919-2003) was a Finnish theologian who spent ten years abroad working in Geneva for the [[World Student Christian Federation]], the [[YWCA|World YWCA]], and the [[World Council of Churches]] as an [[ecumenical]] adviser. Returning to Finland, she worked in adult education and served as the General Secretary of the Finnish Ecumenical Council. After retirement, Castrén continued writing for a decade.

==Biography==
Inga-Brita Ulrika Elisabet Castrén was born 26 March 1919 in [[Helsinki]], Finland. She was the daughter of [[Supreme Administrative Court of Finland]] President and one-time interim Prime Minister [[Urho Castrén]].<ref name=father>{{cite web|title=Castrén, Urho Joonas|url=http://arkisto.kokoomus.net/kokoomusbiografia/elamakerta-artikkelit/castren-urho-joonas/|website=arkisto|publisher=Porvarillisen Työn Arkisto|accessdate=16 July 2015|location=Finland|language=Finnish}}</ref>

Castrén earned a master's degree in theology and philosophy and entered the foreign service,<ref name=obituary>{{cite news|last1=Cantell|first1=Risto|title=Inga-Brita Castrén|url=http://www.hs.fi/muistot/a1364355950076|accessdate=16 July 2015|publisher=Helsingin Sanomat|date=31 December 2003|location=Helsinki, Finland|language=Finnish}}</ref> in the 1940s,<ref name="brief bio">{{cite web|title=Castrén, Inga-Brita (1919 - 2003)|url=http://www.kansallisbiografia.fi/kb/artikkeli/8084/|website=Kansallisbiografia|publisher=Kansallisbiografia|accessdate=16 July 2015|location=Helsinki, Finland|language=Finnish}}</ref> serving as Secretary of Foreign Affairs, overseeing women's work and the Finnish Christian Student Association.<ref name=obituary />

She worked in Geneva between 1958<ref name="1958 Secy to Africa">{{cite web|title=213.14.85 Secretaries, 1956-1958, Dirks-Yazikoff, Correspondence, 1956 - 1958 (Box)|url=http://archives.wcc-coe.org/query/detail.aspx?ID=38532|website=World Council of Churches|publisher=World Council of Churches|accessdate=16 July 2015}}</ref> and 1962 as the [[World Student Christian Federation]]'s Secretary to Africa.<ref name=obituary /> Between 1962 and 1969 she served as the World YWCA Consultant on Christian Education and Ecumenical Affairs.<ref name=YWCA>{{cite journal|title=In Memoriam|journal=Common Concern|date=March 2004|issue=121|page=26|url=http://www.worldywca.org/content/download/707/8190/version/1/file/cc121.pdf|accessdate=16 July 2015|publisher=World Young Women’s Christian Association|location=Geneva, Switzerland|language=|issn=1025-4099|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304194153/http://www.worldywca.org/content/download/707/8190/version/1/file/cc121.pdf|archive-date=4 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> Then in 1969 she joined the [[World Council of Churches]] as executive secretary for mission education. After four years, she left Geneva, returning to Finland in 1973.<ref name=WCC>{{cite web|title=In Memoriam|url=https://www.oikoumene.org/en/resources/documents/central-committee/2005/reports-and-documents/in-memoriam|website=World Council of Churches|publisher=World Council of Churches|accessdate=16 July 2015|location=Geneva, Switzerland|date=22 February 2005}}</ref>

Arriving back in Finland, Castrén supervised adult educational learning activities and from 1974-1984 served as the General Secretary of the {{Interlanguage link multi|Finnish Ecumenical Council|fi|3=Suomen ekumeeninen neuvosto}} (SEN). Even after her retirement, Castrén attended conferences and remained abreast of changes in the professional field. In 2000, Castrén was awarded an honorary PhD in Theology from the [[University of Helsinki]].<ref name=obituary />

Castrén died in Helsinki on 31 December 2003.<ref name=obituary />
