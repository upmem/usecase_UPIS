'''''Gnathothlibus samoaensis''''' is a [[moth]] of the  family [[Sphingidae]]. It is known from [[Samoa]].<ref name="Lachlan"/>

The length of the forewings is 35.4–44.3&nbsp;mm for males and 43.6–44.7 for females. It is similar to ''[[Gnathothlibus fijiensis]]'' but the outer edge of the [[Tegula (insect anatomy)|tegula]] is creamy white.

==Etymology==
The specific name ''samoaensis'' is derived from the Pacific Island nation of Samoa, formally Western Samoa, the only recorded locality for the species.
