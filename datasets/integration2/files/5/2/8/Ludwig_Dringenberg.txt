'''Ludwig Dringenberg''' (born between  1410 and 1415

at  Dringenberg in the [[Prince-Bishopric of Paderborn]]; died in 1477 at [[Sélestat]] in [[Alsace]]), was a German monk, educator and humanist.

Born in [[Dringenberg]] in  [[Westphalia]], Ludwig  probably attended the school of the [[Brethren of the Common Life]], known as the Hieronymusschule in the monastery at [[Böddeken]]. He began his studies at [[Heidelberg]] in 1430 and in 1441, he was appointed director of the [[Latin school]] at Sélestat. At Sélestat he founded the famous [[Humanist Library of Sélestat|Humanist Library]] in 1442. He had the humanist [[Jakob Wimpfeling]] as a pupil.
