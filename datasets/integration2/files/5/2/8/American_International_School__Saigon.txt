The '''American International School''' (AIS) in [[Ho Chi Minh City]] (Saigon), Vietnam, was formed in 2006 to provide American education in [[Vietnam]].<ref name="VNB2008">{{citation|url=http://english.vietnamnet.vn/education/2008/03/771335/ |periodical=Vietnamnet Bridge |date=2008-03-02 |access-date=2010-04-12 |title=Vietnamese-American educator returns home to teach |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090210030652/http://english.vietnamnet.vn/education/2008/03/771335/ |archive-date=2009-02-10 }}</ref> {{asof|2009}}, it enrolled roughly 900 students in grades K-12, 90% of them are Vietnamese <ref name="VNB"/> Among the school's staff are five [[Vietnamese American]] .<ref name="VNB2008"/>

==Curriculum==
American International School uses English as the [[medium of instruction]]. It offers both [[IB Diploma Programme|International Baccalaureate Diploma Program]] and [[Advanced Placement]] program.<ref name="VNB"/>

==Ownership and finances==
Intellectual Resources Management Co., which owns the school, is also the owner of the [[Saigon Institute of Technology]] (SaigonTech).<ref name="VNB"/> AIS operates campuses in [[Bình Thạnh District]], [[District 1, Ho Chi Minh City|District 1]]  and [[District 10, Ho Chi Minh City|District 10]], with construction proceeding on a third [[Vietnamese đồng|VND]]1.5 trillion (US$90 million) campus in [[Nhà Bè District]], Ho Chi Minh City. The campus was opened on August 2014 <ref name="VNB">{{citation|url=http://english.vietnamnet.vn/education/2009/07/857085/ |periodical=Vietnamnet Bridge |date=2009-07-08 |access-date=2010-04-12 |title=Work to start on US$90 mil int’l school |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090719104602/http://english.vietnamnet.vn/education/2009/07/857085/ |archive-date=2009-07-19 }}</ref>

==Accreditation==
American International School, Saigon is accredited by the IBO to teach IBDP and seeking accreditations from the Council of International Schools (CIS) and Western Association of Schools and Colleges (WASC). As of 2015, AIS High School division is accredited by both CIS and WASC.
