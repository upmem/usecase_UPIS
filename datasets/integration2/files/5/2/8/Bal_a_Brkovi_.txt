'''Balša Brković''' ([[Montenegrin Cyrillic]]: Балша Брковић) (born 1966 in [[Podgorica]], [[Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia|Yugoslavia]]) is a [[Montenegrins (citizens)|Montenegrin]] writer, essayist and theatre critic. He is also editor of cultural section of daily newspaper [[Vijesti]], and one of the prominent members of the social liberal  opposition party [[United Reform Action]] (URA).

==Biography==
He graduated from the [[University of Belgrade]] [[University of Belgrade Faculty of Philosophy|Faculty of Philosophy]]. He is an editor for the [[Vijesti]] newspaper for its culture section and is deeply involved with preservation of the original Montenegrin language.<ref>[https://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&sl=auto&tl=en&u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.dw-world.de%2Fpopups%2Fpopup_printcontent%2F0%2C%2C3626114%2C00.html "New characters in the Montenegrin language?" - DW-World]</ref> He is a member of the [[Montenegrin PEN Center]]. He was also a speaker at the International Festival for short stories in 2009.<ref>[https://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&sl=auto&tl=en&u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.pobjeda.co.me%2Fcitanje.php%3Fdatum%3D2009-06-30%26id%3D166779 "Ill with incurable youth" - Pobjeda Online]</ref> In 2017, he has signed the [[Declaration on the Common Language]] of the [[Croats]], [[Serbs]], [[Bosniaks]] and [[Montenegrins]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Derk|first=Denis|title=Donosi se Deklaracija o zajedničkom jeziku Hrvata, Srba, Bošnjaka i Crnogoraca|trans-title=A Declaration on the Common Language of Croats, Serbs, Bosniaks and Montenegrins is About to Appear|language=Serbo-Croatian|url=http://www.vecernji.hr/hrvatska/deklaracija-o-zajednickom-jeziku-iz-zagreba-donosi-se-30-ozujka-u-sarajevu-1159142|publisher=[[Večernji list]]|pages=6–7|location=Zagreb|issn=0350-5006|date=28 March 2017|archivedate=23 May 2017|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6qg0sFVHO?url=https://www.vecernji.hr/vijesti/deklaracija-o-zajednickom-jeziku-iz-zagreba-donosi-se-30-ozujka-u-sarajevu-1159142|url-status=live|accessdate=5 June 2019}}</ref>

Brković published collections of poetry: ''Konji jedu breskve'' (1985), ''Filip boje srebra'' (1991), ''Rt Svete Marije'' (1993), ''Contrapposto'' (1998) and ''Dvojenje'' (2001) and novels ''Privatna galerija'' (2002)<ref>[https://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&sl=auto&tl=en&u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.pobjeda.co.me%2Fcitanje.php%3Fdatum%3D2004-02-01%26id%3D18673 "Laureate Balsa Brkovic" - Pobjeda Online]</ref> and ''Paranoja u Podgorici'' (2010).

His father [[Jevrem Brković]] is also a well-known Montenegrin writer.
