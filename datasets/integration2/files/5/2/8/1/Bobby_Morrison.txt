'''Bobby Morrison''' (born 1945) is a former American football player and coach.  Morrison played [[college football]] at [[Findlay College]].  He coached college football for 31 years at seven programs, including [[Virginia Cavaliers football|Virginia]], [[William & Mary Tribe football|William & Mary]], [[VMI Keydets football|VMI]], [[NC State Wolfpack football|North Carolina State]], [[LSU Tigers football|LSU]], [[Navy Midshipmen football|Navy]], and [[Michigan Wolverines football|Michigan]].<ref name=mich/><ref>{{cite news|title=Bobby Morrison To Retire|newspaper=TheWolverine.com|date=February 6, 2002|url=http://www.virginiatech.rivals.com/content.asp?CID=81132}}</ref>

==Player==
Morrison was born in [[Youngstown, Ohio]], and graduated from [[Findlay College]] in 1967.  He was a linebacker for the Findlay football team.<ref>{{cite news|title=NCS Staff Filled|newspaper=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette|date=March 5, 1976|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=VuQNAAAAIBAJ&sjid=gm0DAAAAIBAJ&pg=4697,537935&dq=bobby-morrison+football+coach&hl=en}}</ref>

==Coach==
In the early 1970s, Morrison was the linebackers coach at William & Mary.<ref>{{cite news|title=Irish Prepare for Michigan|date=August 30, 1999|publisher=University of Notre Dame|url=http://www.und.com/sports/m-footbl/spec-rel/083099aac.html|access-date=October 9, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191214020426/https://und.com/|archive-date=December 14, 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref>  During the 1974 and 1975 seasons, he was an assistant coach in charge of linebackers and, later, offensive guards and centers at the [[Virginia Military Institute]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Keydets' Willison Lineman Of Week|newspaper=Toledo Blade|date=October 8, 1974|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=m_NOAAAAIBAJ&sjid=HAIEAAAAIBAJ&pg=6954,4254416&dq=bobby-morrison+football+coach&hl=en}}</ref>  In March 1976, he was hired as an assistant coach at North Carolina State under [[Bo Rein]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Morrison joins N.C. State staff|newspaper=Star-News|date=March 5, 1976|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=tHQ0AAAAIBAJ&sjid=CxMEAAAAIBAJ&pg=4959,940516&dq=bobby-morrison+football+coach&hl=en}}</ref>

In December 1978, when Bo Rein left NC State for LSU, he brought six of his assistant coaches, including Morrison, with him.<ref>{{cite news|title=Rein takes State aides|newspaper=Star-News|date=December 13, 1979|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=ksQsAAAAIBAJ&sjid=OxMEAAAAIBAJ&pg=5857,3449757&dq=bobby-morrison+football+coach&hl=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Rein Names Four, Goes Recruiting|newspaper=Schenectady Gazette|date=December 3, 1979|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=MxIhAAAAIBAJ&sjid=4XQFAAAAIBAJ&pg=4496,930827&dq=bobby-morrison+football+coach&hl=en}}</ref>  From 1979 to 1981, Morrison was the inside linebackers coach at LSU.<ref>{{cite web|title=LSU Football All-Time Assistant Coaches|publisher=LSU Sports|url=http://www.lsusports.net/ViewArticle.dbml?DB_OEM_ID=5200&ATCLID=1478150}}</ref>

In 1982, Morrison joined the coaching staff at Navy and became the team's defensive coordinator. He remained at Navy through the 1986 season.<ref>{{cite news|title=Transactions |newspaper=Times-Union|date=January 13, 1987|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=nylHAAAAIBAJ&sjid=uXoMAAAAIBAJ&pg=4253,2120792&dq=bobby-morrison+football+coach&hl=en}}</ref>

He was an assistant football coach at the [[University of Michigan]] from 1987 to 2002.  Hired in 1987 by [[Bo Schembechler]], he served as the team's outside linebackers coach (1987-1994), offensive line coach (1995–1996), recruiting coordinator (1996–2002), and special teams coach (1997–2002).  As Michigan's recruiting coordinator, he helped Michigan secure recruiting classes ranked in the top ten nationally in seven of eight years, including the top-rated recruiting class in 1998.<ref name=mich>{{cite web|title=Morrison Takes Leave of Absence from U-M Football|publisher=University of Michigan|date=February 6, 2002|url=http://www.mgoblue.com/sports/m-footbl/spec-rel/020602aac.html}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Traveling takes toll, but it has benefits|newspaper=The Detroit News|date=January 31, 1999|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=DTNB&s_site=detnews&f_site=detnews&f_sitename=Detroit+News%2C+The+%28MI%29&p_multi=DTNB&p_theme=gannett&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0F750030534FBF3D&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Michigan reaps its reward: No. 1 Wolverines land nation's top recruiting class|author=Taylor Bell|date=February 5, 1998 |newspaper=Chicago Sun-Times |url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=CSTB&p_theme=cstb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EB423798D06DFAD&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM}}</ref>
