'''Olaf Olsen Skramstad''' (8 March 1894 – 12 November 1956) was a [[Norway|Norwegian]] politician for the [[Communist Party of Norway|Communist Party]].

He was organized in a trade union and the [[Norwegian Labour Party]] in 1915. He later joined the Communist Party and was a member of [[Åmot]] [[municipal council (Norway)|municipal council]] from 1926 to 1933. He was the secretary in the [[Norwegian Union of Forestry and Land Workers]] from 1929 to 1931. In 1930 he was sentenced to 4 months of jail for participating in the Etna-Dokka labour conflict.<ref name=arbleks>{{cite encyclopedia|year=1936|volume=6|title=Skramstad, Olaf Olsen|encyclopedia=[[Arbeidernes Leksikon]]|editor1=[[Jakob Friis|Friis, Jakob]] |editor2=[[Trond Hegna|Hegna, Trond]] |editor3=[[Dagfin Juel|Juel, Dagfin]] |publisher=Arbeidermagasinets Forlag|location=Oslo|language=Norwegian|page=343}}</ref>

During the [[occupation of Norway by Nazi Germany]], he was imprisoned in October 1941. He was incarcerated in [[Hamar]] from 26 October to 4 November, then in [[Grini concentration camp]] until 3 April 1942, then in [[Sachsenhausen concentration camp]].<ref>{{cite book|editor=[[Kristian Ottosen|Ottosen, Kristian]]|title=Nordmenn i fangenskap 1940–1945|edition=2nd|year=2004|publisher=Universitetsforlaget|location=Oslo|language=Norwegian|isbn=82-15-00288-9|page=623}}</ref> The communist inmates in the camp formed a [[caucus]] of sorts, and Sørum was among the leading members along with [[Kristian Mugaas]], [[Johan Strand Johansen]], [[Halvor Sørum]], [[Olaf Bjerke]] and [[Erling Heiestad]].<ref>{{cite book|last=Pryser|first=Tore|authorlink=Tore Pryser|title=Klassen og nasjonen 1935-1946|year=1988|series=Volume four of ''[[Arbeiderbevegelsens historie i Norge]]''|publisher=Tiden|location=Oslo|language=Norwegian|isbn=82-10-02754-9|page=461}}</ref>

He died in November 1956 and was buried at Åmot.<ref name=dis>{{cite web|url=http://www.disnorge.no/gravminner/vis.php?mode=x|title=Cemeteries in Norway|publisher=DIS-Norge|language=Norwegian|accessdate=4 February 2011}}</ref>
