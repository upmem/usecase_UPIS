{{Politics of the European Union}}
The '''Treaty of Accession 2011''' is an agreement between [[Member state of the European Union|the member states]] of the [[European Union]] and [[Croatia]] concerning Croatia's accession to the EU. It was signed on 9 December 2011 in  [[Brussels]] by the heads of state or government of the 27 member states and 
by the President of Croatia, [[Ivo Josipović]], and Prime Minister [[Jadranka Kosor]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-16106982 |title=Croatia signs treaty to join EU in middle of 2013 |publisher=BBC News |date=9 December 2011}}</ref>

The Treaty entered into force on 1 July 2013, making Croatia the 28th Member state of the European Union although following the withdrawal of the [[United Kingdom]] in January 2020 Croatia then became the 27th Member state.

==History==
{{main|2013 enlargement of the European Union}}

[[Croatia]] submitted its application to join the [[European Union]] on 21 February 2003, became an official candidate on 18 June 2004, and started accession negotiations on 3 October 2005.

On 24 June 2011, the [[European Council]] called for the finishing of negotiations by the end of the month, and signing of the Treaty of Accession by the end of the year. Negotiations were subsequently closed on 30 June 2011, and on 14 September 2011 the accession treaty was finalised and made public. On 12 October 2011 the [[European Commission]] delivered a favourable opinion on the accession of Croatia to the European Union. As a result, on 1 December 2011 the [[European Parliament]] gave assent to the application of Croatia to become member of the European Union. The parliament voted in favour with 564 positive votes, 38 negative votes and 32 abstentions.

The treaty was signed on 9 December 2011 in [[Brussels]] and entered into force on 1 July 2013, having been ratified by Croatia and the European Union's 27 member states.

The full official name of the treaty is:

{{quotation|'''Treaty''' between the [[Belgium|Kingdom of Belgium]], the [[Bulgaria|Republic of Bulgaria]], the [[Czech Republic]], the [[Denmark|Kingdom of Denmark]], the [[Germany|Federal Republic of Germany]], the [[Estonia|Republic of Estonia]], the [[Greece|Hellenic Republic]], the [[Spain|Kingdom of Spain]], the [[France|French Republic]], [[Republic of Ireland|Ireland]], the [[Italy|Italian Republic]], the [[Cyprus|Republic of Cyprus]], the [[Latvia|Republic of Latvia]], the [[Lithuania|Republic of Lithuania]], the [[Luxembourg|Grand Duchy of Luxembourg]], the [[Hungary|Republic of Hungary]], the [[Malta|Republic of Malta]], the [[Kingdom of the Netherlands]], the [[Austria|Republic of Austria]], the [[Poland|Republic of Poland]], the [[Portugal|Portuguese Republic]], [[Romania]], the [[Slovenia|Republic of Slovenia]], the [[Slovakia|Slovak Republic]], the [[Finland|Republic of Finland]], the [[Sweden|Kingdom of Sweden]], the [[United Kingdom|United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland]] (Member States of the European Union) and the [[Croatia|Republic of Croatia]] '''concerning the accession of the Republic of Croatia to the European Union'''.}}

The treaty, which is 250 pages long, provides for amendments to the treaties to add Croatian representatives into EU institutions (including transitional provisions before new elections take place) and outlines Croatia's various financial contributions. The document does not include a [[Mechanism for Cooperation and Verification|monitoring mechanism]] of Croatia by the European Commission to ensure continued reform, as was the case with [[Bulgaria]] and [[Romania]]. Two protocols promised to states during the [[ratification of the Treaty of Lisbon]], one making several [[Treaties of the European Union#Irish protocol on the Lisbon Treaty|guarantees to Ireland]] and another granting an [[Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European Union#Proposed Czech protocol|opt-out from the Charter of Fundamental Rights to the Czech Republic]], were planned to be ratified alongside the accession treaty,<ref>[http://www.vlada.hr/en/naslovnica/novosti_i_najave/2011/srpanj/potpisivanje_ugovora_o_pristupanju_vjerojatno_u_varsavi Croatia's EU accession treaty most likely to be signed in Warsaw] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120103230424/http://www.vlada.hr/en/naslovnica/novosti_i_najave/2011/srpanj/potpisivanje_ugovora_o_pristupanju_vjerojatno_u_varsavi |date=3 January 2012 }}, Croatian Government 20 July 11</ref><ref>[http://croatiantimes.com/news/General_News/2011-09-22/22023/_EU_accession_treaty_goes_public_ EU accession treaty goes public], Croatian Times 22 September 2011</ref><ref>Pop, Valentina (14 September 2011) [http://euobserver.com/15/113618 EU finalises Croatia membership treaty], EU Observer</ref><ref name="Croatia treaty">[http://www.vlada.hr/en/content/download/178616/2606508/file/Ugovor%20o%20pristupanju%20Republike%20Hrvatske%20Europskoj%20uniji%20-%20ENG.pdf Treaty concerning the Accession of the Republic of Croatia] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111201202150/http://www.vlada.hr/en/content/download/178616/2606508/file/Ugovor%20o%20pristupanju%20Republike%20Hrvatske%20Europskoj%20uniji%20-%20ENG.pdf |date=1 December 2011 }} (PDF), Council of the European Union and the Government of Croatia</ref><ref name="Croatia sign date">[http://www.euractiv.com/enlargement/warsaw-treaty-seal-croatias-eu-accession-news-508032 'Warsaw Treaty' to seal Croatia's EU accession], EurActiv 30 September 2011</ref> but both were ultimately delayed.

In addition to the Treaty of Accession, a Final Act was signed. The Final Act registers the results of the accession negotiations, including declarations made by the parties. It also laid down arrangements for the period between signing and entry into force of the treaty.

==Ratification==
The treaty required ratification by all EU member states and Croatia, conforming to their respective constitutional provisions, and deposition of the ratification instruments with the [[Government of Italy]] by 30 June 2013 to come into force on 1 July 2013.<ref name=sabor>{{cite web|publisher=[[Sabor]]|url=http://www.sabor.hr/Default.aspx?sec=5301|title=Ratification Process of the Treaty of Accession in National Parliaments of EU Member States|accessdate=1 March 2013}}</ref> This process was completed on 21 June 2013.<ref name="consilium">{{cite web|url=http://www.consilium.europa.eu/policies/agreements/search-the-agreements-database?command=details&id=&lang=en&aid=2011051&doclang=EN%22|title=Agreement details|publisher=[[Council of the European Union]]|accessdate=2012-08-15}}</ref>

====Slovenia====
In July and September 2012, officials of the Slovenian Parliament and the Slovenian Ministry of Foreign Affairs stated that they would not ratify Croatia's Treaty of Accession until an agreement was reached on [[Croatia–Slovenia relations#Ljubljanska banka|how to handle the debt of Slovenian bank Ljubljanska banka]], which went bankrupt during the [[breakup of Yugoslavia]], to its Croatian customers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://euobserver.com/enlargement/117629|title=Slovenia puts €172mn price tag on Croatia's EU entry|date=2012-09-21|accessdate=2012-09-21|last=Rettman|first=Andrew|publisher=[[EUobserver]]}}</ref> In February 2013, representatives of all major parties in Slovenia agreed to approve Croatia's accession after experts and foreign ministers from both countries reached a compromise deal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.croatiantimes.com/news/General_News/2013-02-18/32173/Slovenia_gives_green_light_for_Croatia's_EU_acession|title=Slovenia gives green light for Croatia's EU acession|date=2013-02-18|accessdate=2013-02-19|publisher=Croatian Times}}</ref>  The Prime Ministers of Slovenia and Croatia signed a memorandum outlining the settlement on 11 March, with [[Janez Janša]], Prime Minister of Slovenia, saying that ratification of the accession treaty would occur "within 30 days" of the signing of the memorandum.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/2013/03/07/us-croatia-slovenia-eu-idUSBRE9260M620130307|title=Croatia reaches deal with Slovenia to unblock EU entry|date=2013-03-07|accessdate=2013-03-17|publisher=[[Reuters]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vecernji.hr/vijesti/uzivo-milanovic-jansa-potpisali-memorandum-ljubljanskoj-banci-clanak-522434|title=Milanović i Janša potpisali memorandum o Ljubljanskoj banci|date=2013-03-11|accessdate=2013-03-11|publisher=[[Večernji list]]}}</ref>  On 2 April 2013, the Slovenian Parliament gave its consent to Croatia's accession.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rtvslo.si/evropska-unija/drzavni-zbor-soglasno-ratificiral-hrvasko-pristopno-pogodbo-z-evropsko-unijo/305755|title=Foto: Državni zbor soglasno ratificiral hrvaško pristopno pogodbo z Evropsko unijo|publisher=}}</ref>
