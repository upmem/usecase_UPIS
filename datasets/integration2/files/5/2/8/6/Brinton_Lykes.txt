'''M. Brinton Lykes''' (born 1949) is an American psychologist who has established a reputation for her work on psychosocial effects of state-sponsored terror and organized violence.

==Life==
'''Lykes''' originally hailed from New Orleans. She obtained her first BA degree from [[Hollins University]] before proceeding to [[Harvard University]] where she obtained an MDiv in Applied Theology.  She obtained a PhD in Community Psychology from Boston College.<ref>{{cite web|title=Brinton Lykes|url=http://www.feministvoices.com/brinton-lykes|accessdate=13 April 2017}}</ref>

'''Lykes''' has worked at [[Boston College]] since 1992 where she was promoted from assistant Professor to full Professor in the Lynch School of Education.  She has held various posts including Department chair and Associate Dean.

==Work==
'''Lykes''' work has focused on understanding the impact of state-sponsored violence and terror.  She has worked mostly in Central America, in particular with the [[Maya peoples]] <ref>{{Cite journal|last=Brinton Lykes|first=M.|date=1994-02-01|title=Terror, silencing and children: International, multidisciplinary collaboration with Guatemalan Maya communities|journal=Social Science & Medicine|volume=38|issue=4|pages=543–552|doi=10.1016/0277-9536(94)90250-X|pmid=8184317}}</ref> of [[Guatemala]]. She has used [[participatory action research]] and [[oral history]] as her primary research methods.<ref>{{cite web|title=PFV Interview with Brinton Lykes|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KDgIvn1flF4|accessdate=14 April 2017}}</ref> She is the co-founder of the Martín-Baró Fund for Mental Health and Human Rights.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Martin Baro Fund|url=http://www.martinbarofund.org/|accessdate=14 April 2017}}</ref>

She is the co-editor of the [[International Journal of Transitional Justice]].

==Awards==
'''Lykes''' has received a variety of awards for her work including the [[APA International Humanitarian Award]]<ref>{{cite web|title=M. Brinton Lykes: International Humanitarian Award|url=http://psycnet.apa.org/index.cfm?fa=buy.optionToBuy&id=2013-42570-047|accessdate=28 April 2017}}</ref> of the American Psychological Association and the Ignacio Martín-Baró Lifetime Peace Practitioner Award of the APA Society for the Study of Peace, Conflict and Violence.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ignacio Martín-Baró Martin Lifetime Peace Practitioner Award|url=http://www.apa.org/about/awards/div-48-martin.aspx|accessdate=29 April 2017}}</ref>
