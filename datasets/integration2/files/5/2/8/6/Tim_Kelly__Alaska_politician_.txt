'''Timothy Donahue Kelly''' (August 15, 1944 &ndash; August 17, 2009) was an American businessman and politician.

Born in [[Sacramento, California]], Kelly graduated from Sacramento High School in 1962. He served in the [[United States Marine Corps]] and later in the Alaska Air National Guard. He was a legislative aide in [[California]] and [[Nevada]]. In 1970, he moved to [[Alaska]] and settled in [[Anchorage, Alaska]]. He was in the banking business and was a [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]]. Kelly served in the [[Alaska House of Representatives]] from 1977 to 1979 and then served in the [[Alaska State Senate]] from 1979 to 2001. In 1989 and 1990, Kelly served as the senate president. Kelly died at his home, in Anchorage, Alaska, from heart problems.<ref>[http://www.akleg.gov/basis/Member/Detail/21?code=KET Alaska Legislature-Tim Kelly]</ref><ref>'Former legislator Tim Kelly dies,' '''Alaska Dispatch News,''' Sheila Toomey, August 17, 2009</ref>
