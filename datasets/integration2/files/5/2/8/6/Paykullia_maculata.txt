==Description==
''Paykullia maculata'' can reach a length of {{convert|5|-|6|mm}}. The adults of this species are very variable, especially in size and in the pattern of wing markings.<ref name=":0" /> These small flies have a shining black body with bristly hair. The apical half of the wings show darkened veins and costal area.<ref name="NS">{{Cite web|url=https://www.naturespot.org.uk/species/paykullia-maculata|title=''Paykullia maculata'' {{!}} NatureSpot|website=www.naturespot.org.uk|language=en|access-date=2018-07-30}}</ref><ref>Bei-Bienko, G.Y. & Steyskal, G.C. ==Biology==
Adults can be seen from May to September, with several generations. Usually they rest or run around on stones or among the foliage.<ref name=":0" /> The larvae are intestinal parasites of ''[[Porcellio scaber]]'' and ''[[Oniscus asellus]]'',<ref name=NS/> and woodlice from ''[[Protracheoniscus]]'' and ''[[Trachelipus]]'' genera.<ref name=":0" />
