'''Domingos Alexandre Martins Costa''' (born 6 September 1979), commonly known as '''Alex''', is a Portuguese former professional [[Association football|footballer]] who played mainly as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|right back]], and a current [[Manager (association football)|manager]].

Over seven seasons (15 years in total as a professional), he amassed [[Primeira Liga]] totals of 170 matches and seven goals, mainly at the service of [[Vitória S.C.|Vitória de Guimarães]]. He also spent four years in Germany, with [[VfL Wolfsburg|Wolfsburg]].

==Playing career==
Alex was born in [[Guimarães]]. After five seasons, spent at both [[AD Fafe]] and [[Moreirense F.C.|Moreirense FC]] he made his [[Primeira Liga]] debut with [[S.L. Benfica]], mainly deputising for established [[Miguel Monteiro|Miguel]]. In [[2004–05 Primeira Liga|2004–05]], he served a season-long loan spell at [[Vitória S.C.|Vitória de Guimarães]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/benfica/detalhe/alex-objectivo-e-voltar-ao-benfica|title=Alex: «Objectivo é voltar ao Benfica»|trans-title=Alex: «Goal is to return to Benfica»|newspaper=[[Record (newspaper)|Record]]|language=Portuguese|date=20 May 2005|accessdate=13 November 2018}}</ref>

After good performances at Vitória, which earned him a [[Portugal national football team|Portugal national team]] call-up (three matches, including two in the [[2006 FIFA World Cup]] [[2006 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)|qualifiers]]),<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.record.pt/internacional/competicoes-de-selecoes/europeu/detalhe/estreias-com-scolari-raramente-sao-a-doer.html|title=Estreias com Scolari raramente são a doer|trans-title=Debuts with Scolari rarely happen when it matters|newspaper=Record|language=Portuguese|date=30 May 2007|accessdate=1 January 2018}}</ref> Benfica sold Alex to [[VfL Wolfsburg]] on the very last days of the summer [[transfer window]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.shortnews.de/id/586148/Fussball-VfL-Wolfsburg-verpflichtet-portugiesischen-Verteidiger-Alex|title=Fußball: VfL Wolfsburg verpflichtet portugiesischen Verteidiger Alex|trans-title=Football: VfL Wolfsburg acquire Portuguese defender Alex|publisher=[[Kicker (sports magazine)|kicker]]|language=German|date=20 August 2005|accessdate=22 March 2010|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110717035913/http://www.shortnews.de/id/586148/Fussball-VfL-Wolfsburg-verpflichtet-portugiesischen-Verteidiger-Alex|archivedate=17 July 2011}}</ref> He had a relatively successful [[2005–06 Bundesliga|debut campaign]], but only appeared four times in the [[Bundesliga]] in the next three combined (none in the last two).

In late May 2009, Alex signed for former club Vitória de Guimarães on a [[Free transfer (association football)|free transfer]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/de/news/827/bundesliga/2009/06/26/1347511/alex-verl%C3%A4sst-wolfsburg-in-richtung-portugal|title=Alex verlässt Wolfsburg in Richtung Portugal|trans-title=Alex leaves Wolfsburg towards Portugal|publisher=[[Goal (website)|Goal]]|language=German|date=26 June 2009|accessdate=22 March 2010}}</ref> During [[2009–10 Primeira Liga|that first season]] he operated mostly as a [[Defender (association football)#Full-back|right back]], going on to be fully reconverted to the position in the following years and being an automatic first-choice.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.record.pt/futebol/futebol-nacional/liga-nos/v--guimaraes/detalhe/gemeos-falsos-489521|title=Gémeos falsos|trans-title=False twins|newspaper=Record|language=Portuguese|date=13 November 2010|accessdate=13 November 2018}}</ref>

==Coaching career==
On 3 July 2013, Alex announced his retirement from professional football at the age of nearly 34, immediately starting his managing career with [[F.C. Felgueiras 1932]] in the regional championships.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.abola.pt/nnh/ver.aspx?id=412655|title=Alex despediu-se em lágrimas|trans-title=Alex said goodbye in tears|newspaper=[[A Bola]]|language=Portuguese|date=3 July 2013|accessdate=11 July 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130706123029/http://www.abola.pt/nnh/ver.aspx?id=412655|archive-date=6 July 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>
