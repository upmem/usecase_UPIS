'''Marvin Stewart Jr.''' (born September 16, 1995) is an [[American football]] [[cornerback]] for the [[Cleveland Browns]] of the [[National Football League]] (NFL). He played [[college football]] at [[North Carolina Tar Heels football|North Carolina]]. He was drafted by the [[Tampa Bay Buccaneers]] in the second round of the [[2018 NFL Draft]].

==College career==
During his sophomore season, Stewart was suspended from the team due to a violation of team rules, along with teammate [[Mike Hughes (American football)|Mike Hughes]].  He was later charged with misdemeanor assault and battery, but charges were later dropped.<ref>{{cite web|title=UNC cornerbacks MJ Stewart, Mike Hughes suspended indefinitely|url=http://www.dailytarheel.com/article/2015/10/mj-stewart-mike-hughes-suspended-indefinitely|date=October 16, 2015|accessdate=March 20, 2018|work=Daily Tar Heel}}</ref>  Stewart was reinstated less than a week later.<ref>{{cite web|title=UNC cornerbacks Mike Hughes, MJ Stewart to be reinstated|author=James, Pat|url=http://www.dailytarheel.com/article/2015/10/stewart-and-hughes-to-be-reinstated|date=October 22, 2015|accessdate=March 20, 2018|work=Daily Tar Heel}}</ref>  There was speculation that Stewart would forgo his senior year and declare for the [[2017 NFL draft]], but he later announced that he would return for the 2017 season.<ref>{{cite web|title=UNC Football: Defensive back MJ Stewart to return|author=Pearson, Zack|url=https://keepingitheel.com/2016/12/31/unc-football-defensive-back-m-j-stewart-return/|website=keepingitheel.com|date=December 31, 2016|accessdate=March 20, 2018}}</ref>  Following his senior season, Stewart was named to the First Team All-ACC by [[Pro Football Focus]].<ref>{{cite web|title=UNC CB MJ Stewart Named First Team All-ACC|author=Martin, Ross|url=https://scout.com/college/north-carolina/Bolt/UNC-CB-MJ-Stewart-Named-First-Team-All-ACC-111829761|website=scout.com|date=December 6, 2017|accessdate=March 20, 2018}}</ref>

===Tampa Bay Buccaneers===
The [[Tampa Bay Buccaneers]] selected Stewart in the second round (53rd overall) of the [[2018 NFL Draft]]. Stewart was the fifth cornerback drafted in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|title=For their second pick of Round 2, Buccaneers pick CB M.J. Stewart|url=https://www.bucsnation.com/2018/4/27/17281994/for-their-second-pick-of-round-2-buccaneers-pick-cb-m-j-stewart|website=BucsNation.com|date=April 27, 2018|accessdate=July 7, 2018}}</ref>
{{external media 
| video1 =
[http://www.nfl.com/videos/nfl-draft/0ap3000000929581/Ronde-Barber-announces-the-Buccaneers-select-M-J-Stewart-No-53-in-the-2018-NFL-Draft Buccaneers draft M.J. Stewart 53rd overall]
}}
On May 10, 2018, the Tampa Bay Buccaneers signed Stewart to a four-year, [[United States dollar|$]]5.08 million [[contract]] that includes $2.48 million guaranteed and a [[signing bonus]] of $1.77 million.<ref>{{cite web |title=Spotrac.com: M.J. Stewart contract |url=https://www.spotrac.com/nfl/tampa-bay-buccaneers/m.j.-stewart-25152/ |access-date=January 1, 2019 |website=Spotrac.com}}</ref>

Throughout training camp, Stewart competed to be a starting cornerback against [[Ryan Smith (cornerback, born 1993)|Ryan Smith]], [[Vernon Hargreaves]], and [[Carlton Davis]].<ref>{{cite web |title=2018 Bucs Roster Reset: Defensive Backs |url=https://www.buccaneers.com/news/2018-bucs-roster-reset-defensive-backs-20733454 |access-date=January 1, 2018 |website=Buccaneers.com |last=Smith |first=Scott |date=June 19, 2018}}</ref> Head coach Dirk Koetter named Stewart the fifth cornerback on the depth chart to start the regular season, behind [[Brent Grimes]], [[Vernon Hargreaves]], Carlton Davis, and Ryan Smith.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ourlads.com: Tampa Bay Buccaneers Depth Chart: 10/01/2017 |url=https://www.ourlads.com/nfldepthcharts/archive/212/TB |access-date=January 1, 2019 |website=Ourlads.com}}</ref>

He made his professional regular season debut in the [[2018 Tampa Bay Buccaneers season|Tampa Bay Buccaneers’]] season-opener at the [[2018 New Orleans Saints season|New Orleans Saints]] and recorded four solo tackles during their 48-40 victory. The following week, he earned his first career start after Vernon Hargreaves sustained a shoulder injury the previous week and was placed on injured reserve.<ref>{{cite web |title=Bucs Lose Vernon Hargreaves to Injured Reserve |url=https://www.buccaneers.com/news/bucs-lose-vernon-hargreaves-to-injured-reserve |access-date=January 1, 2019 |website=Buccaneers.com |last=Smith |first=Scott |date=September 12, 2018}}</ref> Stewart finished the Buccaneers’ 27-21 win against the [[2018 Philadelphia Eagles season|Philadelphia Eagles]] with five combined tackles. In a Week 3, Stewart collected a season-high nine solo tackles and broke up a pass attempt during a 30-27 loss against the [[2018 Pittsburgh Steelers season|Pittsburgh Steelers]]. Stewart was inactive for five games (Weeks 9-13) after injuring his foot.<ref>{{cite web |title=Buccaneers' M.J. Stewart: Ruled out Week 12 |url=https://www.cbssports.com/fantasy/football/news/buccaneers-m-j-stewart-ruled-out-week-12/ |access-date=January 1, 2019 |website=CBSSports.com |date=November 23, 2018}}</ref> Stewart lost his role as a starting cornerback to Ryan Smith during his absence and remained a backup for the last four games of the season. Stewart finished his rookie season in [[2018 NFL season|2018]] with 33 combined tackles (31 solo) and three pass deflections in 11 games and five starts.<ref>{{cite web |title=NFL Player stats: M.J. Stewart (career) |url=http://www.nfl.com/player/m.j.stewart/2560884/careerstats |access-date=January 1, 2019 |website=NFL.com}}</ref>

On August 6, 2020, Stewart was waived by the Buccaneers.<ref>{{cite web|title=Raymond Calais' Return Among Bucs' Roster Moves|url=https://www.buccaneers.com/news/raymond-calais-roster-moves-mj-stewart-training-camp|author=Smith, Scott|website=Buccaneers.com|date=August 6, 2020}}</ref>

===Cleveland Browns===
Stewart was claimed off waivers by the [[Cleveland Browns]] on August 8, 2020.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Gribble |first1=Andrew |title=Browns claim CB M.J. Stewart via waivers, make other roster moves |url=https://www.clevelandbrowns.com/news/browns-claim-cb-m-j-stewart-via-waivers-make-other-roster-moves |publisher=ClevelandBrowns.com |date=August 8, 2020}}</ref>

In Week 4 against the [[2020 Dallas Cowboys season|Dallas Cowboys]], Stewart recorded his first career sack on [[Dak Prescott]] during the 49–38 win. <ref>{{Cite web |title=Cleveland Browns at Dallas Cowboys - October 4th, 2020 |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/202010040dal.htm |access-date=2020-10-06 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref>
