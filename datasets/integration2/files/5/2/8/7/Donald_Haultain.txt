'''Donald Haultain''' was an [[Anglican]] [[priest]] in the first half of the twentieth century.<ref>"Harvest of Grace: Essays in Celebration of 150 Years of Mission in the Anglican Diocese of Nelson" Bester, R (Ed) p118: [[Nelson, New Zealand]]; [[Anglican Diocese of Nelson]]; 2010 {{ISBN|9780473177775}}</ref>

Haultain was educated at [[Moore Theological College]]; and [[ordained]] [[deacon]] in 1914, and [[priest]] in 1915.<ref>[[Crockford's Clerical Directory]] 1948 p 579: [[London]], [[Oxford University Press|OUP]], 1948</ref> After a [[Curate|curacy]] at [[Wahroonga]] he was [[Rector (ecclesiastical)|Rector]] of [[Sale, Victoria]]. He was Vicar of  [[Kyneton]] from 1924 to 1928; [[Dean (religion)|Dean]] of [[Anglican Diocese of Bendigo|Bendigo]]<ref>[http://www.stpaulsbendigo.org.au/ Cathedral web-site]</ref> from  1928 until 1932; Vicar of All Saints, [[Nelson, New Zealand|Nelson]] from 1932 to 1939; and [[Archdeacon]] of [[Marlborough, Queensland|Marlborough]] from 1940 to 1948.
