'''Timothy Edward Sullivan Bateman''' (born 3 June 1987) is a [[rugby union]] player from [[New Zealand]]. He plays mostly [[Inside Centre]].

He was head boy of [[Christchurch Boys' High School]] in Canterbury, New Zealand. He formerly played for the [[Crusaders (rugby union)|Crusaders]] and played in Japan for the [[Coca-Cola Red Sparks]]. He also had a stint at the [[Hurricanes (rugby union)|Hurricanes]] in [[Super Rugby]]. In 2017, he has returned to play for the Crusaders.

Of [[Ngāi Tahu]] descent, Bateman played for the [[Māori All Blacks]] in 2008 and 2012.<ref name="NZM2008">[https://www.allblacks.com/index.cfm?layout=teamMaori2008 New Zealand Maori 2008 squad]. Retrieved 1 June 2013.</ref><ref name="MAB2012">[http://www.allblacks.com/news/20929/Maori-All-Blacks-squad-for-2012-European-tour Maori All Blacks squad for 2012 European tour]. Retrieved 1 June 2013.</ref> He has also represented New Zealand at Under 19 and Under 21 level.<ref>[https://www.allblacks.com/index.cfm?layout=MaoriAllBlacksPlayerProfile&playerID=1392 Tim Bateman]. Maori All Blacks player profile. Retrieved 1 June 2013.</ref>

==Playing career==

===School boy rugby===
While at [[Christchurch Boys' High School]], Bateman appeared alongside All Blacks [[Colin Slade]], [[Matt Todd]] and [[Owen Franks]] as well as former Crusader [[Nasi Manu]].<ref>http://www.thesportreview.com/tsr/2015/07/edinburgh-new-signing-nasi-manu-open-to-scotland-call/</ref>

===Later career===
After spending a few years playing rugby in Japan, Bateman returned home and signed with his former club the {{Rut Crusaders}} for the  [[2017 Super Rugby season|2017]]. He played six games and scored three tries in his return season. Bateman was a part of the 2017  {{Rut Crusaders}} championship winning side in 2017.

Bateman also signed with former provincial team [[Canterbury Rugby Football Union|Canterbury]] ahead of the [[2017 Mitre 10 Cup]] season.

He scored a try in Canterbury's 35-13 Grand Final win against Tasman.
