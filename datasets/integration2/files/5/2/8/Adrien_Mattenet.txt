'''Adrien Mattenet''' (born October 15, 1987) is a [[France|French]] [[table tennis]] player, currently playing for [[AS Pontoise-Cergy TT]].<ref name="ITTF biography">{{cite web |url=http://www.ittf.com/biography/biography_web_details.asp?Player_ID=105937 |title=MATTENET Adrien |publisher=ITTF |accessdate=January 16, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304230411/http://www.ittf.com/biography/biography_web_details.asp?Player_ID=105937 |archive-date=2016-03-04 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ittf.com/ittf_stats/All_events3.asp?ID=10302 |title=MATTENET Adrien (FRA) |publisher=ITTF |accessdate=January 16, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303235848/http://www.ittf.com/ittf_stats/All_events3.asp?ID=10302 |archive-date=2016-03-03 |url-status=dead }}</ref> His World Ranking is at number 19 as of December 2011, the best ranking for a French player since [[Christophe Legout]] in 2001.<ref name="ITTF ranking">{{cite web |url=http://www.ittf.com/ittf_ranking/world_ranking_per_name.asp?Siniors=1&Player_ID=105937 |title=World ranking Record for MATTENET Adrien (FRA) |publisher=ITTF |accessdate=December 9, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.lequipe.fr/Aussi/breves2011/20111201_171945_mattenet-dans-le-top-20.html |title=Mattenet dans le Top 20 |date=December 1, 2011 |work=L'Equipe.fr |accessdate=January 16, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120103110633/http://www.lequipe.fr/Aussi/breves2011/20111201_171945_mattenet-dans-le-top-20.html |archive-date=2012-01-03 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In May 2011, Mattenet was the first Frenchman to qualify for the [[London 2012 Olympic Games]] by beating [[Singapore]]'s [[Gao Ning]] to reach the Quarterfinals of the [[World Table Tennis Championships|2011 World Championships]]. At the 2012 Olympics, he lost to [[Chen Weixing]] in the fourth round.<ref>{{Cite Sports-Reference |url=https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/ma/adrien-mattenet-1.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200417194425/https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/ma/adrien-mattenet-1.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=2020-04-17 |title=Adrien Mattenet |access-date=2017-05-06}}</ref>  In June 2015, he competed in the [[European Games 2015|inaugural European Games]], for [[France at the 2015 European Games|France]] in [[Table tennis at the 2015 European Games|table tennis]], more specifically, Men's team with [[Simon Gauzy]] and [[Emmanuel Lebesson]]. He earned a silver medal.
