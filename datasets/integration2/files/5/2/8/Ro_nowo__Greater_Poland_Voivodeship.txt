'''Rożnowo''' {{IPAc-pl|r|o|ż|'|n|o|w|o}} ({{lang-de|Ruschdorf}}) is a [[village]] in the administrative district of [[Gmina Oborniki]], within [[Oborniki County]], [[Greater Poland Voivodeship]], in west-central Poland.<ref name="TERYT">{{cite web |url=http://www.stat.gov.pl/broker/access/prefile/listPreFiles.jspa |title=Central Statistical Office (GUS) - TERYT (National Register of Territorial Land Apportionment Journal) |date=2008-06-01 |language=Polish}}</ref> It lies approximately {{convert|5|km|mi|0}} north-east of [[Oborniki]] and {{convert|32|km|mi|0|abbr=on}} north of the regional capital [[Poznań]].

Rożnowo is best known as the birthplace of [[Włodzimierz Krzyżanowski]], a military leader and brigade commander in the Union Army during the [[American Civil War]]. He was a first cousin to composer [[Frédéric Chopin]].

The village has a population of 1,500.
