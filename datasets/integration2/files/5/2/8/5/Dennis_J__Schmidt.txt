'''Dennis Joseph Schmidt''' is an [[American people|American]] [[philosopher]] living in [[Sydney, Australia]] where he is Research Professor and Chair at [[Western Sydney University]]. Prior to moving to Sydney in 2015, he taught at [[Binghamton University]] (1982-1994), [[Villanova University]] (1994-2003), and [[Penn State University]] (2003-2015).  He is known for his research on [[ancient Greek philosophy]] and [[Ancient_Greek_literature|literature]], [[post-Kantian philosophy]], [[hermeneutics]] and [[philosophy of art]].<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=RWFBdG7YPDUC&pg=PA66&lpg=PA66&dq=dennis+schmidt+philosophy&source=bl&ots=R3ai7rKyJJ&sig=8abMsoHfCtAQ8MgeWrdTxH4UGhk&hl=en&sa=X&sqi=2&ved=0ahUKEwiFtO7PkOjMAhWBI8AKHdoVARUQ6AEIWDAJ#v=onepage&q=dennis%20schmidt%20philosophy&f=false Stereoscopic Thinking]</ref><ref>[http://www.encyclopedia.com/article-1G2-3446800832/hermeneutics.html Hermeneutics]</ref><ref>[http://scholarship.haverford.edu/philosophy_facpubs/183/ Review of Dennis J. Schmidt's Between Word and Image: Heidegger, Klee, and Gadamer on Gesture and Genesis], by [[Kathleen Wright]]</ref><ref>[http://www.sunypress.edu/p-5060-being-and-time.aspx Being and Time]</ref>
He received his [[PhD]] in [[philosophy]] from the [[Boston College]] in 1982.<ref>[http://www85.homepage.villanova.edu/dennis.schmidt/ About Dennis Schmidt]</ref>

Dennis Schmidt lives with his wife, Jennifer Mensch (who also teaches at Western Sydney University and is the author or 'Kant's Organisim') and his daughter Zoe Schmidt.

Schmidt is the editor of "[[SUNY]] Press Series in Contemporary Continental Philosophy" which has published a number of important works in the contemporary Continental tradition.  In addition to his own publications Schmidt has substantially revised and edited [[Joan Stambaugh]]'s 1995 translation of [[Martin Heidegger]]'s ''[[Being and Time]]'' (1927).
