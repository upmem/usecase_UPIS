'''Shaikh Abdul Majeed Sindhi''' ({{lang-sd|'''شيخ عبدالمجيد سنڌي'''}}) (7 July 1889 &ndash; 24 May 1978) was born in a [[Hindu]] family in [[Thatta]] who later converted to Islam. He was a famous writer, politician, journalist of [[Sindh]]. He died on 24 May 1978.<ref name=Dawn/><ref name=sindhiadabiboard/>

==Education==
Shaikh Abdul Majeed Sindhi matriculated (completed 10th Grade in school) (Sindhi: '''ست درجه فائنل''') from [[Thatta]], Sindh in [[British India]].

==Conversion to Islam==
Since childhood he was in the search of [[mysticism|mystic]] approach and in this regard on 10 February 1908, he became a [[Muslim]] on the hands of Shaikh Abdul Rahim at [[Hyderabad, Sindh]].<ref name=sindhiadabiboard>[http://www.sindhiadabiboard.org/catalogue/Personalties/Book10/Book_page9.html Profile of Shaikh Abdul Majeed Sindhi on Sindh Adabi Board website (in Sindhi language)] Retrieved 6 March 2019</ref>

==Professional career==
Shaikh Abdul Majeed Sindhi became Munshi ({{lang-sd|'''منشي '''}}) at a senior advocate Mr. Deechand Ojha. Afterwards he lived at Hyderabad where Rais [[Ghulam Muhammad Khan Bhurgri]] made him editor of his own newspaper Al Amen {{lang-sd|'''الامين '''}}. Having been inspired from his intellect Bhurgri made him his political adviser.<ref>http://sindhipeoples.blogspot.com/2011/09/blog-post_9388.html</ref>

==Political career==
He was an active participant of [[Silk Letter Movement]] (Reshmi Rumal Tahrik) ({{lang-sd|'''ريشمي رومال تحريڪ '''}}), by [[Ubaidullah Sindhi|Molana Ubeduallah Sindhi]], in which he was imprisoned in 1919 for three years. Later, he addressed a campaign against the [[British Raj]] in a procession in 1920 at [[Larkana]] and was again imprisoned for two years. In 1924, he became editor of Daily Al Waheed, Sindh's only daily newspaper at the time.<ref name=Dawn/> Through this platform, he incited the Muslim population. On 16 November 1929, he participated in [[All-India Muslim League]] session at [[Allahabad]], [[Khilafat Movement|All India Khilafat Conference]] at [[Ajmer]],<ref>[http://ils.smiu.edu.pk/cgi-bin/koha/opac-detail.pl?biblionumber=1821&query_desc=Provider%3ARoyal%20Book Shaikh Abdul Majeed Sindhi life and achievement on Sindh Madressatul Islam University website] Retrieved 6 March 2019</ref> All Parties Muslim Unity Conference and Azad Sindh Conference at [[Karachi]] (1930). His efforts for the separation of Sindh from [[Mumbai|Bombay]] were to be cherished. Sindh was separated from Bombay in 1936,<ref name=Dawn/><ref>http://www.sindhsalamat.com.previewdns.com/threads/35291/</ref> he took part in the first election of [[Provincial Assembly of Sindh|Sindh Assembly]] (1937)<ref>[http://awamiawaz.com/22247/ ] Awami Awaz (Peoples' Voice) website, Retrieved 6 March 2019</ref> from his own party and defeating [[Shah Nawaz Bhutto|Sir Shahnawaz]], he became member of the Sindh assembly.<ref name=Dawn/> In 1940, under the ministry of Mir Bunda Ali Khan, he was made a Minister in Sindh.<ref>http://www.pjlss.edu.pk/pdf_files/2005_1%20&%202/PJLSS_2005_33-36.pdf</ref> In 1943, he left [[All-India Muslim League|Muslim League]] and joined All Pakistan [[Awami Tahreek]] which was lead by [[Khan Abdul Ghaffar Khan]] in 1949.<ref>Book: Legends of Modern Sindh, Written by: Prof: Hassan Bux Noonari, Published by: Roshni Publications</ref><ref name=Dawn>{{cite web|url=http://www.dawn.com/news/1201924 |title=Shaikh Abdul Majeed Sindhi: Sindh's first people's politician|publisher=Dawn (newspaper)|author=Akhtar Balouch|date=21 August 2015|accessdate=6 March 2019}}</ref>

==Death==
Shaikh Abdul Majeed Sindhi died 24 May 1978 at Hyderabad, Sindh and was buried at historical Graveyard [[Makli Hill|Makli]] near the grave of famous writer Makhdoom Mohammad Hashim Thatvi according to his [[Will and testament|will]].
