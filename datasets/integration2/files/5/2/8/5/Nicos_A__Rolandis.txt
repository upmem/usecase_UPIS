'''Nicos A. Rolandis''' is a [[Greeks|Greek]] [[Cyprus|Cypriot]] politician. He has variously served as a [[Member of parliament]], the [[Minister for Foreign Affairs (Greece)|Minister for Foreign Affairs]], and the Minister of Trade, Industry, and Tourism.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=2019-07-04|title=Wayback Machine|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190704231206/http://www.parliament.cy/images/media/assetfile/VIVLIO%2050HRONA.pdf|access-date=2020-09-25|website=web.archive.org}}</ref>  

== Biography ==
Rolandis was born in [[Limassol]] on December 10, 1934.<ref name=":0" /> He studied law at the [[Middle Temple]] in London, and practiced industrial law before entering politics.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|title=Curriculum Vitae|url=http://rolandis.com/#/biography_en|accessdate=2016-05-20|website=Rolandis.com}}</ref> He is married with three children.<ref name=":1" />

==Political career==
In 1976, Rolandis was one of the founding members of the [[Democratic Party (Cyprus)|Democratic Party]] in Cyprus. In March of 1978, he was appointed the Minister of Foreign Affairs by [[Spyros Kyprianou]]. He maintained the position until a disagreement with Kyprianou over the [[Cyprus dispute]] led to his resignation in 1983.<ref name=":1" />

In September 1986, he founded the Liberal Party and led the party until 1998 when it merged with the [[Democratic Rally]]. He was elected as a Member of Parliament from [[Nicosia]] in 1991 and stayed in that role until 1996.<ref name=":1" />

He was a candidate in the [[1998 Cypriot presidential election]]. In the first round, he won 0.78% of the vote, and in the second round he supported [[Glafcos Clerides]], who was eventually elected.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Οι μάχες του β’ γύρου των Προεδρικών|url=https://www.philenews.com/eidiseis/politiki/article/485732/oi-maches-toy-b-groy-ton-proedrikon|access-date=2020-09-25|website=Philenews|language=el}}</ref> Subsequently, Rolandis was appointed to the Minister of Trade, Industry, and Tourism post, in which he remained until the end of Clerides' term.
