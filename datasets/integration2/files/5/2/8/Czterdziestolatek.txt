'''Czterdziestolatek''' or '''40-latek''' (the English translation: A Forty-Year-Old); broadcast in English  by [[TVP Polonia]] under the title of "Being Forty" is a [[Poland|Polish]] television [[comedy]] series originally  broadcast in Poland between 1975-78. The initial series enjoyed so much popularity that it was continued and led to the release of a feature film ''I'm a Butterfly, a 40-year-old's Love Affair'' and a New Year's Eve television program in 1975.

The series was written by Jerzy Gruza and Krzysztof Teodor Toeplitz and was broadcast on [[Telewizja Polska]]. In total, 21 episodes were produced.<ref>{{cite book|author=Haltof, M.|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=aHufS1XmIk4C&pg=PA148&dq=Czterdziestolatek#v=onepage&q=Czterdziestolatek|title=Polish National Cinema|publisher=[[Berghahn Books]]|pages=148|isbn=978-1-57181-275-9|year=2002}}</ref>

The series followed the fate of a [[Warsaw]] family and explored topics related to [[midlife crisis]], such as extra-marital affairs, attempts to quit smoking, obsession with hair loss, efforts to maintain physical fitness, pride in achievements and professional life, the desire to seek self-fulfillment through social activities, etc.

20 years later the series was remade in the mid-1990s with a cast that included Joanna Kurowska, Wojciech Mann and Wojciech Malajkat.
