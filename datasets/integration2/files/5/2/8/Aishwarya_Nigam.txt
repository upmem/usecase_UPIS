'''Aishwarya Nigam''' (born 4 July 1989) is an Indian singer.<ref>{{cite web|title=Meet Aishwarya Nigam, a Top Bollywood Singer from Bihar|url=http://news.biharprabha.com/2013/08/meet-aishwarya-nigam-a-top-bollywood-singer-from-bihar/|accessdate=5 September 2013}}</ref> He is best known as a playback singer in [[Hindi]] films, particularly for films like ''[[Dabangg]]''. He has received several awards for the song "[[Munni Badnaam Hui]]" from ''[[Dabangg]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Struggle is a part of everyone's journey-Aishwarya|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/hindi/music/news/Struggle-is-a-part-of-everyones-journey-Aishwarya-Nigam/articleshow/21741975.cms}}</ref>

== Early and family life ==
He was born in [[Muzaffarpur]], Bihar. His mother Arti Ranjan works as lecturer in Mukherjee Seminary, Muzaffarpur while his father Mukesh Ranjan is a manager in [[Punjab National Bank]].<ref>[http://india.gov.in/myindia/padmashri_awards_list1.php?start=80 Padma Shri awardees Government of India website]</ref> He has done his schooling from Sun Shine Prep High School, Muzaffarpur.<ref>{{cite web|title=Aishwarya Nigam – A true Sonu Nigam fan|url=http://www.pardaphash.com/news/interview-aishwarya-nigam-a-true-sonu-nigam-fan/760126.html|access-date=12 January 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150201191139/http://www.pardaphash.com/news/interview-aishwarya-nigam-a-true-sonu-nigam-fan/760126.html#.VLOxByuUezw|archive-date=1 February 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Nigam has lived his childhood in New Colony Sherpur, Muzaffarpur, Bihar.

== Career ==
Aishwarya Nigam has participated in the Music competition ''[[Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Ek Main Aur Ek Tu]]'' 2006 for [[Zee TV]]. Nigam and [[Ujjaini Mukherjee]] were declared the winners on 24 June 2006.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jodi No 1|url=http://www.sify.com/ekmainaurektu/index.php|access-date=12 January 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924101723/http://www.sify.com/ekmainaurektu/index.php|archive-date=24 September 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> Nigam was one of the contestants of ''[[Jo Jeeta Wohi Superstar]]'', a singing competition on [[Star Plus]] channel. He represented the Champions team. He was eliminated on 25 April 2008. Aishwarya was one of the contestants of music competition show [[Indian Premier League|IPL]] Rockstar on [[Colors (TV channel)|Colors]] TV channel. He was among the top three finalists of IPL Rockstar. He is much inspired from Sonu Nigam: that made him keep title as "Nigam".<ref>{{cite web|title=Aishwarya Nigam goes the romantic way|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/screen/aishwarya-nigam-goes-the-romantic-way/}}</ref>

He sang the title track of ''[[Kitani Mohabbat Hai]]'' for seasons I and II, a daily soap on [[NDTV]] imagine. He has worked with renowned music directors like Lalit Pandit, Anu Malik, Pritam, Sajid-Wajid, Shameer Tandon. He sang a song for the serial ''[[Dill Mill Gayye]]''.

Aishwarya is fondly known for singing the famous item song "[[Munni Badnaam Hui]]" from Salman Khan starer ''[[Dabangg]]''. He has sung "Maara re Sixer Maara Re Four" from the movie ''[[Ferrari Ki Sawaari]]'', a movie by Vidhu Vinod Chopra. The Hit Item number "Tere Mohalle" from the movie "Besharam" starring ''[[Ranbir Kapoor]]'' is also sung by Aishwarya Nigam.
