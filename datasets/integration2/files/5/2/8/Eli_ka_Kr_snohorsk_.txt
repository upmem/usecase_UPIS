'''Eliška Krásnohorská''' (18 November 1847 in [[Prague]] – 26 November 1926 in Prague) was a [[Czech people|Czech]] [[feminist]] author. She was introduced to literature and [[feminism]] by [[Karolína Světlá]]. She wrote works of lyric poetry and literary criticism, however, she is usually associated with children's literature and translations, including works by [[Pushkin]], [[Adam Mickiewicz|Mickiewicz]] and [[Byron]].<ref name="bio">{{cite book|title=A Biographical Dictionary of Women's Movements and Feminisms |last1=de Haan |first1=Francisca |first2=Krasimira |last2=Daskalova |first3=Anna |last3=Loutfi|year= 2006|publisher=[[Central European University Press]] |isbn = 963-7326-39-1|url= https://books.google.com/books?id=hsgQjbgBOAkC&q=%22Eliska+Krasnohorska%22&pg=PA262 |pages=262–65 |accessdate=28 March 2016}}</ref>

Krásnohorská wrote the libretti for four operas by [[Bedřich Smetana]]: ''[[The Kiss (opera)|The Kiss]]'', ''[[The Secret (opera)|The Secret]]'', ''[[The Devil's Wall]]'' and [[Viola (opera)|''Viola'']]. She also wrote the libretto for [[Zdeněk Fibich]]'s opera ''Blaník (Fibich)''.

In 1890 Krásnohorská founded the [[Minerva School]] in Prague, the first ''[[Gymnasium (school)|gymnasium]]'' for girls in the [[Austro-Hungarian Empire]]. Its language of instruction was [[Czech language|Czech]].<ref name="bohemia">{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_yrxrO0lC0YC&q=minerva+school+prague&pg=PA90 |title=The Coasts of Bohemia: A Czech History |page=90 |last=Sayer |first=Derek |isbn=069105052X |date=2000 |publisher=[[Princeton University Press]] |accessdate=28 March 2016}}</ref><ref name="bio"/>
