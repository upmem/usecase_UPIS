'''Evangelical Lutheran Church in Oldenburg''' ({{lang-de|Evangelisch-Lutherische Kirche in Oldenburg}}) is a [[Lutheran]] church in the [[Germany|German]] state of [[Lower Saxony]].
[[Image:EKD Oldenburg.svg|200px|right|Evangelical Lutheran Church in Oldenburg]]
The seat of the church leaders is in [[Oldenburg (city)|Oldenburg]], as is the preaching venue of its bishop at [[St Lamberti Church]]. The Evangelical Lutheran Church in Oldenburg is a [[Landeskirche|regional church]] (German: Landeskirche) and a full member of the [[Evangelical Church in Germany]] (EKD). As one of just two regional churches in the EKD, the church is only a guest member of the [[United Evangelical Lutheran Church of Germany]] (VELKD) and the [[UEK|Union of Evangelical Churches]] (UEK). The church is also a full member of the [[Community of Protestant Churches in Europe]] and the [[Lutheran World Federation]]. The church has 405,253 members (2018)<ref>[https://www.ekd.de/ekd_de/ds_doc/Ber_Kirchenmitglieder_2018.pdf Evangelische Kirche in Deutschland – Kirchemitgliederzahlen Stand 31. Dezember 2018] EKD, January 2020</ref> in 123 parishes, with approximately 260 pastors (men and [[ordination of women|women]]). It is the largest [[Protestant]] denomination in the area of the former [[Free State of Oldenburg|state of Oldenburg]].

== History ==
The [[Protestant Reformation|Lutheran Reformation]] came to the [[Duchy of Oldenburg|County of Oldenburg]] beginning in 1527.<ref>Cnf. Hermann Hamelmann, Oldenburgisch Chronicon, Oldenburg 1599, p. 363.</ref> Until the [[German Revolution]] in 1918, the church was a [[state church]] and the [[monarch]] was the acting bishop (summus episcopus, or supreme governor) of the church. In 1922 the Church in Oldenburg counted 291,000 parishioners.<ref name="Müller-Rolli 1999 30">Sebastian Müller-Rolli in collaboration with Reiner Anselm, ''Evangelische Schulpolitik in Deutschland 1918–1958: Dokumente und Darstellung'', Göttingen: Vandenhoeck & Ruprecht, 1999, (=Eine Veröffentlichung des Comenius-Instituts Münster), p. 30. {{ISBN|3-525-61362-8}}.</ref>

== Practices ==
[[Ordination of women]] and [[blessing of same-sex unions|blessing of same-sex marriages]] were allowed.<ref>[https://www.ndr.de/nachrichten/niedersachsen/oldenburg_ostfriesland/Oldenburgische-Kirche-beschliesst-Trauung-fuer-alle,synode290.html NDR.de: Oldenburgische Kirche beschließt Trauuung für alle], 2018 (German)</ref><ref>[http://www.ev-kirche-oldenburg.com/kirche_struktur/themen/Synode/f5.%20Tagung%20der%2046.%20Synode.asp Synod of Evangelical Lutheran Church in Oldenburg] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927212733/http://www.ev-kirche-oldenburg.com/kirche_struktur/themen/Synode/f5.%20Tagung%20der%2046.%20Synode.asp |date=September 27, 2007 }}</ref>

== Leadership of the church ==
The Evangelical Lutheran Church in Oldenburg has four leading authorities: the [[synod]], the [[bishop]], the superior church council (Oberkirchenrat), and the common church committee.

=== Synod ===
The synod is the highest leading authority in the Church. The election of the 60 members (two-thirds laypersons and one-third clerics) of the synod is for six years.
