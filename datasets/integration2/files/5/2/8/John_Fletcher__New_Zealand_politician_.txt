'''John Shearer Fletcher''' (22 December 1888 – 15 February 1934) was a New Zealand member of the [[New Zealand House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] for {{NZ electorate link|Grey Lynn}} in [[Auckland]]. Born in Scotland, he came to New Zealand in 1916 to join his brothers in their construction business, out of which grew [[Fletcher Construction]].

==Early life==
Fletcher was born at [[Kirkintilloch]], Scotland,{{sfn|Scholefield|1940|p=267}} the son of a builder, John Shearer Fletcher, and his wife, Janet Montgomery.<ref name="DNZB James Fletcher">{{DNZB|Parker|Selwyn James|4f17|Fletcher, James|31 October 2014}}</ref> He received his entire education through scholarships.<ref name="EP obit">{{cite news |title=Obituary |url= https://paperspast.natlib.govt.nz/cgi-bin/paperspast?a=d&cl=search&d=EP19340215.2.92 |access-date=31 October 2014 |work=[[The Evening Post (New Zealand)|The Evening Post]] |volume=CXVII |issue=39 |date=15 February 1934 |page=12}}</ref> He was one of thirteen children.<ref name="bus_hist">{{cite web |title=University of Auckland Business History Project - Fletcher Challenge |url= http://www.businesshistory.auckland.ac.nz/fletchers/founders.html |publisher=[[University of Auckland]] |access-date=1 November 2014}}</ref>

By age 24, he was headmaster under the Kirkintilloch school board for technical and commercial classes. He then moved to [[Glasgow]]<ref name="Herald obit" /> and became secretary for the Scottish Class Teachers' Association, and was on the executive of the Scottish Education Institute.{{sfn|Scholefield|1940|p=267}}<ref name="EP obit" /> He was vice-president of the [[YMCA|Young Men's Christian Association]] (YMCA).{{sfn|Scholefield|1940|p=267}} He undertook studies in construction in the United States and in Canada.<ref name="Herald obit">{{cite news |title=Obituary |url= https://paperspast.natlib.govt.nz/cgi-bin/paperspast?a=d&cl=search&d=NZH19340216.2.166 |access-date=31 October 2014 |work=[[The New Zealand Herald]] |volume=LXXI |issue=21727 |date=16 February 1934 |page=14}}</ref>

==Professional career in New Zealand==
In 1916, Fletcher migrated to [[Dunedin]], New Zealand, to join his brothers in their construction business.{{sfn|Scholefield|1940|p=267}} The company had been founded in 1909 after his brother [[James Fletcher (industrialist)|James]]{{sfn|Hunt|2000|p=174}} had come to Dunedin in the previous year.<ref name="DNZB James Fletcher" /> His brother James started to concentrate on business in [[Auckland]] and [[Wellington]], and John Shearer Fletcher took over the Dunedin branch.{{sfn|Scholefield|1940|p=267}}<ref name="DNZB James Fletcher" /> Fletcher was an executive member of the Dunedin Manufacturers' Association.<ref name="AS obit">{{cite news |title=Mr J Fletcher Dead |url= https://paperspast.natlib.govt.nz/cgi-bin/paperspast?a=d&cl=search&d=AS19340215.2.87 |access-date=31 October 2014 |work=[[Auckland Star]] |volume=LXV |issue=39 |date=15 February 1934 |page=8}}</ref> Of poor health, he retired from business during the 1920s and moved to Auckland.<ref name="Herald obit" />

John Fletcher represented the {{NZ electorate link|Grey Lynn}} electorate in the [[New Zealand House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] from {{NZ election link year|1928}} to 1931.{{sfn|Wilson|1985|p=197}} In the 1928 election, Fletcher defeated [[Fred Bartram]] for Grey Lynn.{{sfn|Wilson|1985|pp=182, 197}}{{sfn|Habershon|1958|loc=Appendix p.vi}} After having threatened his government in September 1929 to cross the house over unemployment and economic issues,<ref>{{cite news |title=Indignant Supporter |url= https://paperspast.natlib.govt.nz/cgi-bin/paperspast?a=d&cl=search&d=NZH19290912.2.125 |access-date=1 November 2014 |work=[[The New Zealand Herald]] |volume=LXVI |issue=20358 |date=12 September 1929 |page=13}}</ref> Fletcher became an [[Independent politician|Independent]] during 1930.<ref name="1930 independent">{{cite news |title=State of Parties |url= https://paperspast.natlib.govt.nz/cgi-bin/paperspast?a=d&cl=search&d=AS19310107.2.17 |access-date=31 October 2014 |work=[[Auckland Star]] |volume=LXII |issue=5 |date=7 January 1931 |page=3}}</ref>{{sfn|Habershon|1958|pages=30, 33 & 45}} In the {{NZ election link|1931}}, he was defeated by [[John A. Lee]] of the Labour Party.{{sfn|Habershon|1958|loc=Appendix p.vi}}{{sfn|Wood|1996|p=90}}{{sfn|Bassett|1982|pp=39, 62}}

==Private life and death==
Fletcher married Alice Emily Murphy in 1919.<ref>{{cite web |title=Marriage Search |url= https://www.bdmhistoricalrecords.dia.govt.nz/Search/Search.aspx?Path=%2FqueryEntry.m%3Ftype%3Dmarriages |publisher=[[Department of Internal Affairs (New Zealand)|Department of Internal Affairs]] |access-date=1 November 2014 |quote=Search for registration number 1919/2175}}</ref> In Auckland, Fletcher participated in public life,<ref name="Herald obit" /> and he was active with the YMCA, other social movements,{{sfn|Scholefield|1940|p=267}} and belonged to the [[Rotary International|Rotary Club]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Auckland Rotary Club |url= https://paperspast.natlib.govt.nz/cgi-bin/paperspast?a=d&cl=search&d=AS19340219.2.82 |access-date=31 October 2014 |work=[[Auckland Star]] |volume=LXV |issue=42 |date=19 February 1934 |page=8}}</ref> He was interested in politics and economics, and involved in [[Association football in New Zealand|association football]], [[Cricket in New Zealand|cricket]], and [[Bowls|lawn bowls]].<ref name="AS obit" /> He died on 15 February 1934 and is buried at Hillsborough Cemetery in Auckland. His wife died on 28 June 1976.<ref name="transcribed inscription" />
