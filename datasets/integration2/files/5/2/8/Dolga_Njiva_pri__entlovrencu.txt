'''Dolga Njiva pri Šentlovrencu''' ({{IPA-sl|ˈdoːu̯ɡa ˈnjiːʋa pɾi ʃɛntˈloːu̯ɾɛntsu|pron}} or {{IPA-sl|- ʃənt-|pron}}) is a small [[village]] north of [[Šentlovrenc]] in the [[Municipality of Trebnje]] in eastern [[Slovenia]]. The area is part of the historical [[Lower Carniola]] region. The municipality is now included in the [[Southeast Slovenia Statistical Region]].<ref>[http://www.trebnje.si/ Trebnje municipal site]</ref>

==Name==
The name of the settlement was changed from ''Dolga Njiva'' to ''Dolga Njiva pri Šentlovrencu'' in 1953.<ref>''Spremembe naselij 1948–95''. 1996. Database. Ljubljana: Geografski inštitut ZRC SAZU, DZS.</ref>

==Church==
A small [[church (building)|church]] in the settlement is dedicated to the [[Feast of the Cross|Finding of the Holy Cross]] and belongs to the [[Parish]] of Šentlovrenc. It is a medieval building and contains fragments of 16th-century [[fresco]]s in the [[nave]].<ref>[http://rkd.situla.org/ Slovenian Ministry of Culture register of national heritage] reference number ešd 2469</ref>
