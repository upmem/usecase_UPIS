'''''The Future Now''''' is the seventh studio album by [[Peter Hammill]], released on Charisma Records in 1978. It was the first solo album Hammill released following the 1978 breakup of his band [[Van der Graaf Generator]], although he had released numerous solo albums while VdGG were active. The album contains twelve short songs, several in the [[new wave music|new wave]] style of VdGG's last studio album, ''[[The Quiet Zone/The Pleasure Dome]]''.

The album cover shows a set of photographs by [[Brian_Griffin_(photographer) |Brian Griffin]], portraying Peter Hammill with half of his moustache and beard shaven.

"If I Could" was re-worked for Hammill's 1984 album ''[[The Love Songs (Peter Hammill album)|The Love Songs]]''.
