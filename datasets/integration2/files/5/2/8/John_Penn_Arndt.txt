'''John Penn Arndt''' (November 25, 1780 &ndash; June 10, 1861) was an [[Americans|American]] merchant, pioneer, and legislator.

Born in [[Durham Township, Bucks County, Pennsylvania|Durham, Pennsylvania]], Arndt built [[Durham boat]]s with his family near [[Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania]]. He moved to [[Buffalo, New York]], and then to [[Mackinac Island]] in [[Michigan Territory]]. In 1824, Arndt moved to [[Green Bay, Wisconsin|Green Bay]], Michigan Territory and then opened a saw mill in present-day [[Oconto County, Wisconsin]] in 1827. Arndt was a merchant, fur trapper, owned a ferry, and was an inn keeper. Arndt served in the first Wisconsin Territorial Council 1836-1838, of the Wisconsin Territorial Legislature. He died in [[Green Bay, Wisconsin]].<ref>'The Story of the Arndts: the life, antecedents, and descedents of Bernhard Arndt,' John Stover Arndt and Warren Smedley Ely: Christopher Sower Company, 1922, Biographical Sketch of John Penn Arndt, pg. 182-188.</ref><ref>[http://www.wisconsinhistory.org/dictionary/index.asp?action=view&term_id=2903&keyword=arndt John Penn Arndt, Wisconsin Historical Society]</ref>
