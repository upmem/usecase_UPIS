'''Francis Arundale''' (9 August 1807 â€“ 9 September 1853)<ref>{{cite DNB|noicon=1|prescript=|wstitle=Arundale, Francis|volume=2}}</ref><ref>[http://victorian.lang.nagoya-u.ac.jp/victorianweb/history/letters/arun.html Francis Arundale] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110118074533/http://victorian.lang.nagoya-u.ac.jp/victorianweb/history/letters/arun.html |date=18 January 2011 }} (Victorian Web).</ref> was an English architectural [[Drafter|draughtsman]].

==Life==
Arundale was born in London. He studied under [[Augustus Charles Pugin|Augustus Pugin]], and accompanied him to [[Normandy]], making drawings for a description which Pugin published of the tour. In 1831 Arundale visited Egypt with [[Robert Hay (Egyptologist)|Mr. Hay]], and in 1833 he joined [[Frederick Catherwood]] and [[Joseph Bonomi the Younger]] on their tour to the [[Holy Land]], filling many portfolios with drawings of the interesting monuments and ruins of [[Palestine (region)|Palestine]]. He subsequently visited France and Italy, and spent several winters in Rome. He made drawings of objects of interest in the cities of Italy, in Greece, [[Sicily]], and [[Asia Minor]]. He executed a few oil paintings from his Eastern sketches.

Arundale died at [[Brighton]] in 1853.
