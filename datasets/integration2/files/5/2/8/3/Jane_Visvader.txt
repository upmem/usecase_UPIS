'''Jane Visvader''' {{post-nominals|country=AUS|FRS|FAA|FAHMS}} is a [[scientist]] specialising in [[breast cancer]] research who works for the [[Walter and Eliza Hall Institute]] in [[Melbourne]], [[Australia]].  She is the joint head of the Breast Cancer Laboratory with Geoff Lindeman.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wehi.edu.au/faculty_members/professor_jane_visvader |title=Professor Jane Visvader |publisher= |date= |accessdate=2014-08-14}}</ref> In 2008, Visvader was a joint recipient of the GlaxoSmithKline Award for Research Excellence<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wehi.edu.au/site/latest_news/drs_lindeman_and_visvader_recognised_for_outstanding_breast_cancer_research |title=Latest News - Walter and Eliza Hall Institute of Medical Research |accessdate=2014-08-14 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140814061801/http://www.wehi.edu.au/site/latest_news/drs_lindeman_and_visvader_recognised_for_outstanding_breast_cancer_research |archivedate=2014-08-14 }}</ref> for outstanding contribution to Breast Cancer research. Visvader was made a [[list of Fellows of the Australian Academy of Science|fellow of the Australian Academy of Science]] in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.science.org.au/fellowship-list?name=visvader&field_d_year_of_election_value%5Bmin%5D%5Byear%5D=1954&field_d_year_of_election_value%5Bmax%5D%5Byear%5D=2014 |title=Fellowship list - Australian Academy of Science |publisher=Science.org.au |date= |accessdate=2014-08-14}}</ref> Her most recent award is the 2014 Royal Society of Victoria Research Medal for Scientific Excellence in Biomedical & Health Sciences.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.royalsocietyvictoria.org.au/news/ |title=News &#124; Royal Society of Victoria &#124; Promoting Science and Science Education |publisher=Royalsocietyvictoria.org.au |date= |accessdate=2014-08-14}}</ref>

==Education ==
Visvader holds a PhD in [[Biochemistry]] from the [[University of Adelaide]] having studied structure and function of citrus exocortis viroid<ref>{{cite web|url=http://trove.nla.gov.au/work/3750664?q=+Visvader&c=book&versionId=21400027+49704861 |title=Structure and function of citrus exocortis viroid / by Jane Ellen Visvader. - Version details - Trove |publisher=Trove.nla.gov.au |date= 2006-12-28|accessdate=2014-08-14}}</ref> 

==Career and research==
After her PhD, she was a [[postdoctoral researcher]] at the Verma Laboratory ([[Salk Institute]], San Diego) and Adams Laboratory (Walter and Eliza Hall Institute (WEHI)).{{citation needed|date=July 2019}} She worked at the Children's Hospital/[[Harvard Medical School]] in [[Boston]] before returning to Victoria in 1997 to establish a Breast Cancer Laboratory at WEHI.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.royalsocietyvictoria.org.au/news/ |title=News &#124; Royal Society of Victoria &#124; Promoting Science and Science Education |publisher=Royalsocietyvictoria.org.au |date= |accessdate=2014-08-14}}</ref> She was awarded a fellowship of the Australian Academy of Health and Medical Sciences in October 2016.<ref name="aahms oct 2016">{{cite web|url=http://www.aahms.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/AAHMS-Press-Release_6-Oct-2016.pdf|title=AAHMS announces its second scientific meeting and induction of new fellows|publisher=Australian Academy of Health and Medical Sciences |accessdate=14 October 2016}}</ref>

Visvader has published work investigating the role of cells of origin in cancer<ref>{{Cite journal | doi=10.1038/nature09781| pmid=21248838| title=Cells of origin in cancer| journal=Nature| volume=469| issue=7330| pages=314–322| year=2011| last1=Visvader| first1=Jane E.}}</ref> and in particular focuses on the role of stem cells,<ref>{{Cite journal | doi=10.1038/nature12948|pmid = 24463516| title=In situ identification of bipotent stem cells in the mammary gland| journal=Nature| volume=506| issue=7488| pages=322–327| year=2014| last1=Rios| first1=Anne C.| last2=Fu| first2=Nai Yang| last3=Lindeman| first3=Geoffrey J.| last4=Visvader| first4=Jane E.}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal | url=http://www.cell.com/cell-stem-cell/abstract/S1934-5909%2812%2900240-8 | doi=10.1016/j.stem.2012.05.007| pmid=22704512| title=Cancer Stem Cells: Current Status and Evolving Complexities| journal=Cell Stem Cell| volume=10| issue=6| pages=717–728| year=2012| last1=Visvader| first1=Jane E.| last2=Lindeman| first2=Geoffrey J.| doi-access=free}}</ref> which she believes may be a key to understanding breast cancer.<ref>{{Cite journal|author= Giovanni Valenti |url=http://www.eurostemcell.org/commentanalysis/interview-jane-visvader-stem-cells-breast |title=Interview with Jane Visvader: stem cells in the breast &#124; Europe's stem cell hub |journal=Nature |volume=439 |issue=7072 |pages=84–88 |doi=10.1038/nature04372 |pmid=16397499 |date=2012-01-11 |accessdate=2014-08-14}}</ref>

In 2020 she was elected a [[Fellow of the Royal Society]] (FRS).<ref> {{cite web|url=https://royalsociety.org/people/Jane-Visvader-25404/|title=Jane Visvader|publisher=Royal Society|accessdate= 20 September 2020}} </ref>
