'''Angela Savage''' (born 1966) is an Australian author.

== Biography ==
Savage was born in Melbourne and educated at [[Siena College (Camberwell)|Siena College]], Camberwell.<ref>{{cite web|title=Siena College 2010 Annual Report|url=http://www.siena.vic.edu.au/_uploads/rsfil/00344.pdf|access-date=2014-08-14|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150311184526/http://www.siena.vic.edu.au/_uploads/rsfil/00344.pdf|archive-date=2015-03-11|url-status=dead}}</ref> She graduated from the [[University of Melbourne]] in 1989 with a BA (Combined Honours) in Criminology and the History and Philosophy of Science. She has worked for the [[Australian Red Cross]] in Southeast Asia,<ref>{{cite web|title=About Angela (author's website)|url=http://angelasavage.wordpress.com/about-angela/}}</ref> 1993–1998; for Sexual Health & Family Planning Australia in the South Pacific, 2000–2002; for the Victorian Council of Social Service, 2002–2007; and for the Association of Neighbourhood Houses and Learning Centres (Melbourne, Victoria), 2009–2014.

==Career==
Savage won the [[Victorian Premier's Unpublished Manuscript Award]] in 2004 for ''Thai Died''.<ref>{{Cite news|url = |title = Premier's prize for a tale of cars and us|last = Steger|first = Jason|date = 19 October 2004|work = |accessdate = |newspaper = The Age|page = 7}}</ref> Her first novel, based on this manuscript (''Behind the Night Bazaar,'' Text Publishing, 2006), was shortlisted for the 2007 [[Ned Kelly Awards|Ned Kelly Award]] for Best First Fiction. Her second novel (''The Half-Child'', Text Publishing, 2010) was shortlisted for the 2011 Ned Kelly Award for Best Fiction.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nedkellyawards.com/index.php/short-list |title=The Ned Kelly Awards - 2011 Shortlist |date=17 November 2011 |accessdate=7 August 2014 |website=The Ned Kelly Awards |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111117010534/http://www.nedkellyawards.com/index.php/short-list |archivedate=November 17, 2011 }}</ref> In 2011 she won the Scarlet Stiletto prize for Australian women's short crime fiction, awarded by [[Sisters in Crime]] Australia, for 'The Teardrop Tattoos'.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sistersincrime.org.au/content/interview-angela-savage-winner-2011-scarlet-stiletto-awards |title=Interview with Angela Savage - winner of the 2011 Scarlet Stiletto Awards |date=6 December 2011 |accessdate=7 August 2014 |website=Sisters in Crime Australia |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140814061809/http://www.sistersincrime.org.au/content/interview-angela-savage-winner-2011-scarlet-stiletto-awards |archivedate=14 August 2014 }}</ref> Her third novel (''The Dying Beach'', Text Publishing, 2013) was shortlisted for the 2014 Ned Kelly Award for Best Fiction,<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.austcrimewriters.com/content/2014-ned-kelly-awards|title = 2014 - Ned Kelly Awards - Shortlists|date = |accessdate = 12 August 2014|website = Australian Crime Writers Association|publisher = |last = |first = |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20140814115856/http://www.austcrimewriters.com/content/2014-ned-kelly-awards|archive-date = 14 August 2014|url-status = dead}}</ref> and the Sisters in Crime Australia 14th Davitt Awards for Australian Crime Writing by Women (Best Adult Novel).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sistersincrime.org.au/content/shortlist-14th-davitt-awards-australian-women%E2%80%99s-crime-writing |title=Shortlist for 14th Davitt Awards for Australian Women’s Crime Writing |date=17 August 2014 |accessdate=18 August 2014 |website=Sisters in Crime Australia |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140819083717/http://www.sistersincrime.org.au/content/shortlist-14th-davitt-awards-australian-women%E2%80%99s-crime-writing |archivedate=19 August 2014 }}</ref> She contributed an essay to the collection, ''If I Tell You...I'll Have To Kill You: Australia's leading crime writers reveal their secrets'' (Michael Robotham ed., Allen & Unwin, 2013).

=== Books ===
* ''Behind the Night Bazaar,'' Text Publishing, 2006
* ''The Half-Child'', Text Publishing, 2010
* ''The Dying Beach'', Text Publishing, 2013
* ''Mother of Pearl'', [[Transit Lounge|Transit Lounge Publishing]], 2019

==Other==
[[Christos Tsiolkas]] dedicated his fifth novel, ''Barracuda'' (Allen & Unwin, 2013), to Savage.
