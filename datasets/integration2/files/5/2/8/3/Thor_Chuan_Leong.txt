'''Thor Chuan Leong''' ({{zh|s=涂振龙|t=涂振龍|p=Tú Zhènlóng}};<ref>{{cite news |url=http://sports.sina.com.cn/o/2014-05-05/13597150919.shtml |title=斯诺克亚锦赛落幕 马来西亚运动员时隔20年再夺冠 |work=Sina News |date=5 May 2014 |accessdate=14 November 2015}}</ref> born 24 March 1988) is a [[Malaysian people|Malaysian]] former professional [[snooker]] player. He is commonly referred to as '''Rory Thor'''.<ref name="wsprof">{{cite web |title=Thor Chuan Leong |url=http://www.worldsnooker.com/page/PlayersAlphabeticalArticle/0,,13165~3940765,00.html |publisher=World Snooker |accessdate=14 August 2014}}</ref>

==Career==
Thor, based in [[Penang]], Malaysia represented his country at the [[2006 Asian Games|2006]] & [[2010 Asian Games|2010]] [[Asian Games]] and in the [[2013 Asian Indoor and Martial Arts Games]] and [[2013 Southeast Asian Games]], in the Southeast Asian Games he won bronze in the snooker singles<ref>{{cite web |title=Snooker Single |date=18 December 2013 |publisher=[[Southeast Asian Games]] |url=http://data4.seagamesmm.com/pdf/8405.pdf |accessdate=14 August 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131219081822/http://data4.seagamesmm.com/pdf/8405.pdf |archivedate=19 December 2013 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> and doubles<ref>{{cite web |title=Snooker Double |date=13 December 2013 |publisher=[[Southeast Asian Games]] |url=http://data4.seagamesmm.com/pdf/8409.pdf |accessdate=14 August 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131219081730/http://data4.seagamesmm.com/pdf/8409.pdf |archivedate=19 December 2013 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> and gold in the six red snooker singles.<ref>{{cite web |title=6 Red Snooker Single |date=15 December 2013 |publisher=[[Southeast Asian Games]] |url=http://data4.seagamesmm.com/pdf/8413.pdf |accessdate=14 August 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131219081641/http://data4.seagamesmm.com/pdf/8413.pdf |archivedate=19 December 2013 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

In 2014, Thor won the ACBS Asian Snooker Championship in May, beating Taiwan's Hung Chuang Ming 7–3 in the final.<ref>{{cite web |title=30th Asian Snooker Championships 2014 |url=http://www.cuesportsindia.com/global/2014/acbs/snooker.htm |publisher=Cue Sports India |accessdate=14 August 2014}}</ref> This victory gained Thor a two-year card on the professional [[World Snooker Tour]] for the [[2014–15 snooker season|2014–15]] and 2015/2016 seasons.<ref name="WPBSA player list14">{{cite web |title=2014/15 List Of Tour Players |url=http://www.worldsnooker.com/page/NewsArticles/0,,13165~3898174,00.html |publisher=[[World Professional Billiards and Snooker Association]] |work=worldsnooker.com |accessdate=14 August 2014 |date=14 August 2014}}</ref> However, despite the tour starting in May, Thor did not start playing on the tour until February 2015.<ref>{{cite web |title=November Start For Malaysia's Thor? |url=http://www.prosnookerblog.com/2014/07/27/november-start-for-malaysias-thor/ |publisher=Pro Snooker Blog |date=27 July 2014 |accessdate=14 August 2014}}</ref> He played in the [[2014 Six-red World Championship|Six-red World Championship]], but lost all five of his group matches.<ref>{{cite web |title=SangSom 6 Red World Championship 2014 |work=World Snooker |publisher=[[World Professional Billiards and Snooker Association]] |url=http://www.worldsnooker.com/staticFiles/7/c3/0,,13165~180999,00.pdf |accessdate=14 August 2014}}</ref> In his first match in a ranking event qualifier he was beaten 1–4 by [[Dechawat Poomjaeng]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Thor Chuan Leong 2014/2015 |url=http://www.snooker.org/res/index.asp?season=2014&player=1509 |publisher=Snooker.org |accessdate=10 April 2015}}</ref> Although Thor lost all four of his matches this season he did push top 16 player [[Stuart Bingham]] to a deciding frame in the first round of the [[2015 Welsh Open (snooker)|Welsh Open]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.echo-news.co.uk/sport/11800713.Stuart_Bingham_wants_another_10_years_at_the_top_ahead_of_Welsh_Open/?ref=rss |title=Stuart Bingham wants another 10 years at the top as he begins Welsh Open campaign |publisher=Echo |accessdate=13 April 2015}}</ref>

Thor again failed to win a single match in ranking events during the [[2015–16 snooker season|2015–16 season]]; in contrast, he performed successfully in the European Tour events, winning all of his first-round matches and reaching last 16 twice, at the [[European Tour 2015/2016 – Event 2|Paul Hunter Classic]] and the [[European Tour 2015/2016 – Event 2|Gdynia Open]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Thor Chuan Leong 2015/2016 |url=http://www.snooker.org/res/index.asp?season=2015&player=1509 |publisher=Snooker.org |accessdate=14 April 2016}}</ref> As a result, Thor was able to secure his place on the Main Tour for two further seasons by finishing tied 31st on the [[Players Tour Championship 2015/2016#European Tour|Order of Merit]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.snooker.org/res/index.asp?template=10 |title=European Order of Merit 2015/2016 |publisher=Snooker.org |accessdate=16 April 2016}}</ref>

A 5–2 victory over [[Jack Lisowski]] saw Thor qualify for the [[2016 World Open (snooker)|2016 World Open]] and he reached the second round by beating [[Luca Brecel]] 5–3, before losing 1–5 to [[Neil Robertson]]. He also advanced to the last 32 of the [[2017 Gibraltar Open|Gibraltar Open]] with wins over Matthew Roberts and [[Gary Wilson (snooker player)|Gary Wilson]], but lost 3–4 to [[Igor Figueiredo]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Thor Chuan Leong 2016/2017|url=http://www.snooker.org/res/index.asp?player=1509&season=2016|publisher=Snooker.org|accessdate=11 April 2017}}</ref>
