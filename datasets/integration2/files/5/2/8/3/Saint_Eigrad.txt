'''Saint Eigrad''' was a sixth-century Welsh [[saint]]<ref>[http://www.catholic.org/saints/saint.php?saint_id=3071  St. Eigrad] at Catholic online.org.</ref> and the founder of the [[St Eugrad's Church, Llaneugrad|St Eugrad's Church in Llaneugrad]], [[Anglesey]].<ref>Nicholas Carlisle, A topographical dictionary of Wales, a continuation of the topography of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland (Nicholas Carlisle, 1811) [https://books.google.com/books?id=ATITAAAAQAAJ&pg=PT5&lpg=PT5&dq=Saint+Eigrad&source=bl&ots=6q0q0kV4Rm&sig=sChzFIDKEjCEMUSiVg9uKN4WKeg&hl=en&sa=X&ei=0f_rU-_BE4W78gXA2oIw&ved=0CCkQ6AEwBA#v=onepage&q=Saint%20Eigrad&f=false page].</ref>

==Life==
He was a son of king [[Caw of Strathclyde]], a brother of saints [[Gildas]],<ref>Lewis, Samuel (1849). "Llaneugrad (Llan-Eigrad)". A Topographical Dictionary of Wales.</ref> and [[Samson of Dol|Samson of York]] and was a student of Saint [[Illtud]].<ref>{{cite book|last1=Baring-Gould|authorlink=Sabine Baring-Gould|first1=Sabine|last2=Fisher|first2=John|title=The Lives of the British Saints|date=1908|publisher=[[Honourable Society of Cymmrodorion]]|page=468|volume=2|url=https://archive.org/details/livesofbritishsa02bariuoft#page/468/mode/1up}}<br/>This work is described at ''[[The Lives of the Saints (Baring-Gould)]]''.</ref>

In 605 AD<ref>[[Samuel Lewis (publisher)|Lewis, Samuel]] (1849). "Llaneugrad (Llan-Eigrad)". A Topographical Dictionary of Wales.</ref> he founded a church at Llaneugrad, Anglesey<ref>http://celticsaints.org/2014/0106d.html</ref> and his brother St [[Saint Gallgo|Gallgo]], founded a [[St Gallgo's Church, Llanallgo|church at Llanallgo]], nearby.<ref>[http://www.royalcharterchurch.org.uk/history.html St Gallgo Parish Webpage]</ref> Today the two churches are in the same parish.

==Memorial==
His [[feast day]] is 6 January,<ref>http://celticsaints.org/2014/0106d.html.</ref> and is celebrated in the villages of [[Egreas]] and [[Llaneigrad]] on Anglesey.<ref>[[Rice Rees]], An Essay on the Welsh Saints Or the Primitive Christians, Usually Considered to Have Been the Founders of the Churches in Wales (Longman, Rees, Orme, Brown, Green, and Longman, 1836) [https://archive.org/details/AnEssayOnTheWelshSaints/page/n244 <!-- pg=228 quote=Saint Eigrad. -->  page 231].</ref>
