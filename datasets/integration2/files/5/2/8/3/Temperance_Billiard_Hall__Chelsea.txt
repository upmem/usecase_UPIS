The '''Temperance Billiard Hall''' at 131–141 [[King's Road]], [[Chelsea, London|Chelsea]], London, is a [[Grade II listed]] building with [[English Heritage]].<ref>{{NHLE|num=1392863 |desc=|accessdate= 14 August 2014}}</ref>

It was built around 1912–14 to a design by [[Thomas Somerford|Thomas Retford Somerford]] for [[Temperance Billiard Halls|Temperance Billiards Halls Ltd.]], and became an antiques centre in the 1960s. It is still retail premises.<ref>[http://www.victorianweb.org/art/architecture/london/145.html ''Former Temperance Billiard Hall''] The Victorian Web, 29 September 2012. Retrieved 14 August 2014.</ref> It is now connected to the former [[Chelsea Garage]] in 15 Flood Street, which is also Grade II listed.<ref name=EnglishHeritage02>{{NHLE |num= 1392893|desc= Former Motorcar Garage|access-date= 27 September 2014}}</ref>
