'''Sandra McLaren''' is an Australian [[geologist]].

Interested in geology and [[geothermal energy]] from a young age,<ref name = "scott">{{cite web|last1=Scott|first1=Rebecca|title=Australia's Hot Underbelly|url=http://archive.uninews.unimelb.edu.au/news/5618/|website=news.unimelb.edu.au|publisher=University of Melbourne News|accessdate=14 August 2014}}</ref> McLaren completed her PhD at [[Adelaide University]] in 2001 and is currently a Research Fellow at the [[University of Melbourne]].<ref>{{Cite web| title = Sandra McLaren| work = Earth Science - University of Melbourne| accessdate = 2015-04-22| url = http://www.earthsci.unimelb.edu.au/researcher-info/sandra-mclaren?initialWidth=550px&width=550px&initialHeight=50%25&height=50%25&render=bare| url-status = dead| archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20150404051348/https://www.earthsci.unimelb.edu.au/researcher-info/sandra-mclaren?initialWidth=550px&width=550px&initialHeight=50%25&height=50%25&render=bare| archivedate = 2015-04-04}}</ref>  She is a noted science communicator and educator, especially on the topic of sustainable energy through [[Enhanced geothermal system|hot rocks]] in southern Australia.<ref name = "scott" />

In 2008, McLaren received a Victorian Young Tall Poppy Award,<ref>{{Cite web| title = Dr Sandra McLaren - Tall Poppies - 2008 Victorian Award Winners| work = Australian Institute of Policy and Science| accessdate = 2015-04-22| url = http://www.aips.net.au/tall-poppies/vic-tall-poppies/past-vic-tall-poppy-winners/2008-victorian-award-winners/dr-sandra-mclaren/}}</ref> and attended the inaugural InterAcademy Panel meeting for Young Scientists.<ref name = "scott" /> In 2008 she received the [[Australian Academy of Science]]'s [[Dorothy Hill Medal|Dorothy Hill award]].<ref>{{Cite web| title = Awardees for 2008 - Academy Medal| work = Australian Academy of Science| accessdate = 2015-04-22| url = https://www.science.org.au/awardees-2008| url-status = dead| archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20150311060207/https://www.science.org.au/awardees-2008| archivedate = 2015-03-11}}</ref>  The same year, she and Professor Mike Sandford received a grant from the [[Australian Research Council]] to research the mechanics of hot rocks and subsequently the potential for geothermal energy in Australia.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Maslen|first1=George|title=Tapping into a core promise|url=http://www.theage.com.au/national/education/tapping-into-a-core-promise-20090312-8w18.html|accessdate=14 August 2014|agency=The Age|date=16 March 2009}}</ref> She was a member of the [[Global Young Academy]]{{fact|date=December 2019}}.
