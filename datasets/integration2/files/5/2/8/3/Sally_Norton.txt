'''Sally Norton''' (born 1973) is an [[Australian]] [[plant scientist]] and national Leader of the [[Australian Grains Genebank]], [[Agriculture Victoria]], in [[Horsham, Victoria|Horsham]]. Norton has over 20 years' experience in the collection, characterisation and management of [[plant genetic resources]] in [[seedbanks]], specialising in crop wild relatives. She is working to establish the Australian Grains Genebank as the national focal point for access to grains [[germplasm]] for use in Australia's research and grain crop breeding programs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mailtimes.com.au/story/2422139/horshams-australian-grains-genebank-grows-grains-collection/|title=Horsham's Australian Grains Genebank grows grain collection|publisher=The Wimmera Mail-Times|accessdate=14 August 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140814041646/http://www.mailtimes.com.au/story/2422139/horshams-australian-grains-genebank-grows-grains-collection/|archive-date=14 August 2014|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Norton was born in [[Brunswick, Victoria|Brunswick]], [[Victoria (Australia)|Victoria]] in 1973. She attended [[Padua College, Melbourne|Padua College]], Mornington, and received a Bachelor of Agricultural Science from [[La Trobe University]] in 1997. She was awarded a PhD from [[Southern Cross University]] in 2007 for her work evaluating the genetic relationships of tertiary genepool and cultivated [[Sorghum]].

== Career ==
Norton began her career with the [[Australian Tropical Crops and Forages Collection]], [[Biloela]], with the then Queensland Department of Primary Industries. Norton worked as a scientist and curator, with a focus on the improvement of seedbank conservation practices for Australia's tropical crop wild relatives.

In 2013, Norton took on the role as national Leader for the Australian Grains Genebank located in Horsham, Victoria. The Australian Grains Genebank was established in 2013 through co-investment of $6 million by the [[Grains Research and Development Corporation]] and the Victorian Department of Environment and Primary Industries<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.weeklytimesnow.com.au/business/grain-and-hay/million-australian-grains-genebank-opened-in-horsham-last-week/story-fnker6ee-1226870709944|title=$6 million Australian Grains Genebank opened in Horsham last week|publisher=Weekly Times Now|accessdate=14 August 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140814042329/http://www.weeklytimesnow.com.au/business/grain-and-hay/million-australian-grains-genebank-opened-in-horsham-last-week/story-fnker6ee-1226870709944|archive-date=14 August 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> and shares resources with a global network of seedbanks, including [[Svalbard Global Seed Vault]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.crawfordfund.org/news-stories/news-aussie-wild-relatives-in-the-mix-at-svalbard-march-2014/|title=Aussie Wild Relatives in the Mix at Svalbard, March 2014|publisher=Crawford Fund|accessdate=14 August 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140814034544/http://www.crawfordfund.org/news-stories/news-aussie-wild-relatives-in-the-mix-at-svalbard-march-2014/|archive-date=14 August 2014|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2014-03-04/norway-seed-bank/5296700|title=Another seed deposit in the doomsday vault|publisher=ABC Rural|accessdate=14 August 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140814093135/http://www.abc.net.au/news/2014-03-04/norway-seed-bank/5296700|archive-date=14 August 2014|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.croptrust.org/content/australian-deposit-svalbard-and-australias-crop-wild-relatives|title=New Australian Seed Deposit -
