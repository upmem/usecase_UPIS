'''Mosese Fotuaika''' (25 March 1992 – 28 February 2013) was a New Zealand professional [[rugby league]] footballer who was contracted to the [[Wests Tigers]] in the [[National Rugby League]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/sport/nrl/wests-tigers-mourn-death-of-young-prop-mosese-fotuaika/story-e6frexnr-1226588692092|title=Wests Tigers mourn death of young prop Mosese Fotuaika|publisher=dailytelegraph.com.au|date=2 March 2013}}</ref> He primarily played {{rlp|pr}}. He was of [[Tongan people|Tongan]] and [[New Zealanders|New Zealand]] [[Māori people|Māori]] descent.

==Playing career==
Mosese Fotuaika played his junior football for [[Keebra Park State High School|Keebra Park]] before being signed by the [[Wests Tigers]]. He played for the Tigers' [[National Youth Competition (rugby league)|NYC]] team in 2012.

In 2012, Fotuaika played in the Tigers' [[2012 NRL Under-20s season|2012 NYC]] Grand Final win over the [[Canberra Raiders]]. Mosese played a key role in Wests Tigers winning their inaugural NYC premiership in 2012 and was a full-time member of the Wests Tigers NRL squad for 2013.

==Death==
On 28 February 2013, Fotuaika was found dead by his girlfriend, Shanice Alaiasa at his home in [[Merrylands, New South Wales|Merrylands]] in [[Greater Western Sydney]]. Police had been called to the home and they confirmed that they had found a man dead and his death was not being treated as suspicious. Hours earlier he had been at the Wests Tigers club [[Concord, New South Wales|Concord]] gym and he injured himself while bench pressing with friends and it is believed that Fotuaika suffered a badly-torn pectoral muscle while exerting himself hours before taking his own life.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/sport/nrl/promising-young-wests-tigers-prop-mosese-fotuaika-dead/story-e6frexnr-1226588044039|title=Promising young Wests Tigers prop Mosese Fotuaika found dead, aged 20|publisher=dailytelegraph.com.au|date=1 March 2013}}</ref>
