'''Johnson College Prep''' is a [[state school|public]] 4–year [[Charter school|charter]] [[high school]] located in the [[Englewood, Chicago|Englewood]] neighborhood on the south side of [[Chicago, Illinois]], United States. Johnson is a part of the [[Noble Network of Charter Schools]] and [[Chicago Public Schools]]. Opening in 2010, The school is named for [[African-American]] businessman and founder of the Chicago–based [[Johnson Publishing Company]] [[John H. Johnson]] and his wife [[Eunice Johnson]].<ref>[http://johnsoncollegeprep.noblenetwork.org/about/history-and-campus-overview History & Campus Partnerships]</ref><ref>[https://chicagodefender.com/2010/10/12/school-named-for-johnson-publishing-co-founder-opens-in-englewood/ Chicago Defender - City School named for Johnson Publishing Co. founder opens in Englewood]</ref>

==Background==
===Academic Offerings & Results===
Johnson College Prep offers JRTOC, 4 Advanced Placement class options, and partners with [https://www.pltw.org/our-programs/pltw-engineering Project Lead the Way] to offer an engineering pathway. Students also have access to ACT/SAT prep courses and college visits.<ref name="cps.edu">{{Cite web|url=https://cps.edu/Schools/Find_a_school/Pages/findaschool.aspx|title=CPS : Find a school : Find a School|website=cps.edu|access-date=2018-12-06}}</ref> In 2018, Johnson College Prep's 5 year graduation rate is 75%, which is on par with Chicago Public Schools.<ref name="cps.edu"/> Additionally, in 2017, a comprehensive [http://credo.stanford.edu/pdfs/CMO%20FINAL.pdf report] was released by [[Stanford University]]'s Center for Education Outcomes (CREDO), which shows that students at the Noble Network of Charter Schools receive the equivalent of nearly two additional years of math education during just one normal school year.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://us11.campaign-archive.com/?u=ef1fda12ab06b5fc6277f2122&id=80401e6146|title=New Report Shows Noble Students Receive Equivalent of 2 Years of Instruction in Just One School Year|website=us11.campaign-archive.com|access-date=2018-12-06}}</ref>

===College Readiness & College Graduation Rate===
In an effort to incorporate college readiness into the school before 12th grade, students participate in the Summer of a Lifetime program. Through Summer of a Lifetime, scholars are provided funding and support to participate in life-changing, summer academic enrichment programs on college campuses nationwide.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://summerofalifetime.noblenetwork.org/|title=Summer of a Lifetime {{!}} Welcome|website=summerofalifetime.noblenetwork.org|access-date=2018-12-06}}</ref> The Noble class of 2018 included 2,300 scholars, and 98% of them were accepted to at least one college, this accumulated to over 14,700 college acceptances. Noble seniors earned a record-breaking $486 million in scholarships, including 71 recipients of the Pritzker Access Scholarship for undocumented students.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://us11.campaign-archive.com/?u=ef1fda12ab06b5fc6277f2122&id=2ee4650d0e|title=Noble June Newsletter: Class of 2018 Graduation, Summer of a Lifetime Sendoff Video, Alumni Association Announcement, Rowe-Clark Rugby State Champions|website=us11.campaign-archive.com|access-date=2018-12-06}}</ref> At Johnson College Prep, a campus of Noble, 77% of graduates enrolled in college in 2018, and 60% of Johnson graduates are persisting in college in 2018, which accounts for the first four graduating classes.<ref name="cps.edu"/>

==Programs, Clubs, and Sports==
Elective courses offered: choir/chorus, drawing, film and video, painting, photography, piano/keyboard, sewing, step dance<ref name="cps.edu"/>

== Distinguished Teacher Program ==
In May 2019, The Noble Network of Charter Schools announced the first 20 recipients of its newly created Distinguished Teacher program.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://nobleschools.org/2019/05/30/20-chicago-public-school-educators-receive-elite-distinguished-teacher-recognition/|title=20 Chicago Public School Educators Receive Elite "Distinguished Teacher" Recognition|last=|first=|date=May 30, 2019|website=nobleschools.org|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> The purpose of the Distinguished Teacher program is to identify, recognize, and reward a select group of Noble teachers who have achieved exceptional results for students at Noble for at least three years and during that time have exemplified the five key questions laid out in the Distinguished Teacher framework.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nobleschools.org/careers/distinguished-teacher/|title=Distinguished Teacher|last=|first=|date=|website=nobleschools.org|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> Prior to being selected, candidates participated in a rigorous application process that began in January 2019, where they first submitted their written narratives along with a portfolio that included comprehensive data. Finalists then participated in classroom observations and debriefs, student surveys, a panel interview, and reference checks.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/local/309/2019/06/04/729338102/chicago-charter-awards-top-teachers-10-k-bonuses-elite-title-to-stay-in-classroom|title=Chicago Charter Awards Top Teachers $10K Bonuses, Elite Title To Stay In Classroom|last=Lutton|first=Linda|date=June 4, 2019|work=WBEZ Chicago|access-date=}}</ref> Among other benefits, those who are selected to be a Distinguished Teacher receive an annual bonus of $10,000, will help facilitate professional development experiences across the Noble Network as well as participate in the selection of future Distinguished Teachers.<ref name=":0" />

Laura McKelvey, a Science teacher, was chosen to be a 2018-2019 Distinguished Teacher at Johnson College Prep.<ref name=":0" />
