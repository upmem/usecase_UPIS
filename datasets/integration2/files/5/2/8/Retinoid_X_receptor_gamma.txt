'''Retinoid X receptor gamma''' ('''RXR-gamma'''), also known as '''NR2B3''' (nuclear receptor subfamily 2, group B, member 3) is a [[nuclear receptor]] that in humans is encoded by the ''RXRG'' [[gene]].<ref name="pmid8034312">{{cite journal | vauthors = Almasan A, Mangelsdorf DJ, Ong ES, Wahl GM, Evans RM | title = Chromosomal localization of the human retinoid X receptors | journal = Genomics | volume = 20 | issue = 3 | pages = 397–403 | date = April 1994 | pmid = 8034312 | doi = 10.1006/geno.1994.1193 }}</ref><ref name="entrez" />

== Function ==

This gene encodes a member of the [[retinoid X receptor]] (RXR) family of nuclear receptors which are involved in mediating the antiproliferative effects of [[retinoic acid]] (RA). This receptor forms heterodimers with the [[retinoic acid receptor|retinoic acid]], [[thyroid hormone receptor|thyroid hormone]], and [[calcitriol receptor|vitamin D receptors]], increasing both DNA binding and transcriptional function on their respective response elements. This gene is expressed at significantly lower levels in non-small cell lung cancer cells. [[Alternative splicing|Alternate transcriptional splice]] variants, encoding different [[protein isoform|isoforms]], have been characterized.<ref name="entrez">{{cite web | title = Entrez Gene: RXRG retinoid X receptor, gamma| url = https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/sites/entrez?Db=gene&Cmd=ShowDetailView&TermToSearch=6258}}</ref>
